# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Compound Sentences</span>

A compound sentence joins two independent clauses—two equal, complete thoughts—using a connector. Both could stand alone, but joining them shows they're related.

**Blueprint:**  
Independent clause + connector + independent clause

**Connectors (FANBOYS):** for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so  
**Alternative:** semicolon (;) when ideas are very closely related

---

## <span style="color: #00838f">When to Use Compound Sentences</span>

Use compound sentences to show that two ideas are equally important and related.

| Type | Example | Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Contrast (but)** | "The government repaired roads, **but** commuters still faced delays." | Task 2 counterarguments |
| **Consequence (so)** | "Traffic increased, **so** the city implemented pricing." | Cause-effect explanation |
| **Addition (and)** | "Public transport expanded, **and** ridership grew significantly." | Showing growth patterns (Task 1) |
| **Alternation (or)** | "Invest in buses, **or** face congestion crises." | Policy options |

---

## <span style="color: #d4af37">The Comma Splice Error</span>

```
❌ WRONG: "Traffic increased, public transport remained unchanged."
   → Only comma joining two independent clauses

✅ RIGHT: "Traffic increased, and public transport remained unchanged."
   OR: "Traffic increased; public transport remained unchanged."
```

**Remember:** A comma alone cannot join two independent clauses. You need a conjunction or semicolon.

---

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">How to Use in IELTS</span>

- **Task 1:** Compare two categories or trends  
- **Task 2:** Connect cause and effect cleanly; acknowledge counterarguments  
- **Speaking:** Use naturally—but avoid overusing "and." Try "but," "so," "yet"

**Addition:**  
"Public transport expanded, and ridership grew significantly."

---

## The Comma Splice Error

The most common error with compound sentences is joining two independent clauses with only a comma—no connector.

Incorrect: "Traffic increased, public transport remained unchanged."  
Correct: "Traffic increased, and public transport remained unchanged."  
Also correct: "Traffic increased; public transport remained unchanged."
