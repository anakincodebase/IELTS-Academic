# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Determiners</span>

Determiners come before nouns to show quantity, possession, or specificity. Articles (a, the), demonstratives (this, that), possessives (my, your), and quantifiers (some, many, few) are all determiners.

In IELTS, the most common errors with determiners involve quantifiers. "Much students" is wrong because "students" is countable. "Many students" is correct. This seems obvious when you see it, but under time pressure, learners make these mistakes repeatedly.

---

## <span style="color: #00838f">Countable vs. Uncountable Quantifiers</span>

This is the core rule. Use **many/few** with countable nouns. Use **much/little** with uncountable nouns.

**Countable:** many students, few opportunities, several reasons, each person, every case  
**Uncountable:** much evidence, little time, some information, any advice

**Common error:** "Much cars are needed." (Wrong: cars is countable)  
**Correct:** "Many cars are needed." Or "Much transportation is needed." (transportation is uncountable)

> [!example] IELTS Example
> Fewer private cars and more buses can reduce congestion.

> [!example] IELTS Example
> Little investment in maintenance often causes long-term damage.

## <span style="color: #00838f">High-Impact Pairs</span>
- each vs every
- some vs any
- few/a few/the few
- little/a little/the little

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Mismatch between determiner and noun type is a common grammar penalty.
>
> Incorrect: much students
>
> Correct: many students
