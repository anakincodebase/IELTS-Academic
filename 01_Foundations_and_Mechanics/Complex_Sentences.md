# Complex Sentences
![[Pasted image 20260412011857.png]]

Complex sentences combine an independent clause with one or more dependent clauses. This is one of the clearest signals of high grammatical range in IELTS.

**Blueprint:**  
Independent clause + dependent clause (or vice versa)

Common subordinators: because, since, although, even though, if, when, after, before, while, unless

---

## <span style="color: #00838f">Why They Matter</span>

Complex sentences show you can explain **relationships between ideas**: cause, contrast, condition, and time. These are the relationships that make arguments convincing.

**Relationship Types:**

| Relationship | Subordinator | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Cause** | because, since, as | "Because prolonged blackouts damage businesses, residents support stricter regulation." |
| **Contrast** | although, even though, whereas | "Although the city expanded roads, congestion remained severe." |
| **Condition** | if, unless, provided that | "Unless policymakers modernize the grid, instability will continue." |
| **Time** | when, after, before, while | "After pricing was implemented, traffic declined measurably." |

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Punctuation Rules</span>

```
Dependent clause FIRST → Use a comma
"Although the city expanded roads, congestion remained severe." ✅

Independent clause FIRST → Usually no comma  
"Congestion remained severe although the city expanded roads." ✅
```

---

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Common Errors</span>

```
❌ FRAGMENT: "Although waste collection improved."
   → Missing independent clause

✅ CORRECT: "Although waste collection improved, many areas remained polluted."

❌ MISSING COMMA: "Because funding was delayed the project stalled."

✅ CORRECT: "Because funding was delayed, the project stalled."
```
