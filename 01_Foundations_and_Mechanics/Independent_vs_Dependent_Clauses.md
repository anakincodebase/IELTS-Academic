# Independent vs Dependent Clauses

## Independent Clause
> [!info] Rule
> An independent clause has a subject and verb and can stand alone as a full sentence.

Example: The city upgraded street lighting.

## Dependent Clause
> [!info] Rule
> A dependent clause has a subject and verb but cannot stand alone.

Common starters:
- although, because, if, when, while, since, unless

Example: Because the budget was reduced.

## How They Work Together
> [!example] IELTS Example
> Because the budget was reduced, infrastructure maintenance slowed.

> [!example] IELTS Example
> Infrastructure maintenance slowed because the budget was reduced.

## Frequent Errors
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Fragment error:
>
> Incorrect: While public transport improved.
>
> Correct: While public transport improved, traffic congestion remained high.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Misplaced comma:
>
> Incorrect: Citizens protested because, services declined.
>
> Correct: Citizens protested because services declined.
