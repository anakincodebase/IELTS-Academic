# Sentences and Clauses

## Core Idea
> [!info] Rule
> A sentence must express a complete thought and contain at least one independent clause.

A clause is a group of words with a subject and a verb. See [[Independent_vs_Dependent_Clauses]].

## Sentence Types at a Glance
- [[Simple_Sentences]]: one independent clause.
- [[Compound_Sentences]]: two or more independent clauses joined correctly.
- [[Complex_Sentences]]: one independent clause plus one or more dependent clauses.
- [[Compound_Complex_Sentences]]: two or more independent clauses plus one or more dependent clauses.

## Why This Matters for IELTS
> [!info] Rule
> Grammatical Range improves when you vary sentence structures naturally across a paragraph.

> [!example] IELTS Example
> Although residents pay high taxes, roads remain damaged, and public trust continues to decline.
>
> This single sentence demonstrates dependent + independent + independent structure.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Overusing only simple sentences can make writing sound repetitive and limit your grammar score.

## Actionable Application
- In Writing Task 2, aim for at least one clear complex sentence in each body paragraph.
- In Speaking Part 3, combine ideas with subordinators like although, while, because, and unless.
- Proofread for fragments and comma splices using [[Independent_vs_Dependent_Clauses]].
