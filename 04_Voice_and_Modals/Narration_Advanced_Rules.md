# Narration Advanced Rules (IELTS)

## Fast Revision Path
- Quick exam-day review: [[Reported_Speech_Quick_Revision]]
- Application-heavy drills: [[Direct_and_Indirect_Speech]]

## Scope of This Note
This is the canonical advanced rulebook for narration/reporting conversions.

## Core Objective
> [!info] Rule
> Narration means converting direct speech into reported speech with correct shifts in pronouns, tense, time, place, and sentence structure.

For IELTS Band 8+, narration should be accurate, concise, and formal.

## 1) Narration Conversion Algorithm (7 Steps)
1. Choose the right reporting verb (say, tell, ask, suggest, warn, explain, etc.).
2. Decide whether backshift is needed (depends on reporting tense and context).
3. Shift pronouns and possessives logically.
4. Shift time/place words where needed.
5. Convert sentence type (statement/question/command/exclamation).
6. Remove quotation marks and comma before quote.
7. Recheck grammar order and punctuation.

## 2) Master Structure Formulas

### A) Statements (Assertive)
- Direct: S + said, "clause"
- Indirect: S + said (that) + clause

Example:
- Direct: He said, "I am busy."
- Indirect: He said that he was busy.

### B) Yes/No Questions
- Direct: S + said/asked, "Aux + subject + ...?"
- Indirect: S + asked + if/whether + subject + verb

Example:
- Direct: She asked, "Do you agree?"
- Indirect: She asked whether I agreed.

### C) Wh-Questions
- Direct: S + asked, "Wh-word + Aux + subject + ...?"
- Indirect: S + asked + Wh-word + subject + verb

Example:
- Direct: He asked, "Where are you going?"
- Indirect: He asked where I was going.

### D) Commands / Requests / Advice
- Indirect structure: reporting verb + object + to-infinitive
- Negative: reporting verb + object + not to-infinitive

Examples:
- "Open the window," she said. -> She told me to open the window.
- "Do not smoke here," he said. -> He warned us not to smoke there.

### E) Suggestions
Common patterns:
- suggested + gerund
- suggested + that + subject + base verb

Examples:
- He said, "Let's leave early." -> He suggested leaving early.
- He suggested that we leave early.

### F) Exclamations
Use verbs like exclaimed, cried out, wished, prayed, lamented, exclaimed with joy/sorrow.

Example:
- "What a beautiful view!" -> She exclaimed that it was a beautiful view.

### G) Wishes and Prayers (Optative)
Use wished/prayed/exclaimed with hope.

Examples:
- "May you succeed!" -> He wished that I might succeed.
- "May God bless you." -> She prayed that God might bless me.

## 3) Reporting Verb Matrix (Advanced)
| Function | Common Verbs |
|---|---|
| neutral reporting | say, tell, state, mention |
| question | ask, inquire, wonder, want to know |
| command | order, command, instruct |
| request | request, urge, beg, plead |
| advice/warning | advise, warn, remind, caution |
| opinion/argument | argue, claim, contend, assert |
| explanation | explain, clarify, point out, add |
| suggestion | suggest, propose, recommend |
| promise/commitment | promise, assure, guarantee |
| refusal/denial | deny, refuse |

## 4) Tense Backshift Table
| Direct Tense | Usual Reported Tense |
|---|---|
| Present Simple | Past Simple |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous |
| Present Perfect | Past Perfect |
| Present Perfect Continuous | Past Perfect Continuous |
| Past Simple | Past Perfect |
| Past Continuous | Past Perfect Continuous |
| will | would |
| can | could |
| may | might |

### No-Backshift Cases (Very Important)
No backshift is usually needed when:
- reporting verb is present/future
- statement is universal truth
- statement is still true at reporting time

Examples:
- The teacher says that water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
- He said that Dhaka is the capital of Bangladesh.

## 5) Pronoun and Determiner Shift
General guide:
- first person -> according to speaker
- second person -> according to the reported addressee/context
- third person -> often unchanged

Examples:
- "I am ready," she said. -> She said that she was ready.
- "You are late," he told me. -> He told me that I was late.

This/these often change to that/those, or to the/it/they based on meaning.

## 6) Time and Place Shift Table
| Direct | Indirect |
|---|---|
| now | then |
| today | that day |
| yesterday | the day before |
| tomorrow | the next/following day |
| last week | the previous week |
| next year | the following year |
| here | there |
| this | that |
| these | those |

Note: Same-day reporting may keep original time words.

## 7) Modal and Conditional Narration
- must can remain must (deduction) or change to had to (obligation)
- should/could/might/ought to often remain unchanged
- Type 2 conditionals usually stay structurally same

Examples:
- He said, "It must be true." -> He said that it must be true.
- He said, "I must finish this." -> He said that he had to finish that.
- She said, "If I were rich, I would travel." -> She said that if she were rich, she would travel.

## 8) Punctuation and Form Control
- Remove quotation marks in indirect speech.
- Do not use question mark in reported questions.
- Use statement word order after if/whether and wh-words.
- Avoid double connectors like that if.

## 9) Advanced Error Traps for IELTS
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: He told to me that he was tired.
>
> Correct: He told me that he was tired.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: She asked where was I going.
>
> Correct: She asked where I was going.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: He suggested to go early.
>
> Correct: He suggested going early. / He suggested that we go early.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: He asked that if I was ready.
>
> Correct: He asked if I was ready.

## 10) IELTS Application (Band 8+)
- Writing Task 2: use reporting verbs to summarize views objectively.
- Speaking Part 3: report others' opinions before presenting your own.
- Avoid long direct quotes unless absolutely necessary.

Model sentence:
- Urban planners contend that public transport investment should be prioritized in high-density areas.

## Mnemonic Bank
- RVPTS: Reporting verb -> Pronoun -> Tense -> Space/time -> Structure.
- IFQ: Yes/No questions use if/whether.
- WHS: Wh-question keeps Wh-word, then statement order.
- CTO: Command/request -> object + to-infinitive.

## 30-Second Final Narration Check
- Correct reporting verb chosen?
- Pronoun shift logical?
- Tense shift correct (or valid no-backshift)?
- Time/place words adjusted where needed?
- Question and command structures converted correctly?
