# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Auxiliary Verbs</span>

## <span style="color: #00838f">Core Objective</span>
> [!info] Rule
> Auxiliary verbs build tense, questions, negatives, voice, and emphasis. Weak control here causes frequent grammar errors.

## <span style="color: #00838f">What Are Auxiliary Verbs?</span>
Primary auxiliaries:
- be (am, is, are, was, were, be, being, been)
- have (have, has, had)
- do (do, does, did)

Modal auxiliaries:
- can, could, may, might, will, would, shall, should, must, ought to

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Main Jobs of Auxiliary Verbs</span>

### Build tenses
- She is studying now.
- They have finished the report.
- We had completed the task before lunch.

### Form negatives
- She does not agree.
- They have not arrived.
- He is not working today.

### Form questions
- Do you agree with this view?
- Has the government reacted yet?
- Are students benefiting from online classes?

### Create passive voice
- The law was introduced in 2020.
- New policies should be implemented carefully.

### Add emphasis (do-support)
- I do understand your concern.
- She did submit the form on time.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Do-Support Rules (Very Important)</span>
Use do/does/did when:
- making questions in simple present/past
- making negatives in simple present/past
- adding emphasis in simple present/past

Examples:
- Do they provide free textbooks?
- He does not like long commutes.
- They did complete the survey.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: Why you think this policy works?
>
> Correct: Why do you think this policy works?

## <span style="color: #00838f">Be vs Have vs Do in IELTS Sentences</span>
- be: progressive and passive structures
- have: perfect structures
- do: simple tense support for questions/negatives/emphasis

Examples:
- The number is increasing quickly. (be + V-ing)
- The number has increased quickly. (have + V3)
- Did the number increase quickly? (do-support)

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">Auxiliary in Inversion (Advanced)</span>
Inversion requires auxiliary before subject.
- Rarely do students revise punctuation carefully.
- Not only has the city expanded roads, but it has also upgraded buses.

If no auxiliary exists, add do/does/did:
- Rarely do people read instructions fully.

## <span style="color: #00838f">Auxiliary Agreement and Error Control</span>
- He does, they do.
- She has, they have.
- There is, there are.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: She do not agree.
>
> Correct: She does not agree.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: They is working on the report.
>
> Correct: They are working on the report.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Short Answers and Tag Questions</span>
### Short answers
- Do you agree? Yes, I do.
- Has he finished? No, he has not.

### Tag questions
- The plan is effective, isn't it?
- They have completed the project, haven't they?
- You do not support this idea, do you?

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">Mnemonic Bank</span>
- BHD Core: Be, Have, Do are core auxiliaries.
- QN Rule: Questions and negatives in simple tenses need do-support.
- INV Rule: In inversion, auxiliary comes before subject.
- ASP Rule: Auxiliary controls Aspect, Sentence type, and Passive voice.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">30-Second Auxiliary Scan</span>
- Every question has the right auxiliary?
- Every negative uses correct auxiliary?
- Simple present/past question uses do/does/did?
- Passive structures include be + V3?
