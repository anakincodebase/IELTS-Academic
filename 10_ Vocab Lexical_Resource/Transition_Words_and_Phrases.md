# Transition Words and Phrases

## Meaning, Definition, and Use
> [!info] Rule
> Transition words are linking words or phrases that connect ideas, sentences, and paragraphs so your writing flows logically.

In IELTS, transition words help you:
- show clear relationships between ideas
- avoid random sentence sequences
- improve Coherence and Cohesion
- make arguments easier to follow

## How to Use Them Correctly
1. Pick a transition based on logic, not style only.
2. Do not overuse one family (for example, moreover in every paragraph).
3. Match punctuation to structure:
- Sentence connector: X. However, Y.
- Clause linker: Although X, Y.
- Semicolon pattern: X; therefore, Y.

## IELTS Band 8.5 Transition Strategy
- Task 1:
  - Comparison: whereas, while, in contrast
  - Trend result: therefore, consequently
- Task 2:
  - Paragraph openers: however, moreover, by contrast
  - Evidence links: for example, specifically
  - Conclusion links: overall, in conclusion
- Speaking Part 3:
  - Use lighter transitions naturally: however, at the same time, on the other hand

## Common Mistakes
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Overuse of one connector:
>
> Moreover..., moreover..., moreover...

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Wrong punctuation:
>
> Incorrect: The plan failed, however people still supported it.
>
> Correct: The plan failed; however, people still supported it.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Logic mismatch:
>
> Do not use contrast markers when the second sentence gives an example.

## 30-Second Transition Check
- Does each transition match the logic?
- Is connector repetition controlled?
- Is punctuation correct?
- Are transitions helping clarity, not decoration?
