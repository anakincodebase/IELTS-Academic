# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Collocations Bank: Band 8 Word Partnerships</span>

Collocations are word pairs that occur naturally together in English. They're not about rules—they're about what native speakers actually say. "Make a decision" is a collocation. "Do a decision" isn't (though "make" and "decision" are perfectly common words on their own).

Band 8 vocabulary means knowing not just sophisticated words, but knowing which words go with which. A Band 6 test-taker might write "gain access." A Band 8 test-taker writes "facilitate access" or "gain traction" or "narrow the divide"—the specific collocation for the specific meaning.

This bank is organized by the verb patterns you'll use most in IELTS writing. Scan for the verb, find the noun, and use the exact phrase. Knowing 20-30 of these well beats knowing 200 vague words.

---

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">How to Use This Bank</span>

When writing an IELTS essay, don't invent verb-noun combinations. If you're unsure whether "build solutions" or "implement solutions" or "propose solutions" is the right collocation, scan this bank and use one you see documented. Over-relying on dictionaries for combinations wastes time. This bank has the ones that matter for IELTS.
