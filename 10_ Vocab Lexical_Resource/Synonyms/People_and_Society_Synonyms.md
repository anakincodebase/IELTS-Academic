# People and Society Synonyms

## Core Objective
> [!info] Rule
> Replace simple words with accurate alternatives by context, not by memorization.

## Core Mapping (IELTS-Safe)
| Core Word | Better Alternatives | Context Rule |
|---|---|---|
| people | individuals, the public | Use individuals for a specific group; use the public for society-wide statements. |
| society | the community, the general populace | General populace is more formal. |
| children | youngsters, young people, the youth, minors | Minors is legal/formal; youngsters is less formal. |
| students | learners, pupils, scholars | Pupils = school children; scholars = high achievers/academic context. |
| teachers | educators, tutors, instructors | Tutors are private/small-group; instructors often teach practical skills. |
| adults | grown-ups, mature individuals | Grown-ups is less formal; mature individuals is formal. |
| family | household, domestic unit | Domestic unit is formal/sociological. |
| individual (noun) | person, citizen | Citizen highlights social/public role. |
| community | society, populace | Populace is formal and less common in speaking. |

## Accuracy Filter for IELTS
### Direct safe substitutions
- people -> individuals, the public
- children -> young people, the youth
- students -> learners
- teachers -> educators
- family -> household

### Context-dependent substitutions
- children -> minors (legal/policy writing)
- students -> pupils (school only), scholars (academic excellence)
- adults -> mature individuals (formal tone)
- family -> domestic unit (very formal)

### Use carefully
- adolescents: only for roughly ages 13-17
- infants: only for babies under 1 year old

## Example Sentences
- Many individuals in urban areas rely on public transport.
- The general populace should receive clear health guidance.
- Young people benefit from educators who give practical feedback.
- The household budget often limits access to private tutors.
- Citizens are expected to participate in civic decision-making.
