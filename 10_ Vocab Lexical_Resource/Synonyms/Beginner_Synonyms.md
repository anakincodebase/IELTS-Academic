## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- novice/newcomer = neutral and common
- amateur can sound negative in some contexts
- layperson = non-specialist (formal)

## Accuracy Filter for IELTS
### Direct beginner words (safe in most IELTS contexts)
- beginner
- novice
- newcomer
- trainee
- learner
- apprentice
- entry-level candidate

### Related words (context-dependent)
- first-time user
- entrant
- fledgling
- non-specialist
- layperson

### Use carefully
- greenhorn, tyro, neophyte (rare or stylistically marked)
- amateur (can imply low quality)

## Example Sentences
- Beginners often need structured feedback in the first month.
- Many novice drivers struggle in heavy traffic.
- The guide is written for laypersons, not specialists.
- Entry-level candidates require clear mentoring frameworks.
- Newcomers usually improve quickly with regular practice.
