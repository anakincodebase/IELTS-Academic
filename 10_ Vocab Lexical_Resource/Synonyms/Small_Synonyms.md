### Related words (context-dependent)
- diminutive, miniature, pocket-sized, petite
- cramped, constrained, restricted, underdeveloped
- sparse, thin, meager, weak
- trivial, fractional, partial

### Not direct synonyms of small (use carefully)
- poor (quality/economic meaning)
- weak (strength meaning, not always size)
- low (can mean level, price, or position)
- young (age meaning)
- short (time/height meaning)
- simple (complexity meaning)

## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- small, little = common everyday terms
- minor, modest, limited = formal and reliable for IELTS tasks
- negligible, marginal = strong for data where change is almost zero
- tiny, minuscule = stronger emotional tone; use carefully in formal writing

Extra guidance:
- In Task 1, marginal increase and negligible difference are very useful.
- In Task 2, modest resources or limited access sounds more academic than very small.
- Avoid exaggeration: choose words that match the real data size.

## Example Sentences
- The chart shows a small decline in car ownership among young adults.
- There was only a marginal difference between the two groups.
- Rural schools often operate with limited resources.
- The village has a compact layout and a modest population.
- The policy had a negligible effect during the first year.
- A small-scale business can still create local employment.
- The report found only a slight improvement in air quality.
