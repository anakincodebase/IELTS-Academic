## Full Explanation of All Synonym Words
| Word/Phrase | Meaning | Tone/Context | IELTS-Safe Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| praise | say something positive about someone/something | neutral, common | Experts praised the new traffic plan. |
| commend | formally praise for quality or effort | formal, strong | The committee commended the school's reforms. |
| applaud | strongly approve or praise, sometimes publicly | strong, public reaction | Many citizens applauded the anti-corruption measures. |
| appreciate | recognize value or merit | neutral to formal | I appreciate the clarity of this report. |
| admire | respect and like qualities/achievements | neutral | Many students admire her discipline. |
| endorse | publicly support/approve | policy, institutional context | Medical experts endorsed the vaccination campaign. |
| acknowledge | recognize effort/fact/value | formal, careful | The minister acknowledged the team's contribution. |
| acclaim | praise enthusiastically, often by many people | strong, public/formal | The project received international acclaim. |
| extol | praise highly and enthusiastically | very formal, rhetorical | Critics extolled the author's analytical depth. |
| celebrate | praise by honoring success | positive, event/result context | The city celebrated its literacy improvement. |
| laud | praise highly (close to commend/extol) | formal | The report lauded the policy's long-term vision. |
| compliment | express approval directly to a person | interpersonal, direct | The examiner complimented her clear structure. |
| eulogize | praise in a speech or writing, often after death | ceremonial/special context | Historians eulogized his contribution to education. |
| glorify | present as very admirable, sometimes excessively | caution: can imply exaggeration | The campaign should not glorify unhealthy work habits. |
| exalt | raise in status or praise very highly | very formal/literary | Some commentators exalted the reform as historic. |
| hail | publicly praise as important/successful | media/headline style | Analysts hailed the agreement as a breakthrough. |
| honor | show deep respect for achievements/service | formal, ceremonial | The university honored the scientist for her work. |
| revere | deeply respect someone | strong respect, serious tone | Many people revere the physician for his service. |
| venerate | revere with high respect | very formal, often historical/religious | The community venerates its founding educators. |
| champion | actively support and promote | advocacy/policy context | NGOs champion equal access to education. |
| approve of | think something is good/right | neutral | Most parents approve of stricter school attendance rules. |
| think highly of | have a very positive opinion of | spoken-neutral | Employers think highly of graduates with practical skills. |
| speak highly of | express a positive opinion verbally | spoken/writing-neutral | Former students speak highly of that training program. |

## Quick Pattern Guide
- person + praise/commend/applaud + noun
- person + endorse/champion + policy/idea
- person + think highly of/speak highly of + person/thing
- institution + honor + person + for + noun
- report/article + acknowledge/commend + contribution/effort

## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- commend, acknowledge, appreciate = neutral/formal
- acclaim, extol = stronger and more formal

## Accuracy Filter for IELTS
### Direct praise verbs (safe in most IELTS contexts)
- praise
- commend
- applaud
- appreciate
- admire
- acknowledge
- acclaim
- laud
- compliment

### Related positive-evaluation verbs (context-dependent)
- endorse
- celebrate
- hail
- honor
- champion

### Use carefully
- extol, glorify, exalt, eulogize, venerate (highly formal or rhetorical)

## Example Sentences
- The report commended local teachers for improving literacy rates.
- Experts praised the policy for reducing urban congestion.
- The committee acknowledged the team's consistent effort.
- The journal article praised the program's measurable outcomes.
- International observers applauded the city's rapid emergency response.
