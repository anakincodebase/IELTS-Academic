### Related words (context-dependent)
- deficient, substandard, alarming, disappointing, unacceptable
- unstable, troublesome, inferior, counterproductive, serious

### Not direct synonyms of bad (use carefully)
- ugly (appearance, not performance)
- sad (emotion, not quality)
- small (size, not quality)
- poor people (economic status, not quality judgment)
- old (age, not quality)

## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- poor, weak, problematic = neutral formal criticism
- detrimental, adverse = strong formal options for Task 2
- dangerous and severe should be used only for high-risk contexts
- avoid overusing very emotional words like terrible in formal writing

Extra guidance:
- Pair adjective with precise noun: adverse impact, ineffective policy, inadequate infrastructure.
- Explain why something is bad to increase Task Response quality.

## Example Sentences
- The city faces a bad traffic system with poor lane discipline.
- Air pollution has a detrimental effect on public health.
- The current plan is ineffective because it ignores root causes.
- Inadequate funding often leads to weak educational outcomes.
- The policy had an adverse impact on low-income households.
- A flawed recruitment process can reduce institutional quality.
- The condition of the road network is unsatisfactory in rural districts.
