## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- delighted, thrilled, ecstatic = strong emotion
- content = calm satisfaction
- cheerful = visible positive mood

Extra guidance:
- Use noun forms correctly: euphoria, jubilation, mirth.
- Use verb forms correctly: rejoice, exult, delight, exhilarate.
- Use action words as actions, not adjectives: chuckle, chortle, frolic, cherish.
- Avoid forcing rare words in Task 2 if meaning is not precise.

## Example Sentences

- Students are often thrilled when they achieve their target band score.
- The team was exuberant after winning the final match.
- She spoke in a jovial tone throughout the interview.
- The students sounded animated and excited after the results.
- The host maintained a convivial atmosphere during the event.
