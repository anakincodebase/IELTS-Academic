### Related words (context-dependent)
- clumsy, inelegant, crude, coarse, uncouth, awkward-looking
- grimy, worn-out, run-down, poorly maintained
- eyesore, visual blight, aesthetically poor
- harsh, rough, badly designed

### Not direct synonyms of ugly (use carefully)
- evil (moral meaning, not appearance)
- immoral (ethical meaning)
- rude (behavioral meaning)
- boring (interest level, not visual quality)
- poor (economic meaning unless clearly visual quality)
- cheap (price meaning unless context is design quality)

## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- ugly, unattractive, unsightly = general appearance
- hideous, grotesque, repulsive = very strong negative judgment
- shabby, dingy, drab = weak appearance due to age/maintenance
- tacky, gaudy, garish = bad style/taste, often about decoration

Extra guidance:
- Use strong words like hideous or repulsive only when your example is clearly extreme.
- For Task 2, neutral terms like unattractive or visually unappealing are often safer.
- In speaking, explain why something looks bad instead of only using one adjective.

## Example Sentences
- The old building looked unattractive because the paint was peeling and the walls were stained.
- Many residents complained that the billboard was an eyesore in an otherwise historic area.
- The market looked dingy at night due to poor lighting and unmanaged waste.
- The room design was too gaudy, so it felt uncomfortable to stay there for long.
- The public park became visually unappealing after years of weak maintenance.
- Some people considered the monument grotesque, while others saw it as modern art.
- The street appeared shabby, but local authorities later restored it successfully.
