# Importance and Quality Synonyms

## Core Mapping (IELTS-Safe)
| Core Word | Better Alternatives | Context Rule |
|---|---|---|
| important | crucial, vital | Crucial often means decisive; vital means essential. |
| good | beneficial, positive | Beneficial usually describes outcomes/effects. |
| effective | productive, potent | Potent is strong but less common in IELTS essays. |
| necessary | essential, imperative | Imperative is strong and formal. |
| significant | substantial, considerable | Usually linked to amount/degree/impact. |
| successful | fruitful, prosperous | Prosperous is often financial or business-related. |

## Accuracy Filter for IELTS
### Direct safe substitutions
- important -> crucial, vital
- good -> beneficial, positive
- necessary -> essential
- significant -> considerable, substantial

### Context-dependent substitutions
- effective -> productive (process/work results)
- effective -> potent (strong effect, careful tone)
- successful -> prosperous (money/business focus)
- necessary -> imperative (urgent/formal claims)

## Example Sentences
- It is crucial to improve waste-management systems in large cities.
- Public transport investment can have a beneficial impact on air quality.
- Early intervention is essential for long-term educational outcomes.
- The policy led to a considerable reduction in traffic congestion.
- Small reforms can still produce fruitful results over time.
