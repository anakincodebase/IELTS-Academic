### Not direct synonyms of slow (use carefully)

- old (age, not speed)
- poor (economic status, not speed)
- small (size, not speed)
- difficult (complexity, not speed)

## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- gradual and steady are excellent for trend description
- sluggish and stagnant imply negative economic performance
- delayed and behind schedule fit project/process contexts
- cautious and phased are useful when progress is intentionally controlled

Extra guidance:
- Distinguish no change from slow change: stagnant means no meaningful growth, while gradual means slow but real movement.
- Pair with clear nouns: gradual increase, delayed implementation, sluggish demand.

## Example Sentences
- Population growth remained gradual over the period.
- The project was delayed due to budget constraints.
- Economic recovery was slow and uneven across regions.
- Demand became sluggish after the initial boom.
- The policy was introduced in a phased manner.
- Progress has been steady but slower than expected.
- Construction is currently behind schedule because of supply issues.
