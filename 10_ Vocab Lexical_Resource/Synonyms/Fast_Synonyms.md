### Related words (context-dependent)
- sudden, abrupt, dynamic, direct, efficient, early, soon
- immediate-effect, short-term, instant

### Not direct synonyms of fast (use carefully)
- good (quality, not speed)
- easy (difficulty, not speed)
- short (length, not speed)
- modern (time period, not speed)
- urgent (importance, not speed)

## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- rapid and swift are formal and strong in Task 1 and Task 2
- sudden and abrupt imply unexpected change, not just speed
- immediate is useful for policy impact claims but should be realistic
- streamlined and efficient are excellent for systems/processes

Extra guidance:
- Use adverb-adjective pairing carefully: rose rapidly, changed swiftly, responded promptly.
- Avoid overusing dramatic speed words without evidence.

## Example Sentences
- Internet use grew rapidly during the decade.
- The government needs a swift response to public health emergencies.
- Digital payment offers a faster and more convenient service.
- The company adopted a streamlined system to reduce delays.
- Urban expansion has been quick-paced in recent years.
- Emergency teams must provide prompt support after disasters.
- The trend shows an accelerated rise after 2015.
