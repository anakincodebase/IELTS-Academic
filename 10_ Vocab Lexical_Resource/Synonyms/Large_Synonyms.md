### Related words (context-dependent)
- colossal, gigantic, gargantuan, mammoth, monumental
- bulky, voluminous, hefty, oversized, jumbo
- dense, crowded, packed, populous
- comprehensive, far-reaching, widespread (scope/impact words)
- macro, sweeping, full-sized

### Not direct synonyms of large (use carefully)
- important (value, not size)
- serious (severity, not size)
- heavy (weight, not always size)
- long (length only)
- tall (height only)
- deep (depth only)

## IELTS-Safe Usage Notes
- large, big = general everyday use
- substantial, considerable = formal and strong for Task 1/Task 2
- vast, extensive, widespread = excellent for area, impact, or coverage
- gigantic, colossal, gargantuan = very strong; use only for clear extremes

Extra guidance:
- For data and trends, prefer substantial, considerable, and significant growth in scale.
- For places and systems, extensive and vast often sound more academic than very big.
- Do not overuse dramatic words in one paragraph.

## Example Sentences
- The city has a large population and an extensive transport network.
- There was a substantial increase in internet users over the ten-year period.
- The government launched a large-scale housing program in urban areas.
- The museum occupies a vast area and attracts international visitors.
- Online learning now has a widespread impact on higher education.
- The factory handles high-volume production during peak seasons.
- The region experienced a considerable rise in tourism revenue.
