## TIER 2: Abstract Concepts & Complex Ideas (40 Words)

**41. Seminal** /ˈsem-ə-nəl/  
SEM-I-NAL = Important, seed-like foundation | From SEED (sem = seed) | Influential, foundational, pivotal | Trivial | *"Darwin's seminal work transformed biology."*

**42. Obfuscate** /ˈɑb-fə-ˌskeɪt/  
OB-FUSE-CATE = Make fuzzy/confused | OB (against) + FUSE (confuse) | Confuse, obscure, cloud | Clarify | *"The report obfuscates the real issue."*

**43. Verisimilar** /ˌver-ə-ˈsɪm-ə-lər/  
VER-I-SIMILAR = Seemingly true | VERITAS (truth) + SIMILAR = truth-like | Plausible, credible, authentic | Implausible | *"The verisimilar narrative convinced readers."*

**44. Ephemeral** /ɪ-ˈfem-ər-əl/  
EPH-EM-ERAL = Short-lived, temporary | Greek "ephemeral" = lasting one day | Temporary, fleeting, transient | Permanent, eternal | *"Fashion trends are often ephemeral."*

**45. Salient** /ˈseɪ-li-ənt/  
SAIL-IENT = Stand out, prominent | SAIL = stand out like a sail | Prominent, striking, noticeable | Inconspicuous, hidden | *"The salient features of the plan are..."*

**46. Innate** /ɪ-ˈneɪt/  
IN-NATE = Born with, natural | IN (inside) + NATE (birth) = born with | Natural, inborn, intrinsic | Acquired, learned | *"Language is partly innate, partly learned."*

**47. Equivocate** /ɪ-ˈkwɪv-ə-ˌkeɪt/  
E-QUIV-O-CATE = Use equal voices = speak ambiguously | EQUI (equal) + VOC (voice) = equal voice | Hedge, evade, be ambiguous | Be clear, be direct | *"The politician equivocated on the tax policy."*

**48. Insidious** /ɪn-ˈsɪd-i-əs/  
IN-SID-IOUS = Sneaky, hidden danger | Sits INSIDE ready to attack | Sneaky, subtle, treacherous | Obvious, open, direct | *"Insidious discrimination affects hiring practices."*

**49. Superficial** /ˌsu-pər-ˈfɪ-ʃəl/  
SUPER-FISH-AL = On the SURFACE | SUPER (surface) + FISH (surface water) | Surface-level, shallow, cosmetic | Deep, profound | *"The superficial analysis missed real causes."*

**50. Manifold** /ˈmæn-i-ˌfoʊld/  
MAN-I-FOLD = Many folds, multiple | MAN (many) + I + FOLD (layers) = many layers | Multiple, varied, numerous | Single, one, sole | *"The manifold benefits include health and economy."*

---

**51. Esoteric** /ˌes-ə-ˈter-ɪk/  
ES-O-TERIC = For insiders only | Greek "esoteros" = inner | Obscure, specialized, restricted | Exoteric, common, public | *"The esoteric knowledge was shared only among experts."*

**52. Pragmatic** /præɡ-ˈmæt-ɪk/  
PRAG-MAT-IC = Practical, action-based | PRAGMA (deed) + MATIC = deed-focused | Practical, realistic, sensible | Idealistic, theoretical | *"A pragmatic approach focuses on results."*

**53. Inane** /ɪn-ˈeɪn/  
IN-ANE = Empty, lacking substance | IN + ANE (empty, like "vain") = pointless | Silly, foolish, pointless, senseless | Wise, sensible, intelligent | *"The inane comments added nothing to the discussion."*

**54. Ardent** /ˈɑr-dənt/  
AR-DENT = Hot, passionate | From Latin "ardere" (burn) = on fire | Passionate, enthusiastic, fervent | Indifferent, apathetic | *"His ardent support for the cause is unwavering."*

**55. Juxtapose** /ˈdʒʌk-stə-ˌpoʊz/  
JUX-TA-POSE = Place next to | JUX (adjacent) + TA (next to) + POSE (place) | Place side by side, contrast | Separate, distance | *"The artist juxtaposed old and new architecture."*

**56. Lucid** /ˈluː-sɪd/  
LU-CID = Clear, bright, shining | LUC = light = clear like light | Clear, transparent, easy to understand | Unclear, confused, opaque | *"The lucid explanation helped everyone understand."*

**57. Oblivious** /ə-ˈblɪv-i-əs/  
OB-LIV-IOUS = Forgetful, unaware | OB + LETHE (river of forgetfulness) = forgetting | Unaware, heedless, ignorant | Aware, conscious, attentive | *"He was oblivious to the dangers ahead."*

**58. Ostentatious** /ˌɑs-ten-ˈteɪ-ʃəs/  
OS-TEN-TATIOUS = Showy, attention-grabbing | From Latin "ostentare" (display) = show off | Flashy, showy, pretentious | Modest, understated, humble | *"The ostentatious mansion displays their new wealth."*

**59. Perspicacious** /ˌpɜr-spɪ-ˈkeɪ-ʃəs/  
PER-SPIC-ACIOUS = Sharp insight | PER (through) + SPIC (see) + ACIOUS = see through clearly | Shrewd, insightful, keen | Dull, obtuse, unperceptive | *"Her perspicacious analysis revealed hidden patterns."*

**60. Sagacious** /sə-ˈɡeɪ-ʃəs/  
SAG-ACIOUS = Wise, sharp judgment | SAG (sage = wise) + ACIOUS = wise-like | Wise, discerning, sensible | Foolish, unwise, poor judgment | *"The sagacious leader navigated complex politics."*

---

**61. Platitude** /ˈplæt-ə-ˌtuːd/  
PLAT-I-TUDE = Flat, trite statement | PLAT (flat) + I + TUDE (state/attitude) = flat statements | Cliché, trite remark, obvious truth | Original, fresh, unique | *"His speech was full of platitudes."*

**62. Raucous** /ˈrɔ-kəs/  
RAU-COUS = Loud, harsh | RAU (raw sound) + COUS = coarse/harsh sound | Loud, harsh, crude | Quiet, gentle, refined | *"The raucous laughter disturbed the class."*

**63. Sanguine** /ˈsæŋ-ɡwɪn/  
SANG-WINE = Blood-red, optimistic | SANG (blood) = bloodred optimism | Optimistic, positive, hopeful | Pessimistic, negative, gloomy | *"He was sanguine about the project's prospects."*

**64. Sardonic** /sɑr-ˈdɑn-ɪk/  
SAR-DON-IC = Bitter, mocking humor | From Sardinia = bitter laughter | Mocking, sarcastic, bitter | Sincere, genuine, earnest | *"Her sardonic smile masked her disappointment."*

**65. Sedate** /sɪ-ˈdeɪt/  
SE-DATE = Calm, composed, serious | SE (itself) + DATE = itself dated/settled = calm | Calm, dignified, quiet | Agitated, excitable, restless | *"The sedate pace of the conference bored attendees."*

**66. Turgid** /ˈtɜr-dʒɪd/  
TUR-GID = Swollen, bloated, grandiose | TUR (turbid/murky/swollen) + GID = bloated writing | Bloated, pompous, bombastic | Simple, concise, elegant | *"The turgid prose made the simple idea confusing."*

**67. Truculent** /ˈtrʌk-jə-lənt/  
TRUCK-ULENT = Combative, aggressive | TRUCK (confrontation) + ULENT = prone to confrontation | Aggressive, combative, belligerent | Peaceful, gentle, cooperative | *"His truculent attitude antagonized colleagues."*

**68. Vacuous** /ˈvæk-ju-əs/  
VAC-UOUS = Empty, hollow, lacking substance | VACUUM = empty | Empty, inane, stupid | Intelligent, substantial, meaningful | *"The vacuous argument didn't address the real problem."*

**69. Vivacious** /vɪ-ˈveɪ-ʃəs/  
VI-VÁ-CIOUS = Lively, energetic, animated | VIVA (alive) + CIOUS = full of life | Lively, spirited, animated | Dull, lifeless, boring | *"Her vivacious personality brightened the room."*

**70. Vituperative** /vaɪ-ˈtuː-pər-ə-tɪv/  
VI-TUP-ER-ATIVE = Abusive, bitter language | VIT (bitter/vituperate) + UPERATIVE = bitterly | Abusive, scathing, harsh | Praise, compliment, gentle | *"The vituperative article attacked the policy harshly."*

## TIER 3: Problem & Solution Words (40 Words)

**71. Mitigate** /ˈmɪt-ə-ˌɡeɪt/  
MIT-I-GATE = Make less severe | MIT (less) + GATE = pass through reduced | Reduce, soften, ease | Worsen, aggravate, intensify | *"Education can mitigate social inequality."*

**72. Rectify** /ˈrek-tə-ˌfaɪ/  
REC-TI-FY = Make right/correct | RECTUS (straight/right) + FY = make straight | Correct, fix, remedy | Worsen, break, damage | *"We must rectify the systemic errors."*

**73. Atone** /ə-ˈtoʊn/  
A-TONE = Make amends, repent | AT (at) + ONE (one) = become one again (reconcile) | Repent, make amends, apologize | Offend, insult, wound | *"He atoned for his past mistakes."*

**74. Alleviate** /ə-ˈlev-i-ˌeɪt/  
AL-LEVI-ATE = Make light/less burden | AL + LEVI (light/levity) + ATE = make light | Ease, relieve, reduce | Worsen, increase, burden | *"The medication alleviates chronic pain."*

**75. Counteract** /ˌkaʊn-tər-ˈækt/  
COUNTER-ACT = Act against something | COUNTER (opposite) + ACT = opposite action | Oppose, neutralize, offset | Support, enhance, assist | *"Exercise counteracts the effects of a sedentary lifestyle."*

---

**76. Redress** /rɪ-ˈdres/  
RE-DRESS = Correct again, remedy | RE (again) + DRESS (set right) = set right again | Correct, remedy, compensate | Ignore, worsen, dismiss | *"The court will redress the injustice."*

**77. Remedial** /rɪ-ˈmē-dē-əl/  
REM-ED-IAL = Intended to remedy | REMEDY + IAL = pertaining to remedy | Corrective, healing, therapeutic | Harmful, damaging, worsening | *"Remedial classes help struggling students."*

**78. Palliate** /ˈpæl-i-ˌeɪt/  
PAL-LI-ATE = Make bearable, ease | PALLIUM (cloak) = cover/ease pain | Ease, comfort, soothe | Aggravate, intensify, worsen | *"The treatment palliates symptoms but doesn't cure."*

**79. Preclude** /prɪ-ˈkluːd/  
PRE-CLUDE = Prevent, make impossible beforehand | PRE (before) + CLUDE (close) = close off before | Prevent, make impossible, exclude | Allow, permit, enable | *"This contract preclude future disputes."*

**80. Abrogate** /ˈæb-roʊ-ˌɡeɪt/  
AB-ROGATE = Cancel, nullify, repeal | AB (away/off) + ROGATE (demand) = demand off/cancel | Cancel, repeal, abolish | Establish, enforce, maintain | *"The government abrogated the old law."*

---

**81. Expurgate** /ˈek-spər-ˌɡeɪt/  
EX-PURGATE = Remove bad parts, censor | EX (out) + PURGATE (cleanse) = cleanse by removing | Remove, censor, delete | Include, keep, maintain | *"The publisher expurgated offensive language."*

**82. Obviate** /ˈɑb-vi-ˌeɪt/  
OB-VI-ATE = Make unnecessary, remove need for | OB (against) + VIA (way) + ATE = remove the way/need | Remove, eliminate, make unnecessary | Create, necessitate, require | *"New technology obviates manual labor."*

**83. Absolve** /əb-ˈzɑlv/  
AB-SOLVE = Free from blame, pardon | AB (from) + SOLVE (loosen/free) = free from | Pardon, exonerate, forgive | Blame, accuse, hold responsible | *"The evidence absolved him of guilt."*

**84. Assuage** /ə-ˈswāj/  
AS-SUAGE = Calm, ease, pacify | AS + SUAGE (soothe) = soothe | Calm, ease, soften | Intensify, irritate, worsen | *"Music assuages her anxiety."*

**85. Appease** /ə-ˈpēz/  
APP-EASE = Make peaceful, satisfy | APP (to) + PEASE (peace) = bring peace | Satisfy, calm, pacify | Anger, irritate, provoke | *"Concessions may appease critics temporarily."*

## TIER 4: Abundance & Scarcity (40 Words)

**86. Copious** /ˈkoʊ-pi-əs/  
CO-PIOUS = Abundant, plentiful | COPIAE (Latin = abundance) = lots of | Abundant, plentiful, ample | Scarce, meager, limited | *"He provided copious evidence for his claim."*

**87. Paucity** /ˈpɔ-sə-ti/  
PAU-CITY = Scarcity, fewness | PAUCUS (few) + ITY = state of fewness | Scarcity, shortage, lack | Abundance, plenty, excess | *"A paucity of resources limited the project."*

**88. Surfeit** /ˈsɜr-fɪt/  
SUR-FIT = Excess, too much | SUR (over/super) + FIT (amount/fit) = too much | Excess, surplus, overabundance | Shortage, deficit, scarcity | *"A surfeit of choices creates decision paralysis."*

**89. Dearth** /ˈdɜrθ/  
DEARTH = Shortage, scarcity | From "dear" (scarce/expensive) + TH = scarcity | Shortage, scarcity, lack | Abundance, plenty, excess | *"There's a dearth of affordable housing."*

**90. Profusion** /prə-ˈfjuː-ʒən/  
PRO-FUSION = Abundant outpouring | PRO (forward) + FUSION (pouring) = abundant pouring | Abundance, plenty, profligacy | Scarcity, shortage, sparse | *"The garden grew in wild profusion."*

## TIER 5: Doubt & Certainty (40 Words)

**91. Equivocal** /ɪ-ˈkwɪv-ə-kəl/  
E-QUIV-OCAL = Ambiguous, having two meanings | EQUI (equal) + VOC (voice) + AL = having equal voices | Ambiguous, unclear, indefinite | Clear, certain, unambiguous | *"The evidence was equivocal and inconclusive."*

**92. Indubitable** /ɪn-ˈduː-bɪ-tə-bəl/  
IN-DUBI-TABLE = Impossible to doubt | IN (not) + DUBITABLE (doubtable) = not doubtable | Certain, indisputable, unquestionable | Doubtful, questionable, uncertain | *"His expertise is indubitable."*

**93. Spurious** /ˈspjʊr-i-əs/  
SPUR-IOUS = False, not genuine | SPURIUS (bastard/false) = not legitimate/false | False, fake, fraudulent | Genuine, authentic, true | *"The spurious evidence was rejected by courts."*

**94. Irrefutable** /ˌɪr-rɪ-ˈfjuː-tə-bəl/  
IR-REFUTABLE = Cannot be denied/disputed | IR (not) + REFUTABLE (can be refuted) = cannot be refuted | Indisputable, undeniable, conclusive | Disputable, questionable, debatable | *"His record provided irrefutable proof."*

**95. Credible** /ˈkred-ə-bəl/  
CRED-IBLE = Worthy of belief | CREDENCE (belief) + IBLE = worthy of belief | Believable, convincing, trustworthy | Unbelievable, false, dubious | *"Her testimony was credible and compelling."*

## TIER 6: Relationships & Connections (40 Words)

**96. Synergy** /ˈsɪn-ər-dʒi/  
SYN-ERGY = Combined effect greater than sum | SYN (together) + ERGY (energy/work) = working together | Cooperation, combined effect, partnership | Conflict, opposition, disconnection | *"Team synergy improved project outcomes."*

**97. Dichotomy** /daɪ-ˈkɑt-ə-mi/  
DI-CHOT-OMY = Division into two parts | DI (two) + CHOT (cut) + OMY (way) = two-cut way | Division, split, contrast | Unity, integration, connection | *"The urban/rural dichotomy affects policy."*

**98. Correlation** /ˌkɔr-ə-ˈleɪ-ʃən/  
COR-RELATION = Relationship between two things | COR (together) + RELATION = related together | Relationship, connection, association | Independence, isolation, unrelated | *"Studies show correlation between sleep and grades."*

**99. Convergence** /kən-ˈvɜr-dʒəns/  
CON-VERGENCE = Coming together, meeting | CON (together) + VERGENCE (turning) = turning together | Coming together, merging, meeting | Divergence, separation, splitting | *"Technology and biology show convergence in biotech."*

**100. Divergence** /daɪ-ˈvɜr-dʒəns/  
DI-VERGENCE = Moving apart, separating | DI (apart/two) + VERGENCE (turning) = turning apart | Separation, difference, splitting | Convergence, unity, similarity | *"Economic policies show significant divergence between nations."*

## TIER 7: Strength & Weakness (40 Words)

**101. Robust** /roʊ-ˈbʌst/  
RO-BUST = Strong, sturdy | ROBUR (strength/oak) = strong like oak | Strong, solid, sturdy | Weak, fragile, delicate | *"The robust economy supports growth."*

**102. Enervate** /ˈen-ər-ˌveɪt/  
EN-ERVE-ATE = Weaken, deprive of strength | EN + NERVE (strength) + ATE = take away strength | Weaken, exhaust, drain | Strengthen, energize, invigorate | *"The disease enervated his body."*

**103. Fortify** /ˈfɔr-tə-ˌfaɪ/  
FOR-TI-FY = Strengthen, reinforce | FORT (strong/fortress) + FY = make strong | Strengthen, reinforce, fortify | Weaken, undermine, deteriorate | *"Education fortifies critical thinking skills."*

**104. Debilitate** /dɪ-ˈbɪl-ə-ˌteɪt/  
DE-BILI-TATE = Weaken, make weak | DE (from) + BILITY (strength) + ATE = take away strength | Weaken, exhaust, incapacitate | Strengthen, empower, revitalize | *"Long-term illness debilitates patients."*

**105. Impotent** /ˈɪm-pə-tənt/  
IM-POTENT = Weak, powerless | IM (not) + POTENT (powerful) = not powerful | Weak, powerless, ineffectual | Powerful, strong, capable | *"The institution became impotent after losing funding."*
