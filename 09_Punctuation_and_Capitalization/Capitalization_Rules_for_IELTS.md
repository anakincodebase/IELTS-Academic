# Capitalization Rules for IELTS

## Core Objective
> [!info] Rule
> Capitalization should be accurate and consistent. Random capital letters reduce perceived control.

## 1) Always Capitalize
- First word of every sentence
- Proper nouns (people, countries, cities, institutions)
- Days, months, official holidays
- Nationalities and languages (Bangladeshi, English)

Examples:
- The survey was conducted in Bangladesh.
- English is widely used in higher education.

## 2) Usually Not Capitalized
- common nouns in general use (government, university, city)
- school subjects in general (math, biology) unless part of a course title
- seasons (spring, summer) unless at sentence start

## 3) Task 1 Data and Label Control
Capitalize exactly as shown in chart labels and titles when reporting data.
- If a chart says "Public Transport," keep that exact capitalization.
- Avoid changing case inconsistently across paragraphs.

## 4) Common IELTS Errors
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: many People migrate to cities.
>
> Correct: Many people migrate to cities.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: The graph compares internet usage in january and february.
>
> Correct: The graph compares internet usage in January and February.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: the united nations supports this policy.
>
> Correct: The United Nations supports this policy.

## 5) Formal Title Use
Capitalize full official names:
- Ministry of Education
- World Health Organization

Lowercase for generic mention:
- the ministry
- the organization

## Mnemonic Bank
- CAPS start: Sentence starts and proper names start with capitals.
- Name it, cap it: If it is a specific official name, capitalize it.
- Generic goes lower: Common nouns stay lowercase.
- Calendar rule: Months and days get capitals; seasons do not.

## 20-Second Capitalization Scan
- Sentence starts capitalized?
- Proper nouns capitalized?
- Random capitals removed?
- Chart label capitalization consistent?
