# Spelling Rules for IELTS

## Core Objective
> [!info] Rule
> Strong grammar scores depend on spelling accuracy. Frequent spelling errors reduce clarity and can lower your language accuracy profile.

## 1) i before e Rule (and Exceptions)
General pattern:
- i before e except after c

Examples:
- believe, achieve
- receive, ceiling

Common exceptions:
- weird, science, efficient

## 2) Final Silent e Rule
When adding a suffix, often drop final e:
- make -> making
- improve -> improving

Keep e before suffixes starting with consonants:
- safe -> safely
- wise -> wisely

## 3) Double Consonant Rule
Double final consonant when:
- one syllable word
- single vowel + single consonant
- adding vowel suffix

Examples:
- plan -> planning
- stop -> stopped
- begin -> beginning

Do not double when stress pattern does not require it:
- open -> opening
- visit -> visited

## 4) -tion, -sion, -cian Endings
-tion: education, pollution, information
-sion: decision, revision, expansion
-cian: musician, politician

## 5) -able vs -ible
No single universal rule, so memorize high-frequency IELTS words:
- acceptable, available, sustainable
- possible, responsible, visible

## 6) Common Prefix Accuracy
- un-: unclear, unfair
- dis-: disadvantage, disagreement
- re-: rewrite, reconsider
- mis-: misunderstand, misinterpret

## 7) British vs American Spelling Consistency
Choose one style and stay consistent.

British style (often preferred in IELTS contexts):
- colour, labour, behaviour
- organise, recognise
- centre, theatre

American style:
- color, labor, behavior
- organize, recognize
- center, theater

## 8) Hyphen Control in Academic Writing
Use hyphens for clarity in compound adjectives before nouns:
- long-term policy
- evidence-based solution
- low-income families

## 9) High-Risk IELTS Words to Memorize
- accommodation
- environment
- government
- development
- separate
- definitely
- necessary
- successful
- opportunity
- education

## Mnemonic Bank
- SEED check: Suffix, Ending e, Extra consonant, Dialect consistency.
- GOVT memory: government has one n, two m letters.
- DEFinitely: think finite inside definitely.
- SEPARate: think PAR in the middle.

## 45-Second Spelling Scan
- Any word repeated with two different spellings?
- Any high-risk words unchecked?
- Hyphenated adjective before noun where needed?
- British or American style used consistently?
