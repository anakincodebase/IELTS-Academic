# Common Misspelled Words for IELTS

## Core Objective
> [!info] Rule
> Master high-frequency misspellings first. This gives the fastest accuracy gain for Writing and speaking note preparation.

## 1) Grammar and Function Words
- separate (not seperate)
- definitely (not definately)
- necessary (not neccessary)
- occurred (not occured)
- beginning (not begining)
- argument (not arguement)
- grammar (not grammer)
- language (not langauge)
- whether (not wether)
- because (not becuase)

## 2) Education and Society Vocabulary
- accommodation (not accomodation)
- university (not univercity)
- curriculum (not curiculum)
- government (not goverment)
- parliament (not parliment)
- society (not socity)
- community (not commuity)
- citizen (not citzen)
- individual (not indivisual)
- responsibility (not responsiblity)

## 3) Economy and Development Vocabulary
- development (not developement)
- economic (not econimic)
- business (not buisness)
- entrepreneur (not enterpreneur)
- employment (not employement)
- salary (not sallary)
- financial (not finanical)
- investment (not investement)
- opportunity (not oppurtunity)
- sustainable (not sustainible)

## 4) Environment and Science Vocabulary
- environment (not enviroment)
- pollution (not polution)

- biodiversity (not biodivercity)
- greenhouse (not green house when used as one word noun)
- species (not speciees)
- scientific (not scienctific)
- technology (not tehnology)

## 6) Academic and Argumentation Vocabulary
- analysis (not analyis)
- thesis (not thsis)
- evidence (not evidance)
- research (not reserch)

- whose / who's
- accept / except

- principal / principle
- compliment / complement
- lose / loose
- quiet / quite

## Mnemonic Triggers
- Accommodation: two c + two m.
- Necessary: one c + two s.
- Separate: think PAR in separate.
- Government: one n, not two.
- Environment: n before m in the middle cluster.

## 60-Second Word Accuracy Drill
- Circle every long word (8+ letters) and verify spelling.
- Check all words from this list used in your essay.
- Fix one family at a time: education, economy, environment, health.
- Re-read final paragraph backward word by word for typo detection.
