# IELTS Band 8.5 Punctuation and Capitalization Checklist

## How to Use (2 Minutes)
Run this checklist after drafting and before final submission.

## 1) Sentence Boundaries
- Every sentence has a clear subject and verb.
- No sentence fragments.
- No run-on sentences.

## 2) Comma Control
- Comma after opening dependent clauses.
- No comma splice between two full sentences.
- List commas used clearly.

## 3) Semicolon and Colon Control
- Semicolons only between related independent clauses.
- Colons only before a list, explanation, or result.
- No overuse (clarity first).

## 4) Apostrophe Accuracy
- Possession forms correct (student's vs students').
- No apostrophes for simple plurals.
- its/it's and whose/who's checked.

## 5) Quotation Mark Control
- Quotes used only when necessary.
- Punctuation style around quotation marks is consistent.
- No fake citations.

## 6) Capitalization Accuracy
- Sentence starts capitalized.
- Proper nouns capitalized.
- Months, days, nationalities, and languages correct.
- No random capital letters in common nouns.

## 7) Spelling Accuracy
- High-risk words checked (government, environment, accommodation, separate).
- No text-message spellings in formal writing.
- Same word spelled the same way across the essay.

## 8) IELTS-Specific Final Sweep
- Task 1: chart labels and units copied with consistent capitalization.
- Task 2: punctuation supports argument flow, not complexity for its own sake.
- Speaking notes: contractions natural in speech, formal writing remains controlled.

## Mnemonic Trigger (Final 10 Seconds)
- C-S-A-C-S: Comma, Semicolon, Apostrophe, Capitals, Spelling.
- Read the final sentence backward for typo detection.

> [!success] Target Standard
> If punctuation and capitalization errors are rare and non-systematic, your grammar accuracy profile is stronger for Band 8 to 8.5 performance.
