# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Commas, Semicolons, and Colons</span>

## <span style="color: #00838f">Core Objective</span>
> [!info] Rule
> Band 8.5 candidates use punctuation to show logic clearly, not to decorate sentences.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Comma Rules You Must Control</span>
Use a comma after an opening dependent clause:
- Although public transport is improving, traffic remains severe.
- If governments invest early, long-term costs decrease.

Use commas to separate items in a list:
- The plan targets schools, hospitals, and local roads.

Use commas around non-essential information:
- My brother, who lives in Dhaka, works in finance.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Because pollution increased the city introduced restrictions.
>
> ✅ CORRECT: Because pollution increased, the city introduced restrictions.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Avoid Comma Splices</span>
A comma splice joins two independent clauses with only a comma.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Housing costs are rising, many young adults delay marriage.
>
> ✅ CORRECT: Housing costs are rising, so many young adults delay marriage.
>
> ✅ CORRECT: Housing costs are rising; therefore, many young adults delay marriage.
>
> ✅ CORRECT: Housing costs are rising. Many young adults delay marriage.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">When to Use a Semicolon</span>
Use a semicolon to join closely related independent clauses.
- The policy is expensive; however, it may deliver long-term benefits.
- Public trust is low; reforms must be transparent.

Quick test:
- If both sides can stand as full sentences, a semicolon may work.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">When to Use a Colon</span>
Use a colon to introduce:
- a list
- an explanation
- a key result

Examples:
- Three sectors need urgent funding: health, education, and transport.
- The reason is clear: wages have not kept pace with inflation.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">IELTS Writing Application</span>
- Task 1: Use colons for clean data commentary in overview sections.
- Task 2: Use semicolons sparingly to link ideas with mature control.
- Keep sentence length moderate; punctuation should improve clarity.

## Mnemonic Bank
- Comma after opener: If the sentence opens with a helper clause, pause once with a comma.
- Semicolon test: Full sentence; full sentence.
- Colon cue: Think "point, then reveal".
- Anti-splice rule: One comma cannot hold two full sentences alone.

## 30-Second Punctuation Scan
- Any comma splice present?
- Dependent-clause commas used correctly?
- At most 1 to 2 semicolons in an essay?
- Colons only used for valid introductions?
