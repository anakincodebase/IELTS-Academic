# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Apostrophes and Quotation Marks</span>

## <span style="color: #00838f">Core Objective</span>
> [!info] Rule
> Apostrophe errors are small but frequent; repeated mistakes can lower grammatical accuracy.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Apostrophes for Possession</span>
Singular noun + 's:
- the student's score
- the government's policy

Plural noun ending in s + ':
- students' essays
- teachers' feedback

Irregular plural + 's:
- children's books
- people's attitudes

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Apostrophes for Contractions</span>
Common contractions:
- don't, can't, it's, they're, we'll

IELTS writing preference:
- In formal Task 2 essays, prefer full forms (do not, cannot, it is).
- In speaking, contractions are natural and acceptable.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Its important to reduce waste.
>
> ✅ CORRECT: It's important to reduce waste.
>
> ❌ WRONG: The company changed it's strategy.
>
> ✅ CORRECT: The company changed its strategy.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">High-Frequency Confusions</span>
- its (possessive) vs it's (it is)
- whose (possessive) vs who's (who is)
- students (plural noun) vs student's (singular possessive)

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Quotation Marks in IELTS Writing</span>
Use quotation marks mainly for:
- direct quotes from a source
- highlighting a term under discussion

Examples:
- The report described social media as a "double-edged tool."
- Many people misuse the term "success."

Guidelines:
- Use quotes sparingly in Task 2.
- Do not rely on fake quotations to sound academic.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Punctuation with Quotation Marks</span>
Standard exam-safe pattern:
- Place commas and periods inside quotation marks if following a style that requires it.
- Keep style consistent across the essay.

## Mnemonic Bank
- Possession apostrophe: Owner first, apostrophe next.
- Its vs it's: Expand it is. If expansion works, use it's.
- Plural warning: Plurals almost never need apostrophes.
- Quotation rule: Quote rarely, quote real, quote consistently.

## Quick Check Before Submission
- Any confusion between its and it's?
- Any unnecessary apostrophes in plurals?
- Quotation marks used only where meaningful?
- Style consistent from start to finish?
