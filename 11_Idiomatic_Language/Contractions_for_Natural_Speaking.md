# Contractions for Natural Speaking

## Why Contractions Matter
Contractions make your speech sound natural and fluent. In speaking, they are usually better than fully expanded forms unless you are emphasizing something.

## Band 8.5 Rule
Use contractions naturally, but keep grammar accurate.

## Spoken Reductions (Use Carefully)
These are common in natural speech but can sound too casual in formal contexts.

| Reduction | Full Form | IELTS Use |
|---|---|---|
| gonna | going to | Use rarely; prefer going to in exam answers. |
| wanna | want to | Better to avoid in IELTS exam speech. |
| gotta | got to / have got to | Better to avoid. |
| kinda | kind of | Occasional use is acceptable in casual examples. |
| sorta | sort of | Occasional use is acceptable. |
| dunno | do not know | Avoid in exam; use I don't know. |
| lemme | let me | Avoid in exam. |

## Common Errors to Avoid
1. Do not write contractions in formal IELTS writing tasks.
2. Do not mix tense forms incorrectly, for example I would gone.
3. Remember that he'd can mean he had or he would. Context decides.
4. Do not overuse ultra-casual reductions.

## Drill: 30-Second Speaking Warm-Up
1. Say one sentence with I am and then convert it to I'm.
2. Do this with 15 high-frequency forms.
3. Record and check whether your contractions are clear, not swallowed.
