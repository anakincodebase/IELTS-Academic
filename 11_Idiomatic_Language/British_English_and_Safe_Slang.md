# British English and Safe Slang

## Important IELTS Rule
Use British expressions only if you can use them accurately and naturally.

## Common British Colloquialisms (IELTS-Safe)

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| not my cup of tea | not something I enjoy | Horror films are not my cup of tea. |
| a bit | a little | I was a bit nervous in the interview. |
| quite | fairly / very (context dependent) | The task was quite challenging. |
| sorted | organized / resolved | My schedule is sorted for this week. |
| fair enough | reasonable point | Fair enough, I can see your argument. |
| give it a go | try it | I might give it a go next month. |
| packed | very busy / full | The city center is packed on weekends. |
| ages | a long time | I have not seen him for ages. |
| spot on | exactly right | Your feedback was spot on. |
| over the moon | very happy | I was over the moon when I passed. |

## Common British Slang (Use Selectively)

| Slang | Meaning | IELTS Safety |
|---|---|---|
| mate | friend | Safe in personal stories if natural. |
| knackered | very tired | Usually safe in casual answers. |
| chuffed | pleased/proud | Safe but use only if you are confident. |
| gutted | very disappointed | Safe in emotional contexts. |
| dodgy | suspicious/unreliable | Safe when describing problems. |
| rubbish | very bad / nonsense | Safe and common in UK English. |
| cheeky | slightly rude but playful | Use carefully; context matters. |
| skint | having no money | Better to use only in casual examples. |
| bloke | man | Casual; use sparingly. |
| fancy (verb) | like / want | Safe: I fancy trying that cafe. |

## Slang You Should Usually Avoid in IELTS
- innit
- gonna / wanna (as default forms)
- bloody (can sound offensive in some contexts)
- extreme internet slang

## Mini Example Answers

### Q: Do you like your neighborhood?
Yes, mostly. It is quite peaceful and the people are friendly. The only downside is traffic, because roads are packed in the evening.

### Q: How do you feel after exam success?
I was over the moon when I got my target score, mainly because I had worked on it for ages.

### Q: Do you enjoy team projects?
Usually yes. If everyone is responsible, things get sorted quickly.

## Pronunciation Reminder
If you use British vocabulary, keep pronunciation clear. Accent is not graded, intelligibility is.
