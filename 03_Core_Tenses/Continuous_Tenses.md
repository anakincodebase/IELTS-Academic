# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Continuous Tenses</span>

Continuous tenses (also called progressive tenses) emphasize actions in progress at a specific moment. Simple present just states facts ("I read books"). Present continuous shows something happening right now ("I am reading a book").

In speaking and writing, continuous tenses add variety and precision. They're essential for Band 8 grammatical range.

---

## <span style="color: #00838f">Present Continuous</span>

**Use for:** Actions happening right now, temporary situations, current trends.

**Form:** Subject + am/is/are + base verb + ing

"I am writing my essay right now."  
"Digital adoption is accelerating across developing nations."  
"She is studying engineering because she's interested in infrastructure."

**Common IELTS use:** Describing ongoing trends in Task 1. "Car ownership is increasing in suburbs while bus ridership is declining in rural areas."

---

## <span style="color: #00838f">Past Continuous</span>

**Use for:** An action in progress at a specific past moment, usually before an interruption.

**Form:** Subject + was/were + base verb + ing

"She was writing her essay when the power went out."  
"While governments were debating the issue, emissions were rising steadily."

Past continuous sets the scene or background. Simple past brings the main event.

"She was studying for her exam (background) when her phone rang (main event)."

Formulas:
- Affirmative: Subject + am/is/are + base verb + ing + object
- Negative: Subject + am/is/are not + base verb + ing + object
- Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + base verb + ing + object?
- Negative question: Am/Is/Are + subject + not + base verb + ing + object?

Examples:
- She is writing a letter while her brother is preparing dinner.
- Because the library is quiet tonight, she is writing her assignment there, and she is focusing better.
- She is not writing to complain, although the service was poor; she is writing to suggest improvements.
- Is she writing the conclusion now, or is she still organizing the main points?
- Is she writing more confidently because she has practiced this structure for weeks?

## Past Continuous
Usage:
- an action in progress at a specific past time
- background action before interruption

Formulas:
- Affirmative: Subject + was/were + base verb + ing + object
- Negative: Subject + was/were not + base verb + ing + object
- Question: Was/Were + subject + base verb + ing + object?
- Negative question: Was/Were + subject + not + base verb + ing + object?

Examples:
- She was writing a letter when the power went out, but she continued by candlelight.
- While her friends were chatting outside, she was writing her notes because the exam was near.
- She was not writing quickly because she was checking each sentence carefully, and she wanted to avoid errors.
- Was she writing the introduction when you entered, or had she already moved to the body paragraph?
- Was she writing to the editor because the article contained inaccurate statistics?

## <span style="color: #00838f">Future Continuous</span>

Usage:
- action that will be in progress at a certain future time

Formulas:
- Affirmative: Subject + will/shall + be + base verb + ing + object
- Negative: Subject + will/shall not + be + base verb + ing + object
- Question: Will/Shall + subject + be + base verb + ing + object?
- Negative question: Will/Shall + subject + not + be + base verb + ing + object?

Examples:
- She will be writing her Task 2 response at 8 PM, while her classmates will be revising vocabulary.
- When you call tonight, she will be writing in the study room, and she will need ten more minutes.
- She will not be writing at noon because she will be attending a speaking session, but she will continue later.
- Will she be writing the conclusion when we arrive, or will she be proofreading by then?
- Will she be writing more fluently by next month because she will be practicing every day?

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">IELTS Application</span>
> [!example] IELTS Example
> Speaking Part 1: I am preparing for my exam this month because my test date is close, and I want to improve my fluency.

> [!example] IELTS Example
> Speaking Part 2: I was studying when the lights went out, but I continued with a flashlight until my battery died.

> [!example] IELTS Example
> Task 2 context: The wealth gap is widening in many countries, and it is creating social tension as living costs keep rising.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Common Errors</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: She is write a letter.
>
> Correct: She is writing a letter.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: Were she studying at 8 PM?
>
> Correct: Was she studying at 8 PM?

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Functional Use (Advanced)</span>
- Use present continuous for current changes and temporary behavior in Task 2 discussions.
- Use past continuous to build background before a main past event in narratives.
- Use future continuous to show polite planning, expected progress, or neutral future activity.
- Use continuous forms when duration in progress matters more than completion.
- Pair continuous clauses with contrast markers (while, whereas) to produce higher-complexity sentences.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Advanced Tense Flow</span>
> [!tip] Flow Pattern
> Past background (continuous) -> Main event (simple past)

- I was revising my notes when the examiner announced a room change, so I moved quickly.
- While living costs are rising, many families are cutting non-essential expenses.
- At 9 PM tomorrow, I will be preparing my outline, and my partner will be checking the data.

## <span style="color: #00838f">Grammar Rules Checklist</span>
- Continuous aspect always needs be + V-ing.
- Match be to subject and time: am/is/are for present, was/were for past.
- Use future continuous as will + be + V-ing.
- Do not use stative verbs in continuous forms in formal usage (know, believe, own, understand).
- Use past continuous for interrupted actions and simple past for the interrupting action.
- Use while for parallel ongoing actions and when for interruption points.
- Keep auxiliary order in questions: auxiliary + subject + V-ing.
- In negatives, place not after be.
- Spelling with V-ing: run -> running, write -> writing, lie -> lying.
- Avoid tense drift inside one timeline unless there is a clear shift in reference time.
