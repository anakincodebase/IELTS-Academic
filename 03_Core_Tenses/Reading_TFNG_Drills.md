# Reading TFNG (True/False/Not Given) Drills & Strategy

## Why TFNG is Hardest Reading Question Type

**Examiner Wisdom:**
- TFNG tests: Reading precision (not just comprehension)
- Band 6: Guesses (50% accuracy) | Band 7: Recognizes qualifiers (70%) | Band 8: Catches nuance (95%+)
- Most common mistake: Choosing "False" when answer is "Not Given" (examiners deliberately include this confusion)

**Critical Distinction:**
```
FALSE = Statement contradicts passage text directly
NOT GIVEN = Statement could be true, but passage doesn't say it (not contradictory, just absent)

Example:
Passage: "The policy reduces emissions by 10%."

Statement 1: "The policy increases emissions." → FALSE (directly contradicts)
Statement 2: "The policy helps the environment." → NOT GIVEN (plausible but not stated)
```

---

## THE GOLDEN RULE: 3-STEP DECISION TREE

```
STEP 1: Find the EXACT topic in the passage
├─ If NO mention anywhere → "NOT GIVEN" ✓
├─ If mentioned → Go to STEP 2
│
STEP 2: Does the statement MATCH what the passage says?
├─ YES, exact match or obvious paraphrase → "TRUE" ✓
├─ NO, contradicts the passage → Go to STEP 3
│
STEP 3: Is it a careful paraphrase or qualified (hedged)?
├─ If passage says "some factors influence X" and statement says "all factors influence X" → "FALSE" ✓
│  (Band 6 gets this wrong; chooses "NOT GIVEN")
├─ If passage simply doesn't comment on this aspect → "NOT GIVEN" ✓
```

---

## THE QUALIFIER TRAP (Band 6→7 Difficulty)

**Definition**: Qualifiers are words that limit or hedge claims:
```
Absolutes: all, every, always, never, completely, entirely, only
Moderates: some, many, most, often, sometimes, usually, generally, arguably, may, might
```

**The Trap:**

Passage: "**Most** students benefit from online learning."

Statement A: "All students benefit from online learning." → **FALSE** ✓
- (Passage says "most" NOT "all"; changes meaning)

Statement B: "Some students benefit from online learning." → TRUE OR NOT GIVEN?
- **NOT GIVEN** in strict reading! Passage doesn't say "some"
- BUT in Band 8 thinking: If "most" are true, then "some" is logically true
- **Band 7 answer: TRUE** (inferential logic acceptable here)

**Key Insight**: IELTS accepts logical inference but NOT wild assumptions.

## 20 TFNG DRILLS WITH EXPLANATIONS

### Drill Set 1: Exact Matching (True/False/Not Given)

**Passage (Read This First):**
```
"A study by researchers at Cambridge University examined the effectiveness of a new teaching method. 
Over 1,000 students participated in the trial. The method, which emphasizes interactive learning, 
showed positive results in mathematics but limited improvement in language subjects."
```

**Drill 1.1**: "More than 1,000 students participated in the study."
- Find the fact: "Over 1,000 students" = "more than 1,000" ✓
- Answer: **TRUE** ✓

**Drill 1.2**: "The new teaching method improved results across all subjects."
- Find the fact: "showed positive results in mathematics but limited improvement in language subjects"
- Statement says "all subjects"; passage says "only mathematics well" 
- Answer: **FALSE** ✓ (Qualifier trap: "all" vs specific subjects)

**Drill 1.3**: "The study involved researchers from Cambridge University."
- Find the fact: "researchers at Cambridge University"
- Direct match: YES
- Answer: **TRUE** ✓

**Drill 1.4**: "The interactive learning method was developed by Cambridge researchers."
- Find the fact: Passage says researchers "examined" the method, not that they "developed" it
- No evidence of who created it
- Answer: **NOT GIVEN** ✓ (Common Band 6 error: assumes "examined" = "created")

**Drill 1.5**: "Language subjects showed no improvement from the method."
- Find the fact: "limited improvement in language subjects"
- Statement says "no improvement"; passage says "limited" (not zero)
- Answer: **FALSE** ✓ (Qualifier precision)

### Drill Set 2: Hedging Word Recognition

**Passage (New):**
```
"Climate change may significantly affect agricultural productivity in sub-Saharan Africa. 
Preliminary analysis suggests that temperature increases could reduce crop yields by up to 30%. 
However, adaptation strategies might mitigate some of these effects, though comprehensive 
implementation remains challenging."
```

**Drill 2.1**: "Climate change will definitely affect agricultural productivity in sub-Saharan Africa."
- Find: "may significantly affect" (not "will definitely"; "may" = possibility)
- Statement says "will definitely" (absolute)
- Passage: qualified with "may" (uncertain)
- Answer: **FALSE** ✓ (Changes certainty level)

**Drill 2.2**: "Temperature increases could reduce crop yields."
- Find: "temperature increases could reduce crop yields by up to 30%"
- Direct match: YES
- Answer: **TRUE** ✓

**Drill 2.3**: "Adaptation strategies will completely eliminate the negative effects of climate change."
- Find: "might mitigate some of these effects" (NOT "all/completely")
- Statement: "completely eliminate" (absolute, all effects)
- Passage: "mitigate some" (partial, some effects)
- Answer: **FALSE** ✓ (Scope change: "some" vs "all")

**Drill 2.4**: "Implementing adaptation strategies is easy and straightforward."
- Find: Passage says "comprehensive implementation remains challenging"
- Statement claims "easy"; passage says "challenging" (opposite)
- Answer: **FALSE** ✓

**Drill 2.5**: "Sub-Saharan Africa will be among the regions most affected by climate change."
- Find: Passage doesn't compare Africa to other regions
- No basis for "among the most affected" comparison
- Answer: **NOT GIVEN** ✓ (Information not in passage)

### Drill Set 3: Inference NOT Allowed (Band 7 Common Error)

**Passage (New):**
```
"The smartphone industry has grown dramatically in the last decade. Sales figures for 
leading manufacturers have increased by 200% since 2010. Battery technology has improved, 
and screen sizes have expanded."
```

**Drill 3.1**: "Smartphone manufacturers are profitable."
- Find: Cannot find this statement in passage; only sales growth mentioned
- Answer: **NOT GIVEN** ✓ (Do NOT infer "sales growth = profits"; not stated)

**Drill 3.2**: "Battery technology in smartphones has improved since 2010."
- Find: "Battery technology has improved" (no date qualifying, but context = last decade)
- Can we infer "since 2010"? YES, from context (2010 mentioned as baseline)
- Answer: **TRUE** ✓ (Logical inference acceptable here)

**Drill 3.3**: "Consumers prefer larger smartphone screens."
- Find: Passage says "screen sizes have expanded" (manufacturers expanded sizes)
- Statement claims "consumers prefer" (demand/preference)
- Does expanding supply prove consumer preference? NO—could be manufacturer push
- Answer: **NOT GIVEN** ✓ (Cannot confirm preference from product expansion)

**Drill 3.4**: "The smartphone industry is competitive."
- Find: Passage says "leading manufacturers" (implies competition)
- Can we infer competition from "leading manufacturers"? YES (logical)
- Answer: **TRUE** ✓ OR **NOT GIVEN** ✗ (Depends on inference tolerance; safer: NOT GIVEN)

**KEY LEARNING**: When in doubt about inference acceptability, choose "NOT GIVEN" when statement is not explicitly confirmed.

### Drill Set 4: Negation Traps

**Passage (New):**
```
"The new building code does not permit construction in flood-prone areas without reinforcement. 
Several developers have expressed concerns about the restrictions, particularly in urban areas 
where space is limited. However, environmental groups support the measure, arguing that 
protecting ecosystems is essential for long-term sustainability."
```

**Drill 4.1**: "The new building code permits construction in flood-prone areas."
- Find: "does NOT permit construction in flood-prone areas without reinforcement"
- Negation rephrased: Construction IS permitted, BUT only with reinforcement (not "without")
- Statement removes the condition: "permits construction in flood-prone areas" = incomplete truth
- Answer: **NOT GIVEN** ✓ (Statement incomplete; doesn't acknowledge the "with reinforcement" condition)

**Drill 4.2**: "Developers support the new building code restrictions."
- Find: "developers have expressed concerns about the restrictions"
- Opposite of support: concerns
- Answer: **FALSE** ✓

**Drill 4.3**: "Environmental groups believe protecting ecosystems benefits long-term sustainability."
- Find: "environmental groups support the measure, arguing that protecting ecosystems is essential for long-term sustainability"
- Match: YES (direct statement of their belief)
- Answer: **TRUE** ✓

**Drill 4.4**: "The building code does not restrict construction in urban flood-prone areas."
- Find: "does not permit construction in flood-prone areas" + "particularly in urban areas"
- Statement says "does not restrict" (but passage says "does not permit", which IS a restriction)
- Semantic trap: "restrict" vs "not permit" = same thing
- Answer: **FALSE** ✓ (Passage clearly states restrictions via "does not permit")

### Drill Set 5-20: [20-Question TFNG Assessment]

**Instructions for Self-Practice**: 
1. Read passage once (2 min max)
2. Answer each statement: T/F/NG (don't look back initially)
3. Review: Find exact evidence for each answer
4. Identify: Which were qualifier traps? Inference issues? Negation issues?

**Passage for Drills 5-20:**
```
"Educational technology has revolutionized classroom learning in developed nations. Interactive 
whiteboards, online platforms, and digital resources enable teachers to present information more 
engagingly. However, implementation remains uneven; rural schools often lack infrastructure investment. 
A 2023 survey found that 75% of urban schools have adopted at least one digital tool, while only 30% 
of rural schools report similar adoption rates. Experts suggest that this digital divide will widen 
unless governments commit substantial funding to rural connectivity."
```

**Drills 5-20 Statements** (Complete these yourself):

1. "Educational technology has become standard in all schools worldwide." (F)
2. "75% of urban schools have adopted digital tools." (T)
3. "Rural schools lack physical classroom space." (NG)
4. "Experts believe government funding could reduce the digital divide." (T)
5. "The digital divide will definitely worsen without intervention." (F—hedged as "will widen")
6. "Interactive whiteboards are used equally in urban and rural areas." (F)
7. "The 2023 survey examined 1,000 schools." (NG)
8. "Digital resources present information more engagingly than traditional methods." (T)
9. "Rural schools receive less investment in technology infrastructure." (T)
10. "Teachers in rural areas are less skilled at using technology." (NG)
11. "Online platforms have revolutionized how students learn worldwide." (F—"revolutionized" limited to developed nations)
12. "30% adoption rate indicates strong digital inclusion in rural schools." (NG)
13. "Governments in developed nations have fully addressed the digital divide." (NG)
14. "Schools without digital tools are less effective at teaching." (NG)
15. "The survey compared urban and rural school adoption rates." (T)
16. "Infrastructure investment is necessary for rural digital adoption." (T—implied)
17. "Digital resources present information to students." (T—implied in "enable teachers to present")
18. "Rural schools are unable to use digital tools." (F—"often lack" ≠ "unable")
19. "Only developed nations have implemented educational technology." (NOT T exactly—passage says "developed nations" but doesn't claim exclusivity; NG safer)
20. "Educational technology adoption varies between urban and rural areas." (T—obvious from data)

## FINAL CHECKLIST: Before Marking TFNG Answer

Before you submit each answer, ask:

✓ **Is the statement a direct quote or obvious paraphrase?** 
  - If YES → Likely TRUE
  - If NO, passage somewhat addresses it → Continue

✓ **Does the statement contain "all," "never," "always," "completely"? Does passage contain opposite qualifier?**
  - If YES → Likely FALSE
  - If NO → Continue

✓ **Does the passage directly address this point AT ALL?**
  - If clearly YES → If statement matches text = TRUE; if contradicts = FALSE
  - If NO → NOT GIVEN

✓ **Am I inferring beyond what the text explicitly says?**
  - If YES, and inference is major → Change to NOT GIVEN
  - If YES, and inference is basic logic → Acceptable as TRUE
