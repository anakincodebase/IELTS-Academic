# IELTS Task Sentence Templates: 

These are the exact sentence structures Band 9 writers use. Copy these patterns. Fill in your own information.

---

## Task 1 (Academic) Writing: Charts, Graphs, Data

### Pattern 1: Describing Overall Trend

**Template:**
```
The [data/chart/graph] demonstrates that [main trend] over the period [time frame].
```

**Examples:**
- "The chart demonstrates that global temperatures increased over the period 1990-2020."
- "The data demonstrates that smartphone sales rose significantly over the decade."

**Why it works:** Clear topic sentence. Sets expectations for reader.

**Your turn:**
- "The _____ demonstrates that _____ over the period _____."

---

### Pattern 2: Comparing Two Data Sets

**Template:**
```
[First category] increased significantly, while [second category] showed a contrasting trend of [direction].
```

**Examples:**
- "Urban populations increased significantly, while rural populations showed a contrasting trend of decline."
- "Employment in manufacturing fell steeply, while service sector jobs showed a contrasting trend of growth."

**Why it works:** Explicitly contrasts two trends. Sophisticated.

**Your turn:**
- "[First] increased significantly, while [second] showed a contrasting trend of [direction]."

---

### Pattern 3: Describing a Peak or Valley

**Template:**
```
[Variable] peaked at [number/percentage] in [year], before declining to [number] by [year].
```

**Examples:**
- "Unemployment peaked at 8.5% in 2009, before declining to 3.8% by 2019."
- "Sales peaked at $500 million in Q2, before stabilizing at $400 million thereafter."

**Why it works:** Precise data description. Professional tone.

**Your turn:**
- "[Variable] peaked at [data] in [year], before declining to [data] by [year]."

---

### Pattern 4: Describing Fluctuation

**Template:**
```
The [variable] fluctuated throughout the period, beginning at [number] and culminating in [number].
```

**Examples:**
- "The exchange rate fluctuated throughout the period, beginning at 1.20 and culminating in 1.45."
- "Annual rainfall fluctuated throughout the decade, beginning at 500mm and culminating in 650mm."

**Why it works:** Accurate for volatile data. Band 8+ language.

**Your turn:**
- "The [variable] fluctuated throughout the period, beginning at [data] and culminating in [data]."

---

### Pattern 5: Identifying Most/Least Significant Change

**Template:**
```
The most significant change occurred in [category/time period], where [variable] [direction] by [magnitude].
```

**Examples:**
- "The most significant change occurred in 2008, where housing prices fell by 15%."
- "The most significant change occurred in the technology sector, where investment increased by 200%."

**Why it works:** Highlights key data. Analytical.

**Your turn:**
- "The most significant change occurred in [time/category], where [variable] [direction] by [magnitude]."

---

### Pattern 6: Ending Conclusion Sentence (Task 1)

**Template:**
```
In conclusion, [variable] demonstrated a general trend of [direction], with [magnitude] change over the [time period].
```

**Examples:**
- "In conclusion, global trade demonstrated a general trend of growth, with 45% increase over the 20-year period."
- "In conclusion, fertility rates demonstrated a general trend of decline, with a 50% reduction over the 30-year period."

**Why it works:** Summarizes main point. Professional closure.

**Your turn:**
- "In conclusion, [variable] demonstrated a general trend of [direction], with [magnitude] change over the [time period]."

## Task 2 (General or Academic) Writing: Essays

### Pattern 1: Topic Sentence with Opinion (Introduction Paragraph, Final Sentence)

**Template:**
```
While [acknowledge other perspective], I [strongly] believe that [your main argument] because [primary reason].
```

**Examples:**
- "While some argue that technology disconnects people, I strongly believe that technology enhances human connection because it enables instant communication across continents."
- "While critics claim that social media is harmful, I believe that social media has transformed society because it democratizes information sharing."

**Why it works:** Acknowledges both sides (balanced). States your position clearly. Gives reason immediately.

**Your turn:**
- "While [acknowledge], I [strongly] believe that [argument] because [reason]."

---

### Pattern 2: Topic Sentence for Body Paragraph

**Template:**
```
The most compelling argument for [your position] is that [topic] [verb] [significant impact].
```

**Examples:**
- "The most compelling argument for higher education is that education increases lifetime earning potential."
- "The most compelling argument for environmental protection is that climate change threatens agricultural productivity."

**Why it works:** Sets up body paragraph. Clear direction.

**Your turn:**
- "The most compelling argument for [position] is that [topic] [verb] [impact]."

---

### Pattern 3: Example Sentence

**Template:**
```
For instance, [specific example] demonstrates that [claim], evidenced by [supporting detail].
```

**Examples:**
- "For instance, the smartphone revolution demonstrates that technology improves efficiency, evidenced by studies showing 40% productivity gains."
- "For instance, the rise of remote work demonstrates that flexibility benefits employees, evidenced by reduced stress-related health issues."

**Why it works:** Specific example + evidence. Concrete and credible.

**Your turn:**
- "For instance, [example] demonstrates that [claim], evidenced by [detail]."

---

### Pattern 4: Counter-Argument Acknowledgment

**Template:**
```
Admittedly, [opposing argument] [verb] [valid point]. However, [your counter-point] [outweighs/overrides] this concern because [reason].
```

**Examples:**
- "Admittedly, automation creates job displacement, a valid concern. However, history of technological advancement overrides this concern because new industries always emerge."
- "Admittedly, social media can be addictive, a valid point. However, regulation and education override this concern because they address the root cause."

**Why it works:** Shows critical thinking. Not one-sided.

**Your turn:**
- "Admittedly, [opposing] [verb] [point]. However, [counter] [outweighs] this concern because [reason]."

---

### Pattern 5: Consequence Sentence

**Template:**
```
Consequently, [action/change] will lead to [result], ultimately resulting in [broader impact].
```

**Examples:**
- "Consequently, investing in renewable energy will reduce carbon emissions, ultimately resulting in a healthier planet."
- "Consequently, improving access to education will increase social mobility, ultimately resulting in reduced inequality."

**Why it works:** Shows causal thinking. Future-oriented.

**Your turn:**
- "Consequently, [action] will lead to [result], ultimately resulting in [broader impact]."

---

### Pattern 6: Conclusion Sentence (Final Summary)

**Template:**
```
In summary, while [acknowledge counterpoint], [restate main position] is [adjective] because [key reason], and society benefits from [outcome].
```

**Examples:**
- "In summary, while automation does displace some jobs, technological advancement is inevitable because it drives economic growth, and society benefits from increased productivity."
- "In summary, while online education cannot fully replace traditional learning, it is transformative because it increases access, and society benefits from more educated populations."

**Why it works:** Balanced, restates position, shows benefit.

**Your turn:**
- "In summary, while [counterpoint], [main position] is [adjective] because [reason], and society benefits from [outcome]."

## Universal (Task 1 + Task 2) Patterns

### Pattern 1: Introducing a Definition

**Template:**
```
By [term], we mean [definition], which encompasses [key features].
```

**Examples:**
- "By sustainable development, we mean economic growth that does not harm the environment, which encompasses renewable energy and conservation."
- "By artificial intelligence, we mean computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, which encompasses learning and adaptation."

**Why it works:** Clarifies terms. Sophisticated.

**Your turn:**
- "By [term], we mean [definition], which encompasses [features]."

---

### Pattern 2: Presenting Statistical Information

**Template:**
```
According to recent data, [statistic], suggesting that [implication].
```

**Examples:**
- "According to recent data, 67% of jobs will be automated by 2050, suggesting that workforce retraining is urgent."
- "According to recent data, global carbon emissions increased by 2% last year, suggesting that climate action is insufficient."

**Why it works:** Introduces data credibly. Shows research awareness.

**Your turn:**
- "According to recent data, [statistic], suggesting that [implication]."

---

### Pattern 3: Cause and Effect

**Template:**
```
Because [cause], [effect] occurs, which [consequence].
```

**Examples:**
- "Because fossil fuels emit carbon dioxide, global warming occurs, which threatens coastal communities."
- "Because education improves skills, employability increases, which reduces unemployment."

**Why it works:** Clear causal chain. Band 8+ thinking.

**Your turn:**
- "Because [cause], [effect] occurs, which [consequence]."

---

### Pattern 4: Comparison

**Template:**
```
Unlike [first thing], [second thing] [difference]. This is because [reason].
```

**Examples:**
- "Unlike traditional learning, online education provides flexibility. This is because students can study at their own pace."
- "Unlike printed books, e-books offer searchability. This is because digital text can be instantly indexed."

**Why it works:** Clear contrast. Educational.

**Your turn:**
- "Unlike [first], [second] [difference]. This is because [reason]."

---

### Pattern 5: Strong Assertion (Use Sparingly)

**Template:**
```
It is undeniable that [claim], as evidenced by [proof/example].
```

**Examples:**
- "It is undeniable that education improves life outcomes, as evidenced by studies showing higher earners are more educated."
- "It is undeniable that climate change is accelerating, as evidenced by rising global temperatures."

**Why it works:** Emphatic. Use only when claim is strong.

**Your turn:**
- "It is undeniable that [claim], as evidenced by [proof]."

---

### Pattern 6: Balancing Perspectives

**Template:**
```
On one hand, [perspective 1]. On the other hand, [perspective 2]. Nevertheless, [your synthesis/position].
```

**Examples:**
- "On one hand, technology enables instant communication. On the other hand, it reduces face-to-face interaction. Nevertheless, technology's benefits outweigh these concerns."
- "On one hand, globalization creates economic opportunities. On the other hand, it threatens local cultures. Nevertheless, balanced regulation can preserve both."

**Why it works:** Shows nuance. Not black-and-white thinking.

**Your turn:**
- "On one hand, [perspective 1]. On the other hand, [perspective 2]. Nevertheless, [synthesis]."

## Common Errors in These Patterns

### Error: Template Too Obvious

**✗:** "The chart demonstrates that the data shows an increase."  
**✓:** "The chart demonstrates that global temperatures rose consistently."

Fill in with specific details, not repetition.

---

### Error: Over-Filler Words

**✗:** "It is very important to note that it seems that the data possibly indicates..."  
**✓:** "The data indicates..."

Remove "very," "possibly," "it seems." Be direct.

---

### Error: Inconsistent Tense in Template

**✗:** "Because fossil fuels emit carbon, global warming occurred, which threatens..."  
**✓:** "Because fossil fuels emit carbon, global warming occurs, which threatens..."

Keep tense consistent.

## Template Selection by Task Type

| Task | Primary Templates to Use | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Task 1 (Charts)** | Trends, comparisons, peaks/valleys, conclusions | Task 1 = describing data |
| **Task 1 (Process)** | Cause-and-effect, sequence, consequences | Task 1 = explaining steps |
| **Task 2 (Opinion)** | Opinion statement, examples, counter-arguments, conclusion | Task 2 = persuasion |
| **Task 2 (Discussion)** | Both perspectives, synthesis, consequences | Task 2 = balanced view |

## Key Rule: Don't Just Copy

**These templates are PATTERNS, not formulas.**

✗ Bad: Copy word-for-word from template
✓ Good: Use template structure, fill with YOUR information

**Example:**
- Template: "The [data/chart/graph] demonstrates that [main trend]..."
- ✗ Bad: "The chart demonstrates that the data shows a trend..."
- ✓ Good: "The chart demonstrates that smartphone adoption increased 400% between 2010-2020..."
