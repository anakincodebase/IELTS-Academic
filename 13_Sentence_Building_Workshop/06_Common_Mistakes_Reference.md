# Quick Fix Reference: 30 Common Writing Mistakes (Band 3-9)

**When you write and something feels wrong, check this page.**

Each mistake shows: **Error ✗ | Fix ✓ | Why | IELTS Impact**

---

## Grammar Errors (Highest Priority)

### 1. Subject-Verb Agreement

**Error ✗:** She study English.  
**Fix ✓:** She studies English.  
**Why:** 3rd person singular (he, she, it) needs -s on the verb.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 6 scorer catches this. Loses 1 point per error.

---

### 2. Verb Tense Inconsistency

**Error ✗:** I go to school and studied there for three years.  
**Fix ✓:** I went to school and studied there for three years.  
**Why:** Don't mix present and past tense in the same sentence.  
**IELTS Impact:** Examiner thinks you don't understand tenses. Major mark loss.

---

### 3. Incomplete Sentence (Fragment)

**Error ✗:** The government implemented many policies.  
**Fix ✓:** The government implemented many new policies to address the crisis.  
**Why:** Sometimes the fragment is too vague. Add specific object/detail.  
**IELTS Impact:** Sounds Band 3. Need complete, clear sentences.

---

### 4. Run-On Sentence (Comma Splice)

**Error ✗:** The economy improved, people felt optimistic.  
**Fix ✓:** The economy improved, so people felt optimistic. (OR) The economy improved. People felt optimistic. (OR) The economy improved; people felt optimistic.  
**Why:** Two complete sentences need a conjunction or semicolon, not just a comma.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 5-6 scorer marks this as a serious error.

---

### 5. Incorrect Verb Form

**Error ✗:** She are coming tomorrow.  
**Fix ✓:** She is coming tomorrow.  
**Why:** "Are" goes with plural (they, we, you). "Is" goes with 3rd singular (she, he, it).  
**IELTS Impact:** Marks are deducted from grammar score.

---

### 6. Wrong Tense for Context

**Error ✗:** Yesterday, I go to the market.  
**Fix ✓:** Yesterday, I went to the market.  
**Why:** "Yesterday" = past time, so use past tense.  
**IELTS Impact:** Shows poor control of tenses.

---

### 7. Missing or Wrong Article (a, an, the)

**Error ✗:** Study is important for students.  
**Fix ✓:** Study is important for students. (OR) The study showed interesting results.  
**Why:** Generally use "the" when something is specific. No article for general statements.  
**IELTS Impact:** Less serious than other errors, but still deducted.

---

### 8. Adjective-Adverb Confusion

**Error ✗:** She speaks English very good.  
**Fix ✓:** She speaks English very well.  
**Why:** After action verbs, use adverbs (well, quickly, clearly). After linking verbs, use adjectives (good, quick, clear).  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 5 scorer marks this. Band 9 scorers don't.

---

### 9. Double Negative

**Error ✗:** I didn't see nobody there.  
**Fix ✓:** I didn't see anybody there. (OR) I saw nobody there.  
**Why:** Don't use two negatives.  
**IELTS Impact:** Sounds uneducated. Band 3 error.

---

### 10. Subject-Pronoun Mismatch

**Error ✗:** When students study hard, they is successful.  
**Fix ✓:** When students study hard, they are successful.  
**Why:** Pronoun "they" is plural, so use "are" not "is."  
**IELTS Impact:** Clear grammar error.

---

## Sentence Structure Errors

### 11. Dangling Modifier

**Error ✗:** Walking down the street, the buildings seemed very tall.  
**Fix ✓:** Walking down the street, I noticed the buildings were very tall. (OR) As I walked down the street, the buildings seemed very tall.  
**Why:** The modifier (walking) should apply to the subject (I), not the buildings.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 7-8 scorer notices this. Makes the sentence sound awkward.

---

### 12. Misplaced Modifier

**Error ✗:** I saw the man running quickly with the telescope.  
**Fix ✓:** With the telescope, I saw the man running quickly. (OR) I saw the running man quickly through the telescope.  
**Why:** Place modifiers near the word they describe.  
**IELTS Impact:** Creates unintended humor or confusion.

---

### 13. Parallel Structure Broken

**Error ✗:** She likes reading, writing, and to speak.  
**Fix ✓:** She likes reading, writing, and speaking.  
**Why:** Keep items in a list in the same form (all nouns, all verbs, etc.).  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 7+ scorer marks this.

---

### 14. Unclear Pronoun Reference

**Error ✗:** The student told the teacher that he should study harder.  
**Fix ✓:** The student told the teacher that the teacher should study harder. (OR) The teacher told the student that the student should study harder.  
**Why:** It's unclear if "he" means the student or teacher.  
**IELTS Impact:** Confuses the reader. Makes you sound Band 5.

---

### 15. Split Infinitive (Minor)

**Error ✗:** To carefully study the problem...  
**Fix ✓:** To study the problem carefully... (OR) Carefully to study the problem... (OR) To carefully study is acceptable in modern English)  
**Why:** Traditional rule says don't put an adverb between "to" and the verb. Modern English is more flexible.  
**IELTS Impact:** Minor. Most modern writing allows this.

---

## Word Choice Errors

### 16. Confusing Similar Words

**Error ✗:** The affect on the economy was significant.  
**Fix ✓:** The effect on the economy was significant.  
**Why:** "Effect" (noun) = result. "Affect" (verb) = influence. "The effect was significant" = the result was significant.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 7+ scorer catches this.

---

### 17. Wrong Preposition

**Error ✗:** I am interested in learning about English.  
**Fix ✓:** I am interested in learning English. (remove "about")  
**Why:** "Interested in" is the correct collocation, not "interested about."  
**IELTS Impact:** Sounds like Band 5. Band 9 writers use the right collocations naturally.

---

### 18. Overusing the Same Word

**Error ✗:** The data shows that data is important. The data proves data is necessary.  
**Fix ✓:** The data shows that information is important. The evidence proves evidence is necessary.  
**Why:** Repetition weakens writing. Use synonyms.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 8+ scorers notice poor lexical range.

---

### 19. Too Formal or Too Casual

**Error ✗:** Yo, the economy is like, totally improving, dude.  
**Fix ✓:** The economy is improving substantially.  
**Why:** Academic writing requires formal register.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 3-4 error. Band 9 writers maintain formality.

---

### 20. Incorrect Collocation

**Error ✗:** Make a decision.  
**Fix ✓:** Make a decision. (this is actually OK)  
Actually: **Error ✗:** Do a research.  
**Fix ✓:** Conduct research.  
**Why:** Research is uncountable. You "conduct" research, not "do" research.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 6-7 scorer notices. Sounds unnatural to Band 8+ scorers.

---

## Punctuation Errors

### 21. Missing Comma in a Complex Sentence

**Error ✗:** Because the economy improved sales increased.  
**Fix ✓:** Because the economy improved, sales increased.  
**Why:** Add a comma after introductory clause.  
**IELTS Impact:** Small deduction, but frequent errors add up.

---

### 22. Comma Before "That"

**Error ✗:** I believe, that education is important.  
**Fix ✓:** I believe that education is important.  
**Why:** Don't use comma before "that" in formal writing.  
**IELTS Impact:** Minor error. Modern English sometimes allows it.

---

### 23. Semicolon Misuse

**Error ✗:** The economy improved; which helped many people.  
**Fix ✓:** The economy improved, which helped many people. (OR) The economy improved. This helped many people.  
**Why:** Semicolon connects two independent clauses, not a clause to a dependent clause.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 7+ scorer notices this.

---

### 24. Apostrophe Misuse

**Error ✗:** The student's are studying hard. / Its' important.  
**Fix ✓:** The students are studying hard. / It's important.  
**Why:** "Students" (plural) doesn't need apostrophe. "It's" = "it is."  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 5 error. Band 9 writers never do this.

---

### 25. Period in the Middle of Thought

**Error ✗:** The economy improved. Which helped sales.  
**Fix ✓:** The economy improved, which helped sales.  
**Why:** "Which helped sales" is a dependent clause, not a complete sentence.  
**IELTS Impact:** Sounds choppy. Band 5-6 error.

---

## Spelling & Capitalization Errors

### 26. Common Misspellings

**Error ✗:** occured, recieve, seperate, begining  
**Fix ✓:** occurred, receive, separate, beginning  
**Why:** These are common spelling mistakes in English.  
**IELTS Impact:** Examiner marks spelling errors. Each wrong = 1 point loss usually.

---

### 27. Improper Capitalization

**Error ✗:** the government implemented new policies.  
**Fix ✓:** The government implemented new policies.  
**Why:** Capitalize the first word of a sentence.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 5 error. Shows lack of attention.

---

### 28. Proper Noun Not Capitalized

**Error ✗:** I studied in france and london.  
**Fix ✓:** I studied in France and London.  
**Why:** Proper nouns (place names, people names) are always capitalized.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 6-7 scorer deducts points.

---

## Advanced Errors (Subtle, but Important)

### 29. Weak Passive Voice (When Active Is Better)

**Error ✗:** It was decided by the government that new policies would be implemented.  
**Fix ✓:** The government decided to implement new policies.  
**Why:** Active is clearer and more direct.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 8+ scorer prefers active voice. Passive overuse = Band 6-7 writing.

---

### 30. Wordiness (Say the Same Thing in Too Many Words)

**Error ✗:** In the modern day and age, it is a fact that the utilization of technology has become increasingly widespread in many aspects of our daily lives.  
**Fix ✓:** Technology is now widespread in daily life.  
**Why:** Concise writing is more powerful.  
**IELTS Impact:** Band 8+ scorers value clarity over verbosity.

## Your Daily Error Check (5 minutes)

**After writing every sentence, ask:**

1. Does the subject match the verb? (she/he/it + -s?)
2. Do all verbs in this sentence use the same tense?
3. Does this sentence have a subject AND verb?
4. Is this sentence complete, or is it a fragment?
5. Did I use an adjective after a linking verb and adverb after an action verb?

If YES to all 5, the sentence is probably correct.

---

## Most Common Errors in IELTS Writing

**These 5 errors appear in 80% of Band 3-6 essays:**

1. **Subject-verb agreement** (she study ✗)
2. **Verb tense inconsistency** (went and study ✗)
3. **Comma splice** (improved, people felt ✗)
4. **Fragment** (Implemented policies ✗)
5. **Wrong article or missing article** (the research ✗ vs research ✓)

Focus on fixing these 5 first. Alone, they can boost your score from Band 6 → Band 7-8.
