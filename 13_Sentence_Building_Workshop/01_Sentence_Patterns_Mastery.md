## Part 1: Task 1 (Academic): Data Description (150 words)

### What IELTS Expects:

✓ Overview of main trend  
✓ Specific data comparisons  
✓ Supporting details  
✓ Clear language (not overly complex)

### Task 1 Sentence Recipe

**Sentence Type 1: Overview (SVO Pattern)**
```
The [chart/graph/table] demonstrates that [main trend].
```

**Sentence Type 2: Specific Data (Noun Phrase + Linking Verb)**
```
[Subject], [participle], [linking verb] [adjective/description].
```

**Sentence Type 3: Comparison (Complex Sentence with subordination)**
```
[First data set] + [coordinator], while [second data set] [verb] [direction].
```

**Sentence Type 4: Peak/Valley (SVO with adverbial information)**
```
[Variable] peaked at [number] in [year], before [direction] to [number].
```

**Sentence Type 5: Conclusion (Complex Sentence)**
```
In summary, [variable] demonstrated a general trend of [direction], culminating in [description].
```

### Full Task 1 Example (150 words)

```
The bar chart illustrates global coffee consumption across five regions over a 20-year period. 

Overall, consumption in Asia increased substantially, while European consumption remained relatively stable. 

Asia, growing consistently throughout the period, emerged as the dominant region by 2020. Coffee consumption in Asia rose from approximately 3 million tons in 2000 to 8 million tons by 2020, representing a 166% increase. 

Europe, though initially the largest consumer, experienced only modest growth of 2 million tons, stabilizing at around 5 million tons by 2020. 

North America and Latin America showed similar trajectories, both increasing gradually to approximately 4 million tons each. Africa's consumption remained minimal throughout, never exceeding 1 million tons.

In conclusion, the data demonstrates a significant shift in global consumption patterns, with Asia's unprecedented growth fundamentally reshaping the industry.
```

**Structures used:**
- Overview: SVO
- Participle phrase: "growing consistently"
- Noun phrase: "166% increase"
- Complex sentences: "though initially the largest..." (contrast)
- Nominalization: "growth," "consumption," "trajectories"

## Part 2: Task 2 (Essays): Opinion/Discussion (250+ words)

### What IELTS Expects:

✓ Clear thesis/position  
✓ 2-3 body paragraphs with arguments + examples  
✓ Conclusion summarizing main points  
✓ Sophisticated but clear language

### Task 2 Sentence Recipe

**Introduction Paragraph:**
- Sentence 1: Hook (general statement)
- Sentence 2: Main issue
- Sentence 3: Your thesis/position

**Body Paragraph (repeat for 2-3 paragraphs):**
- Sentence 1: Topic sentence (your main argument)
- Sentence 2-3: Elaboration (HOW/WHY)
- Sentence 4: Example (concrete evidence)
- Sentence 5: Impact (so what?)

**Conclusion Paragraph:**
- Sentence 1: Restate thesis
- Sentence 2: Implications/broader impact

### Full Task 2 Example (Body Paragraph)

```
The most compelling argument for renewable energy investment is that it mitigates climate change while creating economic opportunities. 

Renewable sources, fundamentally different from fossil fuels, produce energy without emitting carbon dioxide. This distinction is crucial because climate change accelerates at an unprecedented rate, threatening agricultural systems globally. 

For instance, Denmark's substantial investment in wind energy has reduced carbon emissions by 80% since 1990, simultaneously creating 30,000 jobs in the renewable sector. 

Consequently, governments that prioritize renewable development achieve dual benefits: environmental protection and economic growth. This integration of sustainability with prosperity demonstrates that environmental responsibility need not compromise development.
```

**Structures used:**
- Topic sentence: Clear assertion
- Participle phrase: "fundamentally different from fossil fuels"
- Complex sentence: subordination with "because"
- Example: Specific data + consequence
- Nominalization: "investment," "development"
- Relative clause: "that prioritize renewable development"

## Part 3: Advanced Integration Moves

### Move 1: Varying Sentence Length for Effect

**Principle:** Don't make all sentences the same length. Vary for emphasis.

**Short sentence = emphasis:**
```
Technology transforms society. 
This is undeniable.
```

**Medium sentence = explanation:**
```
Technology transforms society by enabling instant communication and democratizing information access.
```

**Long sentence = comprehensive detail:**
```
Technology, advancing ubiquitously across sectors from healthcare to education, transforms society in ways both beneficial and challenging, creating unprecedented opportunities while simultaneously exacerbating existing inequalities.
```

**In combination (effective):**
```
Technology transforms society. 
The effects are profound and multifaceted. 
Globally, individuals now communicate instantly across continents, access infinite information, and collaborate on problems at scale. 
Yet challenges persist.
```

### Move 2: Cohesive Devices (Linking Sentences Together)

**Between sentences, use these words:**
- Moreover / Furthermore (addition)
- However / Nevertheless (contrast)
- Therefore / Consequently (result)
- For instance / Specifically (example)
- In conclusion / To summarize (summary)

**Example (showing cohesion):**
```
Education improves life outcomes. Moreover, it facilitates social mobility. Furthermore, educated populations drive economic growth. Consequently, governments must invest substantially in educational infrastructure.
```

### Move 3: Active vs. Passive Voice (Strategic)

**Active (preferred for Band 9):**
```
"Governments implement policies."
```

**Passive (use when appropriate):**
```
"Policies are implemented by governments."
```

**Strategic use:** Use passive when:
- You want to emphasize the action, not the actor
- The actor is unknown
- You want to sound more formal

## Your Band 9 Checklist (Final Review)

**After writing any IELTS task, check this list:**

### Content (Task Achievement)

- [ ] Does the introduction clearly state your position/topic?
- [ ] Do you have 2-3 well-developed body paragraphs with arguments + examples?
- [ ] Does the conclusion summarize your main points?
- [ ] Are all claims supported by evidence or reasoning?

### Sentence Variety

- [ ] Do I have a mix of sentence lengths (short, medium, long)?
- [ ] Do I have simple, compound, and complex sentences?
- [ ] Do I vary sentence structures (not all SVO)?
- [ ] Do I use advanced structures (noun phrases, participles, relative clauses)?

### Grammar & Accuracy

- [ ] Subject-verb agreement? (she studies, not she study)
- [ ] Consistent tense? (not mixing past and present randomly)
- [ ] Complete sentences? (no fragments)
- [ ] Correct comma usage? (comma before FANBOYS, after intro clause)
- [ ] No run-on sentences? (use period, semicolon, or conjunction)

### Vocabulary & Register

- [ ] Is my vocabulary appropriate for Band 8-9? (not overly simple)
- [ ] Am I using sophisticated but natural words? (not forcing complexity)
- [ ] Are my collocations correct? (not "do a research," but "conduct research")
- [ ] Is my tone consistently academic/formal?

### Linking & Coherence

- [ ] Are my paragraphs connected with linking words (Moreover, However, Consequently)?
- [ ] Does each paragraph flow logically to the next?
- [ ] Are my examples and evidence clearly relevant?
- [ ] Can a reader easily follow my argument?

## Common Week 4 Mistakes (Don't Do These)

**Mistake 1: Too Complex, Unclear**
```
✗ "The amelioration of ubiquitous technological proliferation, which precipitates unprecedented societal delineation, engenders exacerbated disparities."
✓ "Technology's rapid advancement has widened social disparities, requiring targeted policy responses."
```

**Mistake 2: Repeating Simple Structures**
```
✗ "Education is important. Technology is important. Health is important."
✓ "Education, technology, and healthcare are all critical for development."
```

**Mistake 3: Forgetting to Provide Examples**
```
✗ "Technology improves society."
✓ "Technology improves society. For instance, telemedicine enables healthcare access in remote areas."
```

**Mistake 4: Inconsistent Tense**
```
✗ "The government implements policies and increased employment."
✓ "The government implemented policies and increased employment." (past) OR "implements policies and increases employment" (present)
```

**Mistake 5: No Transitions Between Sentences**
```
✗ "Climate change is serious. Scientists agree. We must act. Action is urgent."
✓ "Climate change is serious, and scientists overwhelmingly agree. Consequently, we must act urgently."
```
