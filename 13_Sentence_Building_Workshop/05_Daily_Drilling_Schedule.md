# Daily Drilling Schedule: 70 Minutes to Band 9

**This is your daily routine.** Follow it exactly. Same time every day. Same duration. No shortcuts.

**Time commitment:** 70 minutes per day (morning + afternoon + evening)

---

## Daily Breakdown

### Morning (20 minutes) - Sentence Pattern Drills

**Time: 20 min | Goal: Build automaticity with basic patterns**

#### Week 1-2 (Foundations)

- **Monday-Tuesday (SV & SVO focus)**
  - 10 min: Write 8 simple SV sentences (practice list provided below)
  - 10 min: Write 10 SVO sentences (practice list provided below)
  - CHECK: Every sentence has subject, verb, and (for SVO) object

- **Wednesday-Thursday (SLA focus)**
  - 10 min: Write 8 SLA sentences with "is/are"
  - 10 min: Write 5 sentences with other linking verbs (become, seem, appear)
  - CHECK: Every sentence clearly describes a state or quality

- **Friday (SVIO/DO & SVOC)**
  - 10 min: Write 4 SVIO/DO sentences (give, tell, show, send, bring)
  - 10 min: Write 4 SVOC sentences (find, make, consider, judge)
  - CHECK: Correct word order

- **Saturday (Mixed Review)**
  - 20 min: Write 15 sentences using ANY of the 5 patterns from the week
  - YOU choose which pattern—test yourself

- **Sunday (Light Review)**
  - 20 min: Write 10 sentences reviewing the patterns you struggled with most this week

#### Week 3 (Intermediate - Compound Sentences)

- **Daily (Monday-Sunday)**
  - 10 min: Write 5 compound sentences with AND/BUT/OR
  - 10 min: Write 5 complex sentences with BECAUSE/WHEN/IF/WHILE
  - CHECK: Correct use of conjunctions, no comma splices

#### Week 4 (Advanced - Task-Specific)

- **Daily (Monday-Sunday)**
  - 10 min: Write 3 TASK 1 sentences (describing trends, comparisons)
  - 10 min: Write 3 TASK 2 sentences (arguments, examples, conclusions)
  - CHECK: Sentence matches the task's rhetorical purpose

---

### Afternoon (30 minutes) - Extended Writing Practice

**Time: 30 min | Goal: Apply patterns to longer writing**

#### Week 1 (Monday-Friday)

- **Days 1-3:** Write 8 connected sentences on ONE topic (e.g., your morning routine, your hometown, your favorite hobby)
  - Use the patterns you learned that morning
  - 30 min to write 8 sentences
  - Goal: Sentences flow together, make sense, no errors

- **Days 4-5:** Write a SHORT paragraph (10 sentences) on a topic of your choice
  - Mix different sentence patterns
  - 30 min to write and review

- **Weekends:** Rest or lighter practice (15 min)

#### Week 2 (Monday-Friday)

- **Daily:** Write a 150-word paragraph on an IELTS-like topic (e.g., "Why education is important" OR "How technology affects society")
  - Use 10-12 connected sentences
  - Mix simple, compound, and complex structures
  - 30 min to write and self-check

#### Week 3 (Monday-Friday)

- **Daily:** Write a TASK 1 paragraph (130-150 words) describing data or trends
  - Focus on sentence variety and accuracy
  - 30 min includes time to review and make corrections

- **Weekends:** Write a shorter TASK 2 body paragraph (150 words)

#### Week 4 (Monday-Friday)

- **Daily (M-F):** Write a complete IELTS Task (Task 1 or Task 2)
  - Task 1: 150 words (30 min)
  - Task 2: 250 words (but save for separate, longer practice)
  - Focus on sentence formation accuracy

---

### Evening (20 minutes) - Self-Review & Error Correction

**Time: 20 min | Goal: Learn from mistakes**

#### Every Single Day

**Step 1 (5 min): Read Your Sentences Aloud**
- Listen for awkwardness or choppy phrasing
- If a sentence sounds wrong, mark it

**Step 2 (10 min): Check for These 5 Errors**

Use this checklist for EVERY sentence you write:

1. **Subject-Verb Agreement** ✓
   - Does the verb match the subject?
   - "She study" ✗ → "She studies" ✓
   - "Dogs runs" ✗ → "Dogs run" ✓

2. **Complete Sentences** ✓
   - Does each sentence have a subject AND verb?
   - "Implemented the policy" ✗ (missing subject)
   - "The government implemented the policy" ✓

3. **Object Check (for SVO/SLA/SVOC)** ✓
   - Does the sentence complete the thought?
   - "I believe" ✗ (incomplete)
   - "I believe education is important" ✓

4. **Verb Tense Consistency** ✓
   - Do all verbs in the same timeframe match?
   - "I go to school and studied English" ✗ (mixed present/past)
   - "I go to school and study English" ✓

5. **Adjective vs. Adverb** ✓
   - Are descriptions of nouns adjectives? Are descriptions of verbs adverbs?
   - "She speaks very good" ✗ (should be adverb)
   - "She speaks very well" ✓

**Step 3 (5 min): Write Down Mistakes**
- Keep a "Mistake Log" file
- Every time you make an error, write it:
  - **Error:** "She study English"
  - **Fix:** "She studies English"
  - **Rule:** Subject-verb agreement for 3rd person singular
- Review this log once per week

## Progression Checkpoints

### End of Week 1: Can You Do This?

- [ ] Write 10 SV sentences (simple present, past, future) with NO errors
- [ ] Write 15 SVO sentences with NO errors
- [ ] Write 10 SLA sentences with NO errors
- [ ] Write 5 SVIO/DO sentences with NO errors
- [ ] Write 5 SVOC sentences with NO errors
- [ ] Identify subject-verb agreement errors in your writing
- [ ] Write an 8-sentence paragraph with mostly correct sentences

**If YES:** Move to Week 2  
**If NO:** Repeat Week 1 drill until automatic

### End of Week 2: Can You Do This?

- [ ] Write 10 compound sentences (with and, but, or) with correct punctuation
- [ ] Write 10 complex sentences (with because, when, if, while) with correct subordination
- [ ] Write a 10-sentence paragraph with varied sentence types
- [ ] Identify comma splice errors and fix them
- [ ] Write 150-word IELTS-like paragraph with good sentence variety

**If YES:** Move to Week 3  
**If NO:** Repeat Week 2 until smooth

### End of Week 3: Can You Do This?

- [ ] Write 8 TASK 1 sentences (describing data, comparisons, trends)
- [ ] Write 8 TASK 2 sentences (arguments, supporting examples, conclusions)
- [ ] Write a 130-word TASK 1 paragraph with Band 6-7 accuracy
- [ ] Identify sentence structure errors in the above and fix them
- [ ] Most sentences are complex or compound (not all simple)

**If YES:** Move to Week 4 (Advanced Mastery)  
**If NO:** Repeat Week 3 with more focus

### End of Week 4: Can You Do This?

- [ ] Write a complete TASK 1 (150 words) with Band 8 accuracy (only 1-2 errors max)
- [ ] Write a complete TASK 2 paragraph (200 words) with Band 8 accuracy
- [ ] All sentences are grammatically correct
- [ ] Sentence variety is natural and purposeful (not forced)
- [ ] No subject-verb agreement, verb tense, or word order errors

**If YES:** Celebrate! You've achieved Band 9 sentence formation! 🎉  
**If NO:** Extend Week 4 or review specific error patterns
