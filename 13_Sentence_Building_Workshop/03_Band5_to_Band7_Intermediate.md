# Band 5-7: Compound & Complex Sentences (Week 2 Focus)

**Goal:** Stop writing choppy sentences. Learn to combine ideas smoothly.

**Current problem:** "I sleep. I study. I eat." (3 short sentences)  
**After this week:** "Although I sleep well, I still find studying difficult, which affects my eating schedule." (1 sophisticated sentence)

---

## Part 1: Compound Sentences (Joining Equal Ideas)

**Definition:** Two independent clauses (complete sentences) joined with a coordinator.

### Coordinators: FANBOYS

**F**or, **A**nd, **N**or, **B**ut, **O**r, **Y**et, **S**o

### Pattern:

```
[Independent Clause 1] + [COORDINATOR] + [Independent Clause 2]
```

**CRITICAL:** Use a comma BEFORE the coordinator.

```
✗ "The economy improved but unemployment rose."
✓ "The economy improved, but unemployment rose."
```

### Examples:

**And** (addition):
- "The government implemented new policies, and employment increased."
- "Technology is advancing rapidly, and it transforms industries."

**But** (contrast):
- "Online learning is convenient, but it lacks personal interaction."
- "The strategy worked initially, but long-term results were disappointing."

**Or** (alternatives):
- "Students can study independently, or they can join group sessions."
- "The company will expand globally, or it will focus on domestic markets."

**So** (consequence):
- "Demand increased significantly, so production had to scale up."
- "The data was incomplete, so we conducted additional research."

**Yet** (contrast, similar to but):
- "The task seemed impossible, yet we succeeded."
- "Automation threatens jobs, yet it creates new opportunities."

### Why Compound Sentences Matter:

- Shows you can connect related ideas
- Makes writing flow better
- Band 6+ expectation

### Practice (Week 2, Day 1-2):

Write 10 compound sentences using each coordinator (2 sentences per coordinator):

1. AND:
   - "_____, and _____."
   - "_____, and _____."

2. BUT:
   - "_____, but _____."
   - "_____, but _____."

3. OR:
   - "_____, or _____."
   - "_____, or _____."

4. SO:
   - "_____, so _____."
   - "_____, so _____."

5. YET:
   - "_____, yet _____."
   - "_____, yet _____."

## Part 2: Complex Sentences (Subordination)

**Definition:** One main idea (independent clause) + one supporting idea (dependent clause)

**The dependent clause cannot stand alone.** It needs the main clause.

### Subordinators (Connecting Words)

| **When** | **Why** | **If** | **Although/Though** | **Because** | **That** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Time | Reason | Condition | Contrast | Cause | Direct object |
| When it rains | Because it's raining | If it rains | Although it rains | Because it rains | I think that... |

### Patterns:

**Pattern A:** Dependent clause FIRST
```
[Subordinator + dependent clause], [main clause].
← Comma required
```

**Pattern B:** Dependent clause LAST
```
[Main clause] [subordinator + dependent clause].
← Usually no comma
```

### Examples:

**When** (time):
- "When demand increased, the company expanded production." (dependent first)
- "The company expanded production when demand increased." (dependent last)

**Because** (reason):
- "Because climate change threatens agriculture, investment in adaptation is essential." (dependent first)
- "Investment in adaptation is essential because climate change threatens agriculture." (dependent last)

**Although/Though** (contrast):
- "Although automation creates efficiency, it also displaces workers." (dependent first)
- "Automation creates efficiency, although it displaces workers." (dependent last—comma optional)

**If** (condition):
- "If governments act now, climate disaster can be prevented." (dependent first)
- "Climate disaster can be prevented if governments act now." (dependent last)

**That** (noun clause):
- "I believe that education transforms lives." (dependent last, no comma)
- "That education transforms lives is undeniable." (dependent first, rare in IELTS)

### Why Complex Sentences Matter:

- Shows logical thinking (cause-effect, time, contrast)
- Essential for Band 7+
- IELTS expects this in Task 2

### Common Error: Fragment

**✗** "Because the economy improved." (Just a dependent clause—incomplete)  
**✓** "Because the economy improved, employment increased." (Now complete)

**✗** "When she studied hard." (Just a dependent clause)  
**✓** "When she studied hard, her grades improved." (Now complete)

### Practice (Week 2, Day 3-5):

Write 15 complex sentences (3 per subordinator):

1. **When:**
   - (Dependent first) "When _____, _____."
   - (Dependent first) "When _____, _____."
   - (Dependent last) "_____ when _____."

2. **Because:**
   - (Dependent first) "Because _____, _____."
   - (Dependent first) "Because _____, _____."
   - (Dependent last) "_____ because _____."

3. **Although:**
   - (Dependent first) "Although _____, _____."
   - (Dependent first) "Although _____, _____."
   - (Dependent last) "_____ although _____."

4. **If:**
   - (Dependent first) "If _____, _____."
   - (Dependent first) "If _____, _____."
   - (Dependent last) "_____ if _____."

5. **That:**
   - "I believe that _____."
   - "_____ is true."
   - "_____ is undeniable."

## Part 3: Complex-Compound Sentences

**Definition:** Three or more independent/dependent clauses combined.

**Pattern:** [Independent] + coordinator + [Independent], subordinator [dependent].

### Example:

"The government implemented new policies, and employment increased, which improved the economy."

- Main clause 1: "The government implemented new policies"
- Coordinator: "and"
- Main clause 2: "employment increased"
- Subordinator: "which"
- Dependent clause: "improved the economy"

### Why It Matters:

Shows sophisticated thinking. Band 8+ writing uses this naturally.

### Practice (Week 2, Weekends):

Write 5 complex-compound sentences:

1. "_____, and _____, which _____."
2. "_____, but _____, because _____."
3. "When _____, _____, so _____."
4. "Although _____, _____, and _____."
5. "_____ that _____, while _____."

## Part 4: Punctuation Rules (Critical!)

### Rule 1: Comma Before FANBOYS (Compound Sentences)

```
[Independent clause] , [FANBOYS] [Independent clause]
                      ↑ Always a comma here
```

**✗** "The economy improved and employment rose."  
**✓** "The economy improved, and employment rose."

### Rule 2: Comma After Introductory Dependent Clause

```
[Subordinator + dependent clause] , [Main clause]
                                   ↑ Always a comma here
```

**✗** "Because the economy improved employment rose."  
**✓** "Because the economy improved, employment rose."

### Rule 3: NO Comma if Dependent Clause Comes Last

```
[Main clause] [Subordinator + dependent clause]
              ↑ Usually NO comma
```

**✗** "Employment rose, because the economy improved."  
**✓** "Employment rose because the economy improved."

**Exception:** Use comma if "although/though" clause comes last.  
"Employment rose, although inflation was concerning."

### Rule 4: Semicolon for Related Independent Clauses (Advanced)

```
[Independent clause] ; [Independent clause]
```

**✓** "The economy improved; employment rose."  
(Use this when two independent clauses are closely related. Use semicolon instead of period.)

## Week 2 Error Checklist

After writing each sentence, check:

- [ ] Did I use a comma before FANBOYS (and, but, or, so)?
- [ ] Did I use a comma after an introductory dependent clause?
- [ ] Is my dependent clause attached to an independent clause (not a fragment)?
- [ ] Do my two clauses actually relate to each other (not random)?
- [ ] Is my tense consistent?
