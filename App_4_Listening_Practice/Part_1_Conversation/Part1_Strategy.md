# Part 1: Conversation Strategy

## Part 1 Overview
- **Duration**: ~6 minutes (including time to read questions and answer)
- **Questions**: 10
- **Format**: Casual dialogue between two people (customer/client and service provider)
- **Topics**: Bookings, reservations, inquiries, appointments, payments

## Key Characteristics
- Speaks at **natural speed** with natural stress and intonation
- May contain colloquialisms and contractions
- Background noise is minimal but present
- Speakers may interrupt or repeat information

## Listening Strategies

### Before Listening
1. **Scan all 10 questions** before listening begins
2. **Identify question types**:
   - Multiple choice
   - Short answer
   - Form completion
   - Note completion
3. **Predict content** from question context
4. **Underline key words** you need to listen for

### During Listening
1. **Focus on the first speaker** (usually service provider gives key info)
2. **Listen for direct answers** first, then for implied information
3. **Write key words/abbreviations** - don't try to write complete sentences
4. **Note synonyms** - answers may use different words than questions
5. **Be alert to corrections** - speakers often self-correct (listen to the final version)

### After Listening
1. **Check spelling** of names, places, numbers
2. **Verify tense consistency** in your answers
3. **Review any missed sections** - listen to replay if available

## Common Question Types

### Form/Table Completion
- Listen for specific details (names, dates, prices, times)
- Watch for spelling of proper nouns

### Multiple Choice
- Listen carefully for the exact wording
- Distractors often use keywords from the listening

### Short Answer Questions
- Keep answers brief (1-3 words usually)
- Grammar and spelling matter

## Common Traps
- ❌ Writing information about wrong person
- ❌ Mishearing numbers (18/80, 13/30)
- ❌ Confusing similar-sounding words
- ❌ Missing the correction when speaker corrects themselves
- ❌ Writing more than required

## Vocabulary Focus
- Booking/reservation terms
- Money and payment vocabulary
- Time expressions
- Ordinal and cardinal numbers
