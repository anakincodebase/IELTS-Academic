# Part 2: Monologue Strategy

## Part 2 Overview
- **Duration**: ~6 minutes (including reading and answering)
- **Questions**: 10
- **Format**: One person speaking
- **Topics**: Tours, talks, announcements, descriptions, procedures, events

## Key Characteristics
- Speaker is **clear and well-organized**
- Speech is **formal or semi-formal**
- Information flows logically
- Speaker may pause for emphasis
- Scripted content with natural delivery

## Listening Strategies

### Before Listening
1. **Read ALL questions carefully** - this is your roadmap
2. **Identify the topic** from the introduction
3. **Predict the structure** (chronological? categorical? comparative?)
4. **Mark transition signals** in questions (first, next, finally)
5. **Look for patterns** in answer types

### During Listening
1. **Listen to the introduction** carefully - sets context
2. **Follow the speaker's organization** - questions usually follow speaking order
3. **Note main ideas and supporting details**
4. **Listen for signposting language**:
   - "First/Next/Finally"
   - "In addition/Furthermore"
   - "However/But"
   - "For example"
5. **Be ready for rapid information** in lists or enumerations

### After Listening
1. **Check all spelling** carefully
2. **Verify number accuracy** (quantities, times, prices)
3. **Review word forms** - did you write noun when verb was needed?

## Common Question Types

### Sentence Completion
- Complete with 1-3 words
- Grammar must be correct
- Answer must fit grammatically into sentence

### Multiple Choice
- Listen for distractor information carefully
- Right information in wrong context is a trap

### Form Completion
- Information usually presented in order
- Numbers and dates are common

### Labeling a Diagram/Map
- Visual aids help but be careful about speaker's exact language
- Don't rely solely on visual - listen carefully

## Common Traps
- ❌ Confusing similar topics
- ❌ Writing too much (exceeding word limit)
- ❌ Missing numerical information in lists
- ❌ Spelling errors on place names or proper nouns
- ❌ Not checking if answer fits grammatically

## Vocabulary Focus
- Topic-specific vocabulary
- Descriptive adjectives
- Ordinal numbers and sequences
- Measurement and size vocabulary
- Duration and frequency expressions

## Listen for Emphasis
Speakers emphasize important information through:
- Intonation change
- Slower speech
- Repetition
- Pauses
