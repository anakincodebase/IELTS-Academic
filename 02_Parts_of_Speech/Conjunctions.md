# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Conjunctions</span>

Conjunctions are the connectors that hold your sentences together and show how ideas relate. They determine whether you're adding ideas (and), showing contrast (but), explaining cause (because), or setting conditions (if).

Conjunctions are critical for coherence and cohesion in IELTS. They also control your grammatical range—using varied conjunctions shows sophistication. But using them carelessly (over-linking, wrong punctuation) hurts your score.

---

## <span style="color: #00838f">The Main Types</span>

**Coordinating conjunctions** (FANBOYS): for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so. These join two independent clauses of equal importance.

"The city expanded rapidly, and ridership grew steadily."  
"Traffic increased, but public transport remained underused."

**Subordinating conjunctions**: although, because, while, since, unless, if, when. These introduce dependent clauses.

"Although the city expanded infrastructure, congestion persisted."  
"Because funding was delayed, the project stalled."

**Conjunctive adverbs**: however, therefore, consequently, nevertheless. These bridge two independent clauses with a semicolon.

"The policy was controversial; however, it reduced emissions significantly."

## <span style="color: #00838f">Conjunction Concept Framework</span>
| Layer | Key Question | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | Word/phrase/clause connection? | sentence form |
| Logic | Addition, contrast, cause, condition? | coherence |
| Hierarchy | Equal clauses or dependent clause? | complexity range |
| Punctuation | Comma/semicolon/period needed? | accuracy |

## <span style="color: #00838f">Core Theory</span>
> [!info] Rule
> Conjunctions are logic operators: they connect clauses and control how ideas relate.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">GRA Focus (Band 8/8.5)</span>
- balanced coordination + subordination
- punctuation accuracy with connector type
- clear clause-level logic (cause, contrast, condition)

## <span style="color: #00838f">Must Memorize Conjunction Types</span>
| Type | Core Set | IELTS Use |
|---|---|---|
| Coordinating | for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so | join equal clauses |
| Subordinating | although, because, while, since, unless, if | complex clauses |
| Correlative | either...or, neither...nor, both...and, not only...but also | advanced pairing |
| Conjunctive Adverbs | however, therefore, consequently, nevertheless | sentence bridging |

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Function Map</span>
| Function | Core Form | High-Value Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| Addition | and | moreover, furthermore |
| Contrast | but | however, nevertheless |
| Cause | because | given that, since |
| Result | so | therefore, consequently |
| Condition | if | unless, provided that |

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Punctuation Table</span>
| Structure | Model |
|---|---|
| FANBOYS with full clauses | X, but Y |
| Subordinate opener | Although X, Y |
| Conjunctive adverb bridge | X; however, Y |

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Common Errors</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: The city expanded rapidly, however housing remained limited.
>
> Correct: The city expanded rapidly; however, housing remained limited.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: Although traffic is heavy. Public transport is underused.
>
> Correct: Although traffic is heavy, public transport is underused.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">Task Application</span>
- Task 1: use while/whereas for direct comparisons.
- Task 2: vary connectors by logic function.
- Speaking Part 3: use connectors naturally, not excessively.
