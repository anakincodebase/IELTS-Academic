# Collocations

## What Is Collocation?
> [!info] Rule
> Collocation refers to how words go together in fixed or natural relationships.

Using correct collocations makes your English sound natural and improves IELTS Lexical Resource.

## 1) Typical Collocations
- heavy rain
- high temperature
- scenic view
- have an experience

Examples:
- She has blonde hair.
- She was discharged from hospital.

> [!warning] Usage Note
> Less preferred in patient-discharge context: She was released from hospital.
>
> Preferred in medical context: She was discharged from hospital.
>
> Common prison context: He was released from prison.

## 2) Strong Collocations
Strong collocations are less flexible. If you change one word, the phrase often sounds unnatural.

- whisk an egg
- curly hair
- winding road
- blissfully ignorant
- fast train
- fast food
- a quick shower
- a quick meal

## 3) Types of Collocation
There are several types of collocations made from combinations of verbs, nouns, adjectives, and adverbs.

| Combination | Preferred Example | Less Natural in Neutral Academic Register |
|---|---|---|
| adverb + adjective | completely satisfied | downright satisfied |
| adjective + noun | excruciating pain | excruciating joy |
| noun + noun | a surge of anger | a rush of anger |
| noun + verb | lions roar | lions shout |
| verb + noun | commit suicide | undertake suicide |
| verb + expression with preposition | burst into tears | blow up in tears |
| verb + adverb | wave frantically | wave feverishly |

## 4) Sample Collocations by Type

### A) Adverb + Adjective
- Invading that country was an utterly stupid thing to do.
- We entered a richly decorated room.
- Are you fully aware of the implications of your action?

### B) Adjective + Noun
- The doctor ordered him to take regular exercise.
- The Titanic sank on its maiden voyage.
- He was writhing on the ground in excruciating pain.

### C) Noun + Noun
- Let us give Mr Jones a round of applause.
- The ceasefire agreement came into effect at 11 am.
- I would like to buy two bars of soap.

### D) Noun + Verb
- The lion started to roar when it heard the dog barking.
- Snow was falling as our plane took off.
- The bomb went off when he started the car engine.

### E) Verb + Noun
- The prisoner was hanged for committing murder.
- I always try to do my homework in the morning, after making my bed.
- He has been asked to give a presentation about his work.

### F) Verb + Expression with Preposition
- We had to return home because we had run out of money.
- At first her eyes filled with horror, and then she burst into tears.
- Their behavior was enough to drive anybody to crime.

### G) Verb + Adverb
- She placed her keys gently on the table and sat down.
- Mary whispered softly in John's ear.
- I vaguely remember that it was growing dark when we left.

## IELTS Application
- Writing Task 2: use 2-4 accurate collocations in each body paragraph.
- Speaking Part 2/3: use natural chunks, not forced memorized phrases.
- Editing step: remove unnatural word combinations translated from L1.

## Mnemonic Bank
- NWP: Natural Word Pairing.
- FCP: Fixed Chunk Power.
- C3 Check: Correct, Common, Contextual.
