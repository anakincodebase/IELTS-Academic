# Interjections

## What Is an Interjection?
> [!info] Rule
> An interjection is a short expression of sudden emotion, reaction, or discourse response.

## Interjections Inside Parts of Speech (Concept)
Interjections are register-sensitive markers, not core idea carriers.

Use logic:
- acceptable in spontaneous speech (limited)
- weak in formal academic writing
- replace with stance markers for band-safe responses

## Interjection Concept Framework
| Layer | Key Question | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Register | Formal or informal context? | task appropriateness |
| Frequency | Is it controlled or excessive? | fluency quality |
| Replacement | Can stance marker replace it? | academic tone |
| Impact | Does it aid meaning or add noise? | clarity |

## Core Theory
> [!info] Rule
> Interjections express immediate emotion (oh, wow, ah), but they are mainly register markers.

## GRA Focus (Band 8/8.5)
- limited natural use in speaking
- avoid in formal writing
- replace fillers with discourse markers

## Must Memorize Register Table
| Situation | Avoid | Better Option |
|---|---|---|
| Formal writing | wow, oh no, uh | notably, however, importantly |
| Speaking filler habit | um, uh, like | well, generally speaking |
| Emotional reaction in argument | oh no this is bad | this creates a serious concern |

## IELTS Relevance
- Speaking Part 1/2: one light interjection can sound natural.
- Speaking Part 3: prefer structured stance markers.
- Writing Task 1/2: avoid interjections.

## Common Errors
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Overfilling speech with fillers weakens fluency impression.
>
> Replace repeated fillers with planned pause + structured phrase.

## Task Application
- Keep interjections minimal and controlled.
- Use formal alternatives in writing.
- Prioritize clarity over emotional expression.
