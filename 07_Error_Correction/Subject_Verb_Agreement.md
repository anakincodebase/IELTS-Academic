# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Subject Verb Agreement</span>

## <span style="color: #00838f">Core Rule</span>
> [!info] Rule
> The verb must agree with the grammatical subject, not with the nearest noun.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Singular and Plural Basics</span>
- Singular subject -> singular verb.
- Plural subject -> plural verb.

Examples:
- The student studies every evening.
- The students study every evening.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">High-Risk IELTS Cases</span>

### <span style="color: #d4af37">A. Intervening Phrase Trap</span>
- The quality of these reports is improving.
- The effects of poor planning are severe.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">B. Compound Subject with and</span>
- Pollution and traffic are major concerns.
- Bread and butter is my usual breakfast. (single combined idea)

### <span style="color: #d4af37">C. Compound Subject with or/nor</span>
- Either the teacher or the students are presenting.
- Either the students or the teacher is presenting.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">D. Indefinite Pronouns (Usually Singular)</span>
- everyone, everybody, someone, anybody, nobody, each, either, neither

Examples:
- Everyone has access to the guideline.
- Each of the proposals was reviewed.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">E. Quantifiers and Fractions</span>
- A number of students are absent.
- The number of students is increasing.
- Fifty percent of the class is present.
- Fifty percent of the students are present.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">F. Collective Nouns (Meaning Controls Agreement)</span>
- The team is ready. (team as one unit)
- The team are arguing among themselves. (members acting individually)

### <span style="color: #d4af37">G. Uncountable Nouns</span>
- Information is essential.
- Research shows a clear trend.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">H. Titles, Distances, Amounts, and Time as One Unit</span>
- Ten kilometers is too far to walk daily.
- Five years is a long time in policy planning.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">IELTS Error Patterns</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: People is becoming more dependent on mobile devices.
>
> Correct: People are becoming more dependent on mobile devices.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: Each students have a unique learning style.
>
> Correct: Each student has a unique learning style.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: The number of private cars are rising.
>
> Correct: The number of private cars is rising.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">60-Second SVA Check Before Submission</span>
1. Underline every finite verb.
2. Identify the true subject in each clause.
3. Ignore prepositional phrases between subject and verb.
4. Re-check quantifier and percentage structures.
5. Re-check indefinite pronouns.
