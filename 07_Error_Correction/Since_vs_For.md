# Since vs For

## Core Difference
> [!info] Rule
> Use since for a starting point in time.
> Use for for a duration (length of time).

## Since = Starting Point
Common patterns:
- since 2019
- since Monday
- since childhood
- since I moved here

Examples:
- I have lived here since 2020.
- She has studied English since high school.

## For = Duration
Common patterns:
- for two years
- for a week
- for a long time
- for three months

Examples:
- I have lived here for six years.
- They have worked on this project for months.

## Tense Pairing
Most common with present perfect:
- have/has + V3 + since/for

> [!example] IELTS Example
> I have used public buses for ten years, but service quality has improved only since last year.

## High-Frequency Errors
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: I have been here since three years.
>
> Correct: I have been here for three years.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: She has worked in this office for 2021.
>
> Correct: She has worked in this office since 2021.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: We know each other since five months.
>
> Correct: We have known each other for five months.

## IELTS Application
- Speaking Part 1: since and for are essential for hometown, study, hobby, and routine questions.
- Writing Task 1: use since to mark trend origin points.
- Writing Task 2: use present perfect + since/for to describe long-term social changes.

## Quick Check Formula
- If the phrase answers when did it start? -> since
- If the phrase answers how long? -> for

## Mini Drill
1. I have waited ______ 8 a.m.
2. They have lived here ______ ten years.
3. The trend has continued ______ 2015.

Answers:
1. since
2. for
3. since
