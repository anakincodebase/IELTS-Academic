# Myself Misuse in Introductions

## Core Idea
> [!info] Rule
> myself is a reflexive pronoun. It should not replace I or me in introductions.

Correct reflexive use:
- I taught myself French.
- I prepared myself for the exam.

Incorrect identity introduction:
- Myself Anakin.

Correct identity introduction:
- I am Anakin.
- My name is Anakin.

## Why This Error Hurts IELTS
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Using myself as a subject in introductions sounds unnatural and can reduce perceived grammar control in Speaking.

## Correct vs Incorrect Patterns
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: Myself am a student from Dhaka.
>
> Correct: I am a student from Dhaka.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: Myself believe this policy is helpful.
>
> Correct: I believe this policy is helpful.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: Please contact myself for details.
>
> Correct: Please contact me for details.

## When Myself Is Correct
Use myself only when subject and object refer to the same person:
- I introduced myself to the new teacher.
- I reminded myself to revise grammar daily.

Use myself for emphasis after I/me (less frequent but valid):
- I myself checked every sentence.

## IELTS Application
- Speaking Part 1: start with I am..., I live..., I work..., I study...
- Speaking Part 2/3: use I for opinions and experiences, me for objects
- Writing Task 2: avoid overusing first person, but if used, keep forms correct

## Pronoun Role Snapshot
- Subject: I
- Object: me
- Possessive adjective: my
- Possessive pronoun: mine
- Reflexive: myself

## Mini Drill
Fix the sentence:
1. Myself prefer online classes.
2. The manager asked myself to wait.
3. I looked at myself in the mirror.

Answers:
1. I prefer online classes.
2. The manager asked me to wait.
3. Correct as written.
