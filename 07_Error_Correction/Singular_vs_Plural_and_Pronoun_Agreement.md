# Singular vs Plural and Pronoun Agreement

## Core Objective
> [!info] Rule
> High-band accuracy requires correct number agreement in present tense and clear pronoun-antecedent matching.

## 1) Present-Tense Agreement Map
### Singular subjects -> singular verbs
- he/she/it has
- he/she/it does
- everyone has
- each student writes
- the number is

### Plural subjects -> plural verbs
- they have
- they do
- many people are
- both options are
- a number of students are

## 2) High-Frequency Trap Words
### Grammatically singular (very common IELTS traps)
- everyone, everybody, everything
- someone, somebody, something
- anyone, anybody, anything
- no one, nobody, nothing
- each, either, neither

Correct examples:
- Everyone is welcome.
- Everyone has a phone.
- Each student is responsible.
- Neither option is perfect.

### Grammatically plural
- both, many, few, several, people, students

Correct examples:
- Both plans are expensive.
- Many students have part-time jobs.

## 3) Pronoun Agreement Rules
### Rule A: Pronoun must match number
- Incorrect: Every student must submit their forms. (traditional strict rule)
- Formal IELTS-safe: Every student must submit his or her form.
- Modern accepted English: Every student must submit their form.

> [!info] Practical IELTS Guidance
> In writing, use one style consistently. For maximum conservative safety, use singular nouns where possible: All students must submit their forms.

### Rule B: Antecedent must be clear
- Weak: When teachers talk to students, they should be respectful.
- Clear: When teachers talk to students, teachers should be respectful.
- Clear: When teachers talk to students, students should be respectful.

## 4) Present-Tense Verb Form Errors
### Is/Are
- Incorrect: The benefits of exercise is obvious.
- Correct: The benefits of exercise are obvious.

### Has/Have
- Incorrect: Everyone have equal rights.
- Correct: Everyone has equal rights.

### Does/Do
- Incorrect: She do not agree.
- Correct: She does not agree.

## 5) Special Structures That Cause Errors
### One of + plural noun + singular verb
- One of the students is absent.

### More than one + singular noun + singular verb
- More than one reason explains this trend.

### The number of + plural noun + singular verb
- The number of cars is increasing.

### A number of + plural noun + plural verb
- A number of cars are parked outside.

### Each/Every + singular noun + singular verb
- Every child needs support.
- Each participant has an ID card.

## 6) GRE-Level Precision Notes
- Prefer precise pairs: each participant has, all participants have.
- Avoid vague pronoun chains in long sentences.
- In Task 2, maintain one agreement pattern across the paragraph.

## 7) Essential Singular and Plural List (IELTS Academic)
### Regular plural patterns
- noun + s: book -> books, trend -> trends
- noun + es: class -> classes, box -> boxes
- consonant + y -> ies: city -> cities, policy -> policies
- f/fe -> ves (common cases): leaf -> leaves, life -> lives

### High-frequency irregular plurals
- child -> children
- person -> people
- man -> men
- woman -> women
- foot -> feet
- tooth -> teeth
- mouse -> mice
- goose -> geese
- criterion -> criteria
- phenomenon -> phenomena
- analysis -> analyses
- basis -> bases
- thesis -> theses
- crisis -> crises
- datum -> data
- medium -> media

### Same form in singular and plural
- sheep
- deer
- species
- series
- aircraft

### Nouns usually used in plural form
- scissors
- trousers
- jeans
- goods
- outskirts
- premises

### Essential uncountable nouns (do not pluralize)
- information
- advice
- research
- equipment
- evidence
- furniture
- luggage
- traffic
- homework
- knowledge
- progress
- news

Accuracy examples:
- Incorrect: Many informations were collected.
- Correct: Much information was collected.
- Incorrect: The researches are reliable.
- Correct: The research is reliable.

## 8) Essential Pronoun Gender List (Masculine, Feminine, Neutral)
### Core pronoun sets
- Masculine singular: he, him, his
- Feminine singular: she, her, hers
- Neutral singular/object: it, its
- Neutral singular/plural human: they, them, their, theirs
- Formal generic: one, one's

### IELTS Academic preference
- Prefer gender-neutral references where gender is unknown or irrelevant.
- Keep one system consistently across the essay.

Examples:
- When a student is stressed, they may perform poorly.
- When students are stressed, they may perform poorly.

## 9) Essential Masculine/Feminine Word Accuracy for Academic Style
### Common gendered words and neutral upgrades
- chairman -> chair / chairperson
- spokesman -> spokesperson
- businessman / businesswoman -> businessperson / entrepreneur
- manpower -> workforce
- mankind -> humankind / humanity
- fireman -> firefighter
- policeman / policewoman -> police officer
- stewardess -> flight attendant

### Masculine/feminine noun pairs (recognition list)
- actor / actress
- waiter / waitress
- host / hostess
- prince / princess
- duke / duchess
- hero / heroine

> [!info] IELTS Writing Rule
> In Task 2, neutral professional labels are usually safer and more academic than gender-marked forms.

## 10) Quick Accuracy Drill
1. Everyone ___ (has/have) a role.
2. Both proposals ___ (is/are) feasible.
3. A number of workers ___ (is/are) absent.
4. The number of workers ___ (is/are) rising.
5. Neither side ___ (agree/agrees) fully.

Answer key:
1. has
2. are
3. are
4. is
5. agrees
