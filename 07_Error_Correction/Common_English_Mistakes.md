# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Common English Mistakes</span>

Small grammar errors add up. A Band 7 essay filled with two dozen tiny errors might drop to Band 5.5 for Grammatical Range and Accuracy. The ideas stay strong, but the execution fails.

This note focuses on the mistakes that appear most frequently in student writing—the ones that repeat across different test takers and actually lower scores.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Subject-Verb Agreement</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: The number of students are increasing.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: The number of students is increasing.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Everyone have a smartphone.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Everyone has a smartphone.

Quick rule:
- He/She/It + verb-s in present simple
- Indefinite pronouns like everyone, nobody, each are singular
- Use singular verb forms with everyone/each/neither: is, has, does

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Countable vs Uncountable Nouns</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Many researches show this trend.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Much research shows this trend.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: The government provided many advices.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: The government provided much advice.

Common uncountables:
- information, advice, research, equipment, evidence, infrastructure

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Article Errors</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: She is engineer.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: She is an engineer.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: The life is difficult in modern cities. (general meaning)
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Life is difficult in modern cities.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Preposition Errors</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Discuss about the issue.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Discuss the issue.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Married with a doctor.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Married to a doctor.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Depend of technology.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Depend on technology.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Sentence Structure Errors</span>
### <span style="color: #d4af37">Comma Splice</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Traffic is getting worse, the government must act.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Traffic is getting worse, so the government must act.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Traffic is getting worse; therefore, the government must act.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Fragment</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Because public transport is expensive.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Because public transport is expensive, many people drive.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Word Form Errors</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: This policy is very benefit.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: This policy is very beneficial.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: She explained the problem very clear.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: She explained the problem very clearly.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Tense Consistency Errors</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty

>
> ✅ RIGHT: Last year, the number rose sharply.

> [!warning] Band Penalty

> ✅ RIGHT: I visited Paris in 2019.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Ten High-Frequency Word and Form Mistakes</span>

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Avoiding Short Forms (Formal Writing)</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG (Task 2): Governments shouldn't ignore this issue.
>
> ✅ RIGHT (formal): Governments should not ignore this issue.

Rule:
- In IELTS Writing, prefer full forms (do not, cannot, should not) over contractions.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Self-Introductions (Myself vs I)</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Myself Anakin.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: I am Anakin.

### <span style="color: #00838f">Blunder vs Mistake</span>
Rule:
- mistake = normal error (neutral)
- blunder = serious or careless error (strong negative)

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WEAK/UNNATURAL: I made a blunder in spelling.
>
> ✅ BETTER: I made a mistake in spelling.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">It's vs Its</span>
Rule:
- it's = it is / it has
- its = possessive form

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: The company increased it's revenue.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: The company increased its revenue.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Gone vs Went</span>
Rule:
- went = past simple (finished action)
- gone = past participle, usually with has/have/had

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: He has went to Canada.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: He has gone to Canada.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Yesterday, she has gone to the library.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Yesterday, she went to the library.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Since vs For</span>
Rule:
- since = starting point
- for = duration

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: I have lived here since five years.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: I have lived here for five years.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Accept vs Except</span>
Rule:
- accept = receive/agree
- except = excluding

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Everyone accept Rafi passed the exam.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: Everyone except Rafi passed the exam.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Do vs Does</span>
Rule:
- do = I/you/we/they
- does = he/she/it

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: She do not like online classes.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: She does not like online classes.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Percent vs Percentage</span>
Rule:
- percent is used with a number: 25 percent
- percentage is used without an exact number: a high percentage

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: The percentage of students was 25 percentage.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: The proportion of students was 25 percent.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">There vs Their</span>
Rule:
- there = place/existence
- their = possessive adjective

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> ❌ WRONG: Their are many reasons for this trend.
>
> ✅ RIGHT: There are many reasons for this trend.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">IELTS Application</span>
- Writing Task 1: check tense against chart time references before finalizing.
- Writing Task 2: scan for countable/uncountable and article errors in each paragraph.
- Speaking: prioritize clear and correct forms over risky, memorized complex sentences.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">Fast Proofreading Checklist</span>
- Subject and verb agree?
- Noun countability correct?
- Correct preposition or no preposition?
- Any sentence fragments or comma splices?
- Tense matches time marker?
