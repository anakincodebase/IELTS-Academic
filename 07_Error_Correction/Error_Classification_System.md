## CATEGORY 1: Subject-Verb Agreement

**What It Is**: Verb form doesn't match subject (singular/plural, person)

### Band 6 Examples (Critical Errors)

```
❌ "The student are studying hard."
   (plural verb with singular subject)

❌ "Everyone participate in the discussion."
   (no -s on verb; "everyone" = singular)

❌ "The government have implemented new policies."
   (British: "government" can be collective, but inconsistency is Band 6)
```

### Band 7 Mistakes (Still blocking)

```
❌ "Neither option are acceptable." 
   (neither = singular; need "is")

❌ "Economics are a difficult subject."
   (Academic subject; treated singular: "Economics is")

❌ "Data shows that corruption increase annually."
   (Data is plural in English; correct: "Data show" or "Dataset shows")
```

### Band 8: Mastery Pattern

```
✅ "The government has implemented..." (consistent)

✅ "Neither option is acceptable..." (correct singular)

✅ "These data indicate..." (respects collective nouns)

✅ "A series of events has shaped..." (series = singular; has)
```

### Self-Diagnosis Test

Do these correctly to verify mastery:
- [ ] "Each student _____ (has/have) completed the assignment."
- [ ] "The team _____ (works/work) effectively when..."
- [ ] "Statistics _____ (shows/show) that..."
- [ ] "Neither approach _____ (is/are) viable."

## CATEGORY 2: Tense Consistency

**What It Is**: Incorrect or inconsistent verb tense for rhetorical context

### Band 6 Examples

```
❌ "Climate change is a serious problem. The government implemented new 
    policies last year, and it will change society."
(Tense jumping: present → past → future without cause)

❌ "Education shapes future opportunities. It also encouraged critical thinking."
(Present → past without reason; inconsistent)
```

### Band 7 Mistakes

```
❌ "Research has shown that exercise improves health. However, many 
    people did not prioritize fitness."
(Present Perfect shift to Simple Past; should maintain consistency unless time shift)

❌ "The study indicates that consumers prefer sustainable products. They 
    also valued affordability."
(Present → Past incorrectly)
```

### Band 8: Mastery Pattern

```
✅ For historical examples: "When countries implemented education policies 
   (Simple Past), they subsequently achieved higher living standards 
   (Present Perfect showing result)."

✅ For general claims: "Education shapes career opportunities (Present Simple). 
   Research has demonstrated this pattern (Present Perfect for findings)."

✅ Tense shift with time anchors: "In 2015 (Simple Past), the trend 
   was clear. Currently (Present Simple), the pattern persists."
```

### Self-Diagnosis

Correct these:
- [ ] "Schools provide education. They also _____ (prepared/prepare) students for employment."
- [ ] "The policy was implemented last year. It _____ (has improved/improved) outcomes."
- [ ] "Research _____ (shows/showed) that technology _____ (changes/changed) society."

## CATEGORY 3: Article Errors (a/an/the)

**What It Is**: Incorrect or missing articles; most persistent error for Asian speakers

### Band 6 Examples

```
❌ "Education is important for the society."
(the society = specific; should be "society" generally or "a society")

❌ "A research shows that..."
(Research is uncountable; can't use "a")

❌ "The technology has transformed world."
(world needs "the" for specific global reference)

❌ "He attended university and worked at company."
(Generic singular institutions: "a" is often correct)
```

### Band 7 Mistakes

```
❌ "The data indicate the trend has continued."
(No article needed for "data" in this context; should be "Data indicate...")

❌ "An environmental protection is necessary."
("environmental protection" = uncountable; should be "Environmental protection...")

❌ "The most of people prefer..."
(Incorrect construction; should be "Most people..." no article)
```

### Band 8: Mastery Pattern

```
✅ Countable singular: "a student", "the outcome"
✅ Countable plural: "students", "the outcomes" (if specified)
✅ Uncountable: "education", "research", "pollution" (no article usually)
✅ Generic classes: "Education shapes society." (no the)
✅ Specific mentions: "The education provided by schools is..." (the for specific)

✅ Complex: "The implementation of a comprehensive policy demonstrates 
   the effectiveness of coordinated systems."
```

### Self-Diagnosis

Get these right:
- [ ] "_____ education is important. _____ education provided by schools has..."
- [ ] "_____ research shows that _____ technology transforms _____ society."
- [ ] "_____ majority of people prefer _____ sustainable products."

## CATEGORY 4: Word Form Errors

**What It Is**: Wrong part of speech (noun/verb/adjective/adverb)

### Band 6 Examples

```
❌ "Technology has developed rapidly in recent years and has many 
    development in new sectors."
(Should be: "developments" = noun, or "has developed significantly")

❌ "The environment is significant for human survival."
(Correct, but Band 6 often says: "Environmental is important" = wrong form)

❌ "Education can improve person's ability successfully."
(Should be: "person's abilities" = noun; and "successfully" = adverb)
```

### Band 7 Mistakes

```
❌ "The implement of new policies is necessary."
(Should be: "The implementation" or "Implementing")

❌ "Different approach has different effective."
("effective" = adjective; should be "effectiveness" = noun)

❌ "To productivity increase, organizations require..."
(Should be: "To increase productivity..." or "Productivity increases...")
```

### Band 8: Mastery Pattern

```
✅ Nominalizations: improve→improvement, develop→development, investigate→investigation
✅ Verb forms: "Implementing new policies..." (gerund) vs "Policies implemented..." (participle)
✅ Adverb construction: "Significantly, the trend..." (adverb) vs "Significant findings..." (adjective)
✅ Precision: "The effectiveness of..." (correct noun form for abstract concept)

✅ Complex: "The implementation of rigorous methodologies enables systematic 
   evaluation of intended outcomes."
```

### Self-Diagnosis

Correct these:
- [ ] "Research develop new _____ (technology/technologies)."
- [ ] "The_____ (effective/effectiveness) of policies is being_____ (evaluate/evaluated)."
- [ ] "_____ (Rapid/Rapidly) advancement in AI has _____ (transformation/transformed) industries."

## CATEGORY 5: Preposition Errors

**What It Is**: Wrong or missing preposition after nouns/verbs/adjectives

### Band 6 Examples

```
❌ "Education is important for the society."
(Usually no preposition: "important to society" or "for society" depending on context)

❌ "I'm interested about this topic."
(Should be: "interested in")

❌ "The increase of crime is concerning."
(Acceptable, but "in crime" more natural)

❌ "Focus at these problems."
(Should be: "Focus on" or "Focus on these")
```

### Band 7 Mistakes

```
❌ "The correlation between development and education is significant."
(Correct! But often Band 7 says: "correlation of" = wrong)

❌ "The method based by research..."
(Should be: "based on research")

❌ "Different from traditional approaches, new methods..."
(Correct, but Band 7 sometimes says: "different to")
```

### Band 8: Mastery Pattern

```
✅ Preposition patterns for common collocations:
   - interested IN
   - focus ON
   - explore the relationship BETWEEN/AMONG
   - differ FROM
   - benefit FROM
   - contribute TO

✅ Academic preciseness: "pertaining to", "with respect to", "in light of"

✅ Complex: "The correlation between education and income, in light of 
   recent studies, demonstrates systematic relationships."
```

## CATEGORY 6: Sentence Fragments

**What It Is**: Incomplete sentence (dependent clause standing alone; no main verb)

### Band 6 Examples

```
❌ "Education being important to society."
(No main verb; needs: "Education, being important, is...")

❌ "Because students require skills for employment."
(Dependent clause only; needs independent clause)

❌ "The reason that education matters."
(Needs predicate: "The reason education matters is that..." or "The reason why...")
```

### Band 7 Mistakes

```
❌ "Although research indicates positive outcomes. Many people remain skeptical."
(First part is dependent fragment; needs to attach to main clause)

❌ "With the implementation of new policies. The results improved."
(Introductory phrase shouldn't stand alone.)
```

### Band 8: Mastery Pattern

```
✅ Fragments used INTENTIONALLY for rhetorical effect:
   "Education shapes futures. Not just academically. Professionally. Personally."
   (Used deliberately for emphasis)

✅ Correct dependent clause attachment:
   "Although research indicates positive outcomes, many people remain skeptical."

✅ Complex sentences with multiple clauses all connected to main verb:
   "When policies were implemented, outcomes improved because systems were 
   coordinated with...strategies that reflected..."
```

## CATEGORY 7: Run-on Sentences & Comma Splices

**What It Is**: Two independent clauses incorrectly joined (not enough punctuation)

### Band 6 Examples

```
❌ "Education is important, it shapes society."
(Comma splice: two independent clauses joined with comma)

❌ "Climate change threatens ecosystems it also affects human health."
(Run-on: no punctuation between clauses)

❌ "Technology has developed in the past decade, computers are now essential."
(Comma splice: should be period, semicolon, or conjunction)
```

### Band 7 Mistakes

```
❌ "Research indicates significant outcomes; however, implementation remains problematic."
(Actually correct! But Band 7 sometimes incorrectly:)
"Research indicates outcomes, however implementation remains..."  (missing semicolon)

❌ "The policy was effective. However, it was expensive it required..."
(Fragment after "However" + run-on)
```

### Band 8: Mastery Pattern

```
✅ Semicolon with transitional adverbs:
   "Implementation was challenging; however, outcomes justified the effort."

✅ Independent clauses properly joined:
   "Education shapes futures, and societies benefit economically." (coordinating conjunction)

✅ Dependent clauses correctly attached:
   "Because implementation was coordinated carefully, the transition succeeded."

✅ Complex compound-complex:
   "Although obstacles emerged initially, stakeholders persisted because the 
   vision was clear, and momentum built systematically."
```

## CATEGORY 8-12: QUICK REFERENCE

### CATEGORY 8: Pronoun Agreement

| Error | Correction | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| "Each student should complete their homework." | Each student should complete his/her homework OR All students should complete their homework. | "Each" = singular; "their" = plural mismatch |
| "Everyone has to manage their time." | Everyone has to manage his/her/one's time. | "Everyone" = singular |
| "The committee made their decision." | The committee made its decision. | Collective singular (US) |

### CATEGORY 9: Parallelism

```
❌ "Education develops critical thinking, creativity, and to communicate."
✅ "Education develops critical thinking, creativity, and communication skills."
   (All nouns; parallel structure)

❌ "The policy aims to reduce costs, improving efficiency, and that climate..."
✅ "The policy aims to reduce costs, improve efficiency, and combat climate change."
   (All infinitives; parallel structure)
```

### CATEGORY 10: Dangling Modifiers

```
❌ "Running through the park, the dog chased me."
   (Who's running: me or dog? Unclear)

✅ "Running through the park, I was chased by a dog."
   (Clear: I am running)
```

### CATEGORY 11: Double Negatives

```
❌ "I don't have no time."
✅ "I don't have any time."
   (or: "I have no time.")
```

### CATEGORY 12: Comma Splices

```
❌ "The study was thorough, the results were compelling."
✅ "The study was thorough; the results were compelling."
   (Semicolon or: The study was thorough. The results were compelling.)
```

## Self-Diagnostic Flowchart

```
[Read your sentence]
     ↓
[Is there a main verb with subject?]
  NO → FRAGMENT ERROR
  YES ↓
[Are there 2+ independent clauses?]
  NO → [Is article usage correct?] → Article errors
  YES ↓
[Are they properly connected? (period/semicolon/conjunction)]
  NO → COMMA SPLICE / RUN-ON
  YES ↓
[Do subject and verb agree in number?]
  NO → SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT
  YES ↓
[Are all words in correct part of speech?]
  NO → WORD FORM ERROR
  YES ↓
[Are prepositions correct after words?]
  NO → PREPOSITION ERROR
  YES ↓
[Is tense consistent with context?]
  NO → TENSE INCONSISTENCY
  YES ↓
                    ✅ LIKELY CLEAN
```
