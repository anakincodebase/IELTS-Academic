# <span style="color: #1a3a52">Cohesion Strategies</span>

Cohesion is the glue that holds sentences together. It's how you link ideas so readers follow your argument without having to re-read. In IELTS, examiner comments like "ideas are disconnected" usually mean weak cohesion.

Cohesion works through four tools: reference (pronouns and demonstratives), lexical chains (repetition with synonyms), substitution (avoiding repetition), and connectors (linking words).

## <span style="color: #00838f">Cohesion vs Coherence</span>

- Cohesion = how sentences are linked.
- Coherence = whether the idea sequence makes logical sense.

## <span style="color: #00838f">Four Cohesion Systems You Must Control</span>

### <span style="color: #00838f">A) Reference cohesion</span>

Use pronouns and demonstratives with clear antecedents.

- this issue, this trend, these measures, such policies

Weak:

- The government changed policy. This improved it.

Strong:

- The government changed transport policy. This reform improved bus reliability.

### <span style="color: #00838f">B) Lexical cohesion</span>

Build one controlled lexical chain per paragraph.

Band 8 chain example:

- pollution -> air pollution -> emissions -> particulate matter -> respiratory risk

### <span style="color: #00838f">C) Substitution and ellipsis</span>

Avoid unnecessary repetition without losing clarity.

Examples:

- The first proposal is cheaper than the second one.
- Some cities subsidize buses, while others do not.

### <span style="color: #00838f">D) Conjunctive cohesion</span>

Use connectors only where logic needs signaling.

- however, therefore, meanwhile, by contrast, consequently

## <span style="color: #d4af37">High-Band Cohesion Rules</span>

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Rule 1: Anchor Noun Rule</span>

When using it, they, this, or these, keep a clear noun anchor in the previous sentence.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Rule 2: One Strong Connector Rule</span>

One high-value connector per sentence is usually enough.

Avoid stacking: moreover, furthermore, in addition, also.

### <span style="color: #d4af37">Rule 3: Three-Link Chain Rule</span>

In one paragraph, track one core noun through at least three related references.

Example:

- online learning -> this model -> such platforms

### <span style="color: #1b5e20">Rule 4: Given-to-New Flow</span>

Start from known information, then move to new information.

This reduces abrupt jumps and improves readability.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">IELTS Task Application</span>

### <span style="color: #5e35b1">Writing Task 1</span>

- Use that and those for precise comparison.
- Use respectively and by contrast for clean data linking.

Example:

- The figure for rail was higher than that for bus travel.

### <span style="color: #5e35b1">Writing Task 2</span>

Use demonstrative + abstract noun bundles:

- this issue, this approach, this pattern, this imbalance

### <span style="color: #5e35b1">Speaking Part 3</span>

Use lighter natural bridges:

- generally, at the same time, on the other hand, in practice

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Weak vs Strong Cohesion Examples</span>

### Example 1

Weak:

- Social media is popular. It is bad. It affects people. It causes problems.

Strong:

- Social media is now embedded in daily communication. This dependence can be harmful when users rely on constant validation. As a result, many teenagers report lower confidence and attention control.

### Example 2

Weak:

- Public transport is useful. Moreover, people use it. Furthermore, it is cheap.

Strong:

- Public transport is useful because it reduces private-car dependency. In addition, it is often more affordable for low-income commuters.

## <span style="color: #d4af37">High-Frequency Cohesion Errors</span>

- Warning: unclear pronoun reference (it or they without a clear noun source).
- Warning: synonym forcing that changes meaning.
- Warning: connector overload that sounds memorized.
- Warning: sudden topic shift with no bridge sentence.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">10-Minute Cohesion Drill</span>

1. Write one 90-120 word paragraph on a Task 2 topic.
2. Underline every connector.
3. Circle every pronoun and mark its noun anchor.
4. Check lexical-chain consistency.
5. Rewrite once with fewer but stronger links.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">20-Second Cohesion Scan</span>

- Are references explicit and unambiguous?
- Do connectors reflect real logic, not decoration?
- Is one lexical chain maintained across the paragraph?
- Does any sentence feel detached from the chain?
