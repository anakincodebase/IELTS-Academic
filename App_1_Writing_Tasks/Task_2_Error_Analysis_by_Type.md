# Task 2: Error Analysis by Type (Band 6→7→8 Progression)

## Task 2 Structure (Band 8 Template)

```
Paragraph 1 (Introduction): 40-50 words
- Paraphrase the question
- State your thesis/position clearly
- No examples yet

Paragraph 2-3 (Body): 80-100 words each
- Topic sentence with clear claim
- Explanation (2-3 sentences)
- Real example(s)
- Link back to thesis

Paragraph 4 (Conclusion): 40-50 words
- Restate thesis in new words
- Summarize main arguments
- Final thought on relevance

Total: 250-280 words (not 400+)
```

---

## THE 7 TASK 2 ERROR CATEGORIES

### ERROR TYPE 1: UNCLEAR THESIS (Band 6 Indicator)

**What This Looks Like:**
```
Band 6: "There are many opinions on whether technology is helpful for education. 
Some people think it is good, others think it is bad. I will discuss both sides."
(Doesn't answer the actual question; just lists opinions)

Band 8: "While technology offers substantial educational benefits through personalized 
learning and global resource access, its implementation requires careful pedagogical 
integration to prevent over-reliance and maintain critical thinking skills. On balance, 
technology's advantages outweigh its limitations in contemporary educational contexts."
(Directly answers: position + key reasoning)
```

**How to Fix:**
```
BAND 6 mistake: Avoiding taking a clear stance
BAND 8 approach: 
- If asked opinion → state clear opinion in introduction
- If asked discuss both → acknowledge both, then clarify which you believe more
- If asked solution → present your solution early
- Never write "I will discuss both" (just do it naturally)
```

### ERROR TYPE 2: TOPIC ABANDONMENT (Band 6 Indicator)

**What This Looks Like:**
```
Question: "Some people think modern education should focus on practical skills 
rather than theoretical knowledge. Discuss both sides."

Band 6: Starts discussing practical vs. theoretical, but by paragraph 3 starts 
talking about: student motivation, how expensive education is, why homework exists...

Band 8: Stays completely within scope:
- Paragraph 2: Benefits of practical skills focus
- Paragraph 3: Benefits of theoretical knowledge
- Paragraph 4: Clear position and relevance
(Every sentence connects back to the core question)
```

**How to Fix:**
```
Before writing:
1. Underline the EXACT topic in the question
2. Write 1-sentence answer to each body paragraph
3. During writing: Check EVERY sentence = relevant to question?
```

### ERROR TYPE 3: INSUFFICIENT EXPLANATION (Band 7 Plateau)

**What This Looks Like:**
```
Band 7: "Technology helps education because it provides better resources. 
Students can find information online. The Internet is useful."

Band 8: "Technology facilitates personalized learning pathways; students can access 
specialized educational content tailored to individual learning speeds and preferences. 
Furthermore, digital platforms enable real-time feedback mechanisms unavailable in 
traditional classroom settings, allowing educators to identify and address comprehension 
gaps immediately. This responsiveness accelerates knowledge consolidation compared to 
conventional annual assessment cycles."
```

**Why This Matters:**
- Band 7: WHAT the benefit is (what +s)
- Band 8: HOW it works + WHY it matters + WHAT the consequence is

**How to Fix:**
```
For each claim, answer:
- WHAT is the argument? (what happens)
- HOW/WHY does it work? (the mechanism)
- WHAT'S THE IMPACT? (consequence or significance)

Weak: "Using technology improves learning."
Better: "Using technology improves learning BY enabling personalized pacing. 
This matters BECAUSE students learn at different speeds, and individualized 
instruction allows faster learners to advance while giving struggling students 
additional consolidation time, RESULTING IN deeper mastery."
```

### ERROR TYPE 4: WEAK EXAMPLES (Band 6→7 Blocker)

**Band 6 Examples (Generic):**
```
"For example, students can use computers to do homework."
"India is a big country where many people use technology."
"In my opinion, my friend told me that he likes online classes."
```

**Band 8 Examples (Specific):**
```
"Learning management systems like Moodle enable submission of assignments, 
automatic plagiarism detection, and algorithmic distribution of grading tasks, 
reducing instructor workload by approximately 40% according to university surveys."

"Countries like Singapore and South Korea have integrated AI-driven adaptive 
learning platforms into national curricula, demonstrating 15-20% improvement in 
standardized assessment scores compared to control classrooms."
```

**How to Fix:**
```
Weak: "People use technology in education."
Better: "Machine learning algorithms personalize content delivery, with 
research from Stanford's Learning Analytics Lab indicating that students 
using adaptive platforms demonstrate 30% faster skill acquisition."

(Specific + Demonstrated impact + Research basis)
```

### ERROR TYPE 5: GRAMMATICAL ERRORS (Band 6 Ceiling)

**Common Task 2 Grammar Issues:**

| Error | Band 6 | Band 8 |
|---|---|---|
| Subject-Verb | "Technology and education is important." | "Technology and education are important." |
| Articles | "Education is essential for developing the skills." | "Education is essential for developing skills." |
| Word Form | "The improvement of education's quality..." | "Educational improvement..." (nominalization) |
| Tense | "Technology was changing how students learn." | "Technology is changing..." (present ongoing) |
| Passive | "It is believed by many that..." | "Many believe that..." (agency) |
| Prepositions | "Discussion about technology" | "Discussion of technology" |
| Fragments | "Which provides many benefits." | Connect to main clause |
| Run-ons | "Education develops skills students need them." | "Education develops skills students need." |

**Strategy:**
```
After writing, check EVERY sentence for:
1. Subject + verb agreement? 
2. Correct articles (a/an/the)?
3. Correct word form (noun/verb/adj/adv)?
4. Right preposition for that verb?
5. Proper tense?
(Use [[Error_Classification_System]] for reference)
```

### ERROR TYPE 6: REPETITIVE VOCABULARY (Band 6→7 Blocker)

**Band 6 Approach (Repetition):**
```
"Technology is important for education. Technology helps students learn. 
Technology provides many things. Technology is used by modern students."

Synonym count: "technology" appears 4 times in 4 sentences
Result: Sounds immature; Band ceiling = 6.5
```

**Band 8 Approach (Variation):**
```
"Digital platforms fundamentally reconfigure pedagogical delivery. These technologies 
facilitate personalized learning pathways. Computational systems enable real-time 
assessment mechanisms. Such innovations cultivate critical competencies essential 
for contemporary employment contexts."

Vocabulary: platforms, technologies, systems, innovations
+ Synonyms: pedagogical delivery, learning pathways, real-time assessment, critical competencies
Result: Sophisticated vocabulary; Band floor = 7.5
```

**How to Fix:**
1. Write first draft (allow repetition)
2. Identify repeated words (mark every instance)
3. Replace: Use thesaurus + synonyms that fit the academic register
4. Check: Did replacement maintain meaning?

### ERROR TYPE 7: WEAK CONCLUSION (Band 6 Indicator)

**Band 6 Conclusions (Weak):**
```
"In conclusion, I think technology is important for education. Technology helps 
students, and education is necessary. My opinion is that technology should be used 
more in schools."

Issues: Repetition, vague, no synthesis, personal pronoun, doesn't solidify argument
```

**Band 8 Conclusions (Strong):**
```
"While legitimate concerns persist regarding technology's potential to undermine 
face-to-face interaction and critical thinking development, the pedagogical 
advantages—particularly personalized learning pathways and immediate feedback 
mechanisms—substantially outweigh these limitations. Future educational institutions 
will likely prioritize technologically enhanced learning as foundational infrastructure, 
provided implementation maintains pedagogical intentionality and human-centered 
principles."

Elements:
- Acknowledges complexity (not simplistic)
- Reinstates thesis in new language
- Summarizes key supporting arguments
- Provides forward-looking perspective
- Maintains academic tone
```

## BAND 6→7→8 PROGRESSION: SAMPLE ESSAY

### QUESTION: "Some people believe technology has been detrimental to human relationships. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

### BAND 6 RESPONSE (220 words)

```
"I disagree with this opinion. Technology has been very important for people to 
communicate with each other. Many people use social media, email and phone calls 
to contact friends and family. Technology is good for relationships.

First, technology helps people stay in contact with friends and family even if 
they live far away. For example, my friend moved to another city and we use 
Whatsapp to talk every day. Without technology, we would not be able to communicate 
so easily. So technology helps relationships.

Second, people can meet new friends on the internet. Facebook and Twitter allow 
people to connect and discuss topics. This is good for making relationships. Also, 
people can share their life with others on social media. For instance, I post photos 
on Instagram and people comment on them.

In conclusion, I believe technology is good for relationships. It helps people 
communicate, make new friends, and stay in contact. In the future, technology will 
be even more important for relationships."

BAND 6 Issues:
- Repetition: "technology", "good", "people", "relationships"
- Weak examples: "share their life" (too general)
- Personal anecdotes: "my friend", "I post" (less academic)
- Simple sentences: No complex clauses
- Thin explanation: "helps relationships" stated but not explained how/why
- Missing counterargument acknowledgment
- Run-on concluding thoughts
```

### BAND 7 RESPONSE (280 words)

```
"I substantially disagree with the assertion that technology has been detrimental 
to human relationships. While legitimate concerns regarding reduced face-to-face 
interaction merit consideration, technological innovations have demonstrably enabled 
relationship formation and maintenance previously impossible.

Technology has fundamentally expanded relational connectivity across geographic and 
temporal barriers. Digital communication platforms enable individuals to maintain 
meaningful connections despite physical separation. For instance, researchers at 
Stanford University documented that individuals utilizing video-conferencing systems 
experienced relationship satisfaction ratings comparable to in-person interactions, 
suggesting that technological mediation does not inherently diminish relational quality. 
Furthermore, digital platforms facilitate relationship initiation among individuals 
with shared interests, enabling community formation around specialized topics that 
geographic proximity alone could not facilitate.

However, excessive technology consumption may correlate with reduced face-to-face 
interaction quality. Certain studies indicate that heavy social media usage demonstrates 
weak correlations with reported loneliness metrics. Addressing this requires intentional 
communication practices rather than rejecting technology itself.

Overall, technology represents a neutral tool whose relational impact depends upon 
implementation practices. Rather than inherently damaging relationships, digital 
platforms have expanded human capacity for connection, particularly benefiting 
geographically dispersed individuals and facilitating community formation around shared 
identities. Future social development will likely require integrating technological 
connectivity with sustained face-to-face interaction investment."

BAND 7 Strengths:
- Academic language: "demonstrably enabled", "merit consideration"
- Research reference: Provides specific source attribution
- Concession: "While legitimate concerns..."
- Varied examples: Research + theoretical reasoning
- Complex sentence structures

BAND 7 Remaining Gaps:
- Could provide more specific research data
- Could strengthen explanation mechanisms
- Could develop counterargument more thoroughly
```

### BAND 8 RESPONSE (280 words)

```
"I substantially disagree with the proposition that technology has been detrimental 
to human relationships. Rather, technological innovations have fundamentally reconfigured 
relational dynamics, expanding human capacity for connection while simultaneously 
introducing new challenges requiring intentional navigation.

Technology has transcended geographic and temporal constraints that historically limited 
relationship formation and maintenance. Digital communication platforms enable individuals 
to cultivate meaningful connections despite physical separation. Research by Sprecher et 
al. (2008) indicates that individuals utilizing synchronous video-conferencing systems 
experienced relationship satisfaction ratings comparable to in-person interactions, 
suggesting that technological mediation does not inherently compromise relational 
authenticity. Furthermore, asynchronous digital platforms facilitate relationship 
initiation among individuals with specialized interests, enabling community formation 
around shared identities that geographic proximity alone could not facilitate—illustrating 
technology's role in reducing social isolation among marginalized populations.

Legitimate concerns regarding excessive technology consumption warrant acknowledgment. 
Certain longitudinal studies document weak correlations between heavy social media 
consumption and self-reported relationship quality. However, this phenomenon reflects 
implementation practices rather than technological determinism; intentional communication 
boundaries mitigate these challenges without requiring technology rejection.

Rather than inherently damaging relationships, technology represents a contextually-dependent 
tool enabling unprecedented connection while requiring disciplined usage practices. Digital 
platforms have particularly benefited geographically dispersed individuals and facilitated 
community formation among previously isolated populations. Future relational flourishing 
will depend upon integrating technological connectivity with sustained face-to-face 
interaction investment, suggesting synthesis rather than dichotomy between modes."

BAND 8 Distinctions:
- Sophisticated terminology: "transcended", "asynchronous", "relational authenticity", "technological determinism"
- Academic citation with author/date: (Sprecher et al., 2008)
- Nuanced concession: Acknowledges concerns but provides mechanism explanation
- Complex sentence structures with embedded clauses
- Sustained academic register throughout
- Strategic vocabulary precision: "contextually-dependent", "synthesis rather than dichotomy"
- Word choice shows sophistication: "mitigate" (not "fix"), "marginalized populations"
```

## QUICK TASK 2 CHECKLIST

- [ ] Introduction paraphrases question + states clear position?
- [ ] Body paragraphs (2+) have topic sentence + explanation + example?
- [ ] Examples are specific (research/statistics/case studies, not generic)?
- [ ] Every sentence relates to the question?
- [ ] Vocabulary varied? (checked for repetition)
- [ ] Complex sentence structures used?
- [ ] No personal anecdotes ("In my opinion, my friend told me...")?
- [ ] Conclusion restates thesis + summarizes arguments?
- [ ] Grammar checked? Article/preposition/verb form accuracy?
- [ ] 250-280 words (not 150 or 500)?
