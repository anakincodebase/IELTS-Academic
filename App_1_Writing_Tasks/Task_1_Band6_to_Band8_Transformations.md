## TRANSFORMATION 1: Line Graph - Sales Data

**Band 6 Response (Weak):**
```
"This graph shows sales of three products from 2010 to 2020. Overall, all three products 
went up. Product A went up the most. It started at 50 and went to 150. Product B also 
went up but not as much. It went from 40 to 120. Product C went up too, from 30 to 100. 
Product A was better than the others. All products sold more at the end."
```

**Analysis - Why This Is Band 6:**
- Repetitive language: "went up" × 5 (no synonyms)
- No specific trends/turning points identified
- No comparison of rates/trajectories
- Informal tone: "better than the others"
- Missing overview structure
- No temporal anchors (e.g., "by 2015...")
- Word count acceptable but quality weak

**Band 8 Response (Strong):**
```
"The line graph illustrates the sales performance of three products over a decade 
(2010-2020). Overall, all three products demonstrated significant growth, though at 
divergent rates, with Product A substantially outperforming its competitors.

Product A exhibited the most dramatic increase, surging from approximately 50 units 
in 2010 to 150 units by 2020, representing a threefold expansion. Growth remained 
relatively consistent throughout the period, though a notably steeper trajectory 
emerged between 2015 and 2018. Product B displayed more moderate growth, increasing 
from 40 units to 120 units, with acceleration particularly evident in the latter 
half of the decade. Conversely, Product C demonstrated the slowest expansion, rising 
from 30 units to 100 units, with a plateau apparent between 2012 and 2015 before 
resuming gradual growth.

By 2020, the gap between Product A and Product C had widened substantially to 50 units, 
indicating increasing market divergence. The data suggest that early adoption and 
sustained momentum distinguished the highest-performing product."
```

**Specific Upgrades Made:**

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 8 |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | "shows sales" | "illustrates sales performance" |
| Trend verbs | "went up" | "surged/exhibited/demonstrated" |
| Comparisons | "A was better" | "substantially outperforming" |
| Specificity | "went to 150" | "rising from...to...representing X% growth" |
| Turning points | None identified | "Between 2015-2018, notably steeper trajectory" |
| Structure | Linear list | Grouped analysis (intro, details, conclusion) |
| Precision | "all went up" | "divergent rates" |

## TRANSFORMATION 2: Bar Chart - Department Spending

**Band 6 Response (Weak):**
```
"The chart shows spending in five departments. Finance has the most money at 4.2 
million. Marketing has 2.1 million. IT has 2.7 million. HR has 1.8 million. Operations 
has 3.2 million. So Finance is the biggest. HR is the smallest. The difference between 
Finance and HR is 2.4 million. Finance got much more money than the others."
```

**Analysis - Why This Is Band 6:**
- Lists all values without analysis
- No percentages or contextual framing
- No grouping or pattern identification
- Informal: "has the most money"
- No comparison language (proportional relationships)
- Repetitive subject structures
- Missing overview/conclusion

**Band 8 Response (Strong):**
```
"The bar chart represents departmental budget allocation across five organizational 
divisions in 2024. Overall, financial resources were distributed unevenly, with Finance 
receiving a disproportionately large allocation while HR received the most limited budget.

Finance dominated the budgeting landscape, securing £4.2 million, equivalent to approximately 
32% of total expenditure. This substantially exceeded all other departments. Operations 
received the second-largest allocation at £3.2 million (24% of total), followed by IT at 
£2.7 million (21% of total). Marketing and HR received considerably more modest allocations 
at £2.1 million and £1.8 million respectively, collectively representing only 23% of the 
total budget.

The disparity between the highest and lowest-funded departments totaled £2.4 million, 
reflecting a 233% difference in allocation levels. Notably, Finance's budget exceeded 
HR's by more than double, suggesting significantly divergent organizational priorities. 
The data indicate a clear hierarchy of resource allocation, with financial management 
and operational functions receiving substantially greater investment than human resources 
and marketing."
```

**Specific Upgrades Made:**

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 8 |
|---|---|---|
| Intro | "shows spending" | "represents departmental budget allocation" |
| Percentages | Absent | "approximately 32% of total expenditure" |
| Grouping | Listed separately | "collectively representing 23%" |
| Comparisons | "much more" | "233% difference in allocation" |
| Analysis | None ("biggest/smallest") | "disproportionately large" |
| Conclusion | Absent | "Clear hierarchy of resource allocation" |
| Language | Informal | Academic/professional |

## TRANSFORMATION 3: Pie Chart - Energy Sources

**Band 6 Response (Weak):**
```
"This pie chart is about energy sources in 2024. Fossil fuels is the biggest part 
at 58%. Renewables are 28%. Nuclear is 14%. So fossil fuels is more than renewables 
and nuclear together almost. Renewables are growing. This is good because the environment. 
Fossil fuels are bad because pollution."
```

**Analysis - Why This Is Band 6:**
- Personal opinion ("good because the environment")
- Lack of mathematical relationships
- Informal language ("is more than...almost")
- No significance analysis
- Incomplete reasoning
- Missing interpretation of data meaning
- Task 1 shouldn't include critique/opinion

**Band 8 Response (Strong):**
```
"The pie chart illustrates the energy source composition globally in 2024, revealing 
a continued dependence on conventional energy sources despite the renewable transition.

Fossil fuels dominate the energy portfolio at 58%, representing more than half of global 
energy production. This commanding share reflects the persistent reliance on coal, oil, 
and natural gas in international energy systems. Renewable energy sources account for 28% 
of the total, indicating an emerging but still-limited proportion of global energy supply. 
Nuclear energy contributes a smaller yet significant portion at 14%, supporting roughly 
one-seventh of global energy production.

Notably, renewable energy combined with nuclear sources represents only 42% of total 
energy supply, highlighting the continued dominance of fossil fuels despite increasing 
environmental pressures for transition. The data suggest that while renewable adoption 
is accelerating, the existing global energy infrastructure remains overwhelmingly dependent 
on conventional sources. Achieving energy sector transformation toward sustainability 
would require substantially accelerating the transition away from fossil fuels and 
expanding renewable capacity considerably."
```

**Specific Upgrades Made:**

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 8 |
|---|---|---|
| Opening | "is about" | "illustrates...revealing" |
| Percentages | Listed only | "more than half" + significance |
| Analysis | None ("is bad") | "commanding share reflects persistent reliance" |
| Relationships | "more than...almost" | "renewable + nuclear = 42%" |
| Opinion removal | Present ("good/bad") | Objective data interpretation |
| Conclusion | Missing | Connected to global context |

## TRANSFORMATION 4: Table - Quarterly Revenue

**Band 6 Response (Weak):**
```
"The table shows revenue in four quarters. Q1 has 2.3 million. Q2 has 2.8 million. 
Q3 is 3.1 million. Q4 is 2.9 million. So it goes up then down. Revenue went up from 
Q1 to Q3. Then it came down in Q4. The highest is Q3 and the lowest is Q1. The difference 
is 0.8 million."
```

**Analysis - Why This Is Band 6:**
- Lists values without pattern analysis
- No terminology for trends ("goes up then down" informal)
- Arithmetic errors missed (0.8M is correct but no analysis of significance)
- No seasonal/cyclical interpretation
- Repetitive subject structures
- Missing interpretation of cause/business context

**Band 8 Response (Strong):**
```
"The table presents quarterly revenue figures across a financial year, revealing a 
cyclical revenue pattern with pronounced mid-year peaks.

Revenue demonstrated progressive growth during the first three quarters, commencing 
at £2.3 million in Q1 and escalating to £3.1 million by Q3, representing a 35% increase. 
This growth trajectory accelerated particularly between Q2 (£2.8 million) and Q3 (£3.1 
million), suggesting heightened commercial activity during the third quarter. Notably, 
Q3 represented the revenue zenith, surpassing Q1 by £0.8 million (approximately 35%).

However, revenue contracted in Q4 to £2.9 million, declining by approximately 6% from 
the Q3 peak. This quarter-on-quarter decline, while moderating the overall annual growth, 
remained substantially higher than opening-quarter figures. Overall, annual revenue 
trajectory demonstrates a characteristic cyclical pattern common to many retail and 
service sectors, with mid-year peaks and end-of-year consolidation reflecting seasonal 
business dynamics. The net effect across the year yielded an overall increase of £0.6 
million (26% growth from Q1 to Q4), indicating positive annual revenue performance 
despite Q4 seasonal contraction."
```

**Specific Upgrades Made:**

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 8 |
|---|---|---|
| Trend description | "goes up then down" | "cyclical...with pronounced mid-year peaks" |
| Calculations | Listed; no context | "35% increase", "6% decline" |
| Pattern recognition | "highest/lowest" | "Q3 represented the revenue zenith" |
| Interpretation | None | "characteristic cyclical pattern...seasonal dynamics" |
| Structure | Linear list | Grouped analysis with contextual interpretation |

## TRANSFORMATION 5: Process Diagram - Manufacturing

**Band 6 Response (Weak):**
```
"The diagram shows how to make a car. First, materials come in. Second, the engine 
is built. Third, the body is assembled. Fourth, wheels are put on. Fifth, the car 
is painted. Sixth, quality is checked. Finally, the car is shipped to the store. 
So the process has seven steps."
```

**Analysis - Why This Is Band 6:**
- Overly simplistic descriptions
- Missing technical terminology
- Passive voice throughout but lacks sophistication
- No explanation of purpose/context
- Steps listed but not explained with detail
- Missing industry-appropriate language

**Band 8 Response (Strong):**
```
"The diagram outlines the manufacturing sequence for automotive production, comprising 
seven sequential stages that transform raw materials into finished vehicles ready for 
market distribution.

The production process commences with raw material procurement, wherein component 
suppliers deliver engineered parts to the manufacturing facility. Factory workers then 
undertake engine assembly and integration, a critical stage requiring precision engineering 
and quality verification. Concurrently, the vehicle chassis and bodywork are assembled 
through automated and manual welding techniques. Following frame completion, component 
integration occurs, including the fitting of wheels, electrical systems, and interior 
components through specialized assembly stations.

The subsequent finishing phase involves multi-stage painting processes to achieve durability 
and aesthetic standards. Rigorous quality control procedures are implemented throughout, 
with comprehensive inspection protocols at each quality checkpoint to ensure adherence 
to manufacturing specifications. Finally, completed vehicles undergo final inspection and 
preparation before shipment to distribution centers and dealerships for market delivery.

In total, the manufacturing sequence represents an integrated process requiring coordination 
across multiple specialized departments and technologies, with each stage building upon 
previous completion to achieve the final product."
```

**Specific Upgrades Made:**

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 8 |
|---|---|---|
| Intro | "how to make a car" | "manufacturing sequence...comprises seven stages" |
| Terminology | "put on" | "integration", "assembly stations", "welding" |
| Detail | Simple lists | Purpose & technical context explained |
| Sequencing | First/Second/etc. | "commences", "concurrently", "subsequently" |
| Connections | Listed separately | "building upon previous completion" |
| Purpose | Missing | "ready for market distribution" |

## TRANSFORMATION 6: Mixed Visual - Immigration & Employment

**Band 6 Response (Weak):**
```
"There are two charts here. The first shows immigration from 2010 to 2020. The second 
shows employment rates. Immigration went up and employment went up too. So more people 
came and more people got jobs. This is good for the economy. The numbers show immigration 
is 2.5 million and employment is 85%. Both things increased."
```

**Analysis - Why This Is Band 6:**
- Simplistic connection between visuals
- No analysis of relationship/causation
- Opinion included ("good for economy")
- Missing key data points/trends
- No temporal analysis of divergence/convergence

**Band 8 Response (Strong):**
```
"The paired charts illustrate the relationship between net migration patterns and 
employment rates over a decade (2010-2020), revealing a complex relationship between 
immigration trends and labor market outcomes.

The left chart demonstrates net migration increased substantially from approximately 
1.5 million in 2010 to 2.5 million by 2020, representing a 67% expansion. This growth 
trajectory displayed particular acceleration between 2015 and 2018, before moderating 
slightly in 2019-2020. Conversely, the right chart illustrates employment rates, 
commencing at 72% in 2010 and rising to 85% by 2020, indicating a 13 percentage-point 
improvement in labor market participation.

Notably, the temporal trajectory of these indicators reveals interesting divergence: 
immigration surged most sharply between 2015-2018, yet employment rate growth proved 
more gradual and consistent throughout the period. This pattern suggests that while 
immigration increased substantially, employment expansion proceeded at a more measured 
pace, potentially indicating labor market absorption challenges or structural mismatches 
between immigrant skill profiles and available employment opportunities. By 2020, the 
combined effect yielded both increased migration and elevated employment, though the 
relationship appears non-linear rather than directly proportional."
```

**Specific Upgrades Made:**

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 8 |
|---|---|---|
| Connection | "went up and went up too" | "Temporal trajectory...reveals interesting divergence" |
| Analysis | Lists separately | Compares rates and interprets relationship |
| Opinion removed | "good for economy" | Objective analysis of relationship |
| Temporal precision | None | "particular acceleration 2015-2018" |
| Interpretation | Simple | "potential labor market absorption challenges" |
