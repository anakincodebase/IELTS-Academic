# Writing Task 1 Grammar Strategies

## Core Objective
> [!info] Rule
> In Task 1, grammar score depends on accurate tense control, precise comparison structures, and clean sentence variety.

## 1) Choose Tense from Time Data
- Past years only -> mostly Past Simple
- Past to present (up to now) -> Present Perfect
- Future projection -> Future forms (is projected to, will)
- Process diagram (no timeline) -> Present Simple Passive

> [!example] IELTS Example
> The percentage rose steadily between 2000 and 2010, then declined slightly.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Incorrect: The chart shows figures from 1990 to 2000, and the value increases rapidly.
>
> Correct: The chart shows figures from 1990 to 2000, and the value increased rapidly.

## 2) Master Comparison Grammar
Use comparison frames:
- higher/lower than
- more/fewer than
- the highest/the lowest
- similar to / in contrast to

> [!example] IELTS Example
> The figure for private cars was significantly higher than that for buses.

## 3) Alternate Verb and Noun Trend Reporting
- Verb style: rose, fell, peaked, remained stable, fluctuated
- Noun style: a rise in, a decline in, a peak of, a period of stability

> [!info] Rule
> Alternating structures prevents repetition and improves range.

## 4) Use Passive Voice in Processes
- are collected
- is transported
- is processed
- are packaged

> [!example] IELTS Example
> First, raw materials are sorted, after which they are processed in a heating chamber.

## 5) Sentence Variety Blueprint
- 1 simple sentence for overview
- 2 to 3 complex/compound sentences for key trends
- 1 sentence with while/whereas for contrast

Useful openers:
- Overall,
- In contrast,
- Meanwhile,
- By comparison,

## 6) Data Accuracy Grammar
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Do not confuse by and to.
>
> increase from 10% to 30% = end value
>
> increase by 20% = amount of change

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Use fewer for countables, less for uncountables.

## Quick Template Frames
- There was a sharp increase in X between A and B.
- X remained stable at Y.
- While X rose, Y declined.
- X reached a peak of Y in [year].

## 60-Second Final Check
- Tense matches years?
- Any incorrect prepositions (in/on/at, from/to/by)?
- Any repeated sentence starter 3+ times?
- Comparison grammar accurate?
