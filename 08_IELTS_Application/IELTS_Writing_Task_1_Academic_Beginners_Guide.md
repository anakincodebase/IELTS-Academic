# <span style="color: #1a3a52">IELTS Writing Task 1 Academic Beginners Guide</span>

## <span style="color: #00838f">Mindset and Core Rules</span>
> [!info] Rule
> Task 1 checks whether you can describe visible data clearly in English, not whether you can do deep data science analysis.

### Do Task 2 first
- Task 2 has double weight compared with Task 1.
- Finish Task 2 in about 40 minutes.
- Use about 20 minutes for Task 1 to avoid overthinking.

### Word-count target
- Typical effective range: 160 to 210 words.
- Never write below 150 words.
- Avoid going much above 220 words unless clarity remains strong.

### No opinion
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Task 1 is a summary task, not an opinion task. Do not explain why trends happened unless the chart explicitly gives causal data.

### Use approximations
- approximately
- around
- just under
- just over

> [!info] Rule
> If exact values are unclear, use accurate approximations instead of risky invented precision.

## <span style="color: #00838f">Understand the Data First (30,000-Foot View)</span>
Before writing, understand the purpose of the visual.

### <span style="color: #1b5e20">Static vs dynamic</span>
- Static: category comparison at one point/time block.
- Dynamic: change over time.

### <span style="color: #1b5e20">The 3-question filter</span>
1. Newspaper headline: what is the biggest story in this chart?
2. Gun-to-the-head summary: if you can mention only 2 to 4 features, what are they?
3. Boss report: what trends matter most for quick decision understanding?

## <span style="color: #00838f">Report Structure You Must Follow</span>
Task 1 is an image-based report, not an opinion essay. You may get one or a combination of six visuals:
- line graph
- bar chart
- pie chart
- table
- process diagram
- map difference

Use this three-part structure:

### <span style="color: #1b5e20">1. Opening (usually one sentence)</span>
- This is your first paragraph.
- Paraphrase the question statement directly.
- Keep the meaning identical while changing key words.

Examples:
- The graph shows... -> The graph illustrates...
- The chart shows... -> The chart provides information about...

### <span style="color: #00838f">2. Overview</span>
> [!warning] High-Impact Rule
> A clear overview is essential for Band 7+.

- Summarize only the most important global trends.
- Mention highest/lowest or dominant overall movements.
- Do not include specific numbers.
- You can place overview after opening or at the end.

### <span style="color: #1b5e20">3. Body Paragraphs</span>
- Provide grouped details, comparisons, and key data points.
- Body organization depends on diagram type.
- No opinion, no reasons unless shown by the visual.
- No separate essay-style conclusion needed.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Vocabulary for Trend Movement (Line, Bar, Pie)</span>
Use all five movement types naturally:

### Slight increase
- Verbs: grew, climbed, expanded, rose
- Modifiers: slightly, moderately, minimally
- Noun style: a moderate rise, a slight expansion

### Slight decrease
- Verbs: reduced, went down, decreased, dropped, declined

### Dramatic increase
- Verbs: escalated, rocketed, soared, jumped
- Usually no adverb needed because these verbs are already strong.

### Dramatic decrease
- Verbs: collapsed, slumped, crashed, plunged, plummeted

### No change
- stood at
- stayed at
- stabilized at
- leveled off at

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">Strategies by Diagram Type</span>

### Bar charts and line graphs
- Organize body paragraphs by X-axis groups (time/categories), not Y-axis value listing.
- Apply Highest -> Lowest -> Middle grouping.
- Compare major contrasts first, then cluster close values.

### Pie charts
- Do not write one paragraph for year A and one for year B only.
- Organize by category/commodity and compare category shifts across years.
- Highlight categories that rose into dominance or fell from dominance.

### Tables
- Never describe every number.
- Group highest values together.
- Group lowest/zero values together.
- Summarize remaining mid-range values briefly.

### Process diagrams
- Focus on flow and stage order.
- Use sequencing vocabulary: initially, first, then, next, after that, eventually, finally.
- Keep passive structures where natural.

### Map differences
- Treat earliest year as the base year.
- Paragraph 1: describe base year only (no future comparison).
- Paragraph 2: describe later year as changes from base year.
- Use change verbs: demolished, converted, expanded, replaced, constructed.

### Multiple diagrams (for example, line graph plus table)
- Keep structure simple.
- One opening sentence for both visuals.
- One body paragraph for visual 1.
- One body paragraph for visual 2.
- Integrate only if a clear direct correlation is obvious.

## <span style="color: #5e35b1">Step-by-Step Test-Day Execution</span>
1. Read the prompt carefully.
2. Read title, axes, units, and key/legend.
3. Identify 2 to 4 major features (1 to 3 minutes only).
4. Chunk data into logical blocks.
5. Identify comparisons, intersections, overtakes, peaks, and lows.
6. Write one-sentence opening (paraphrase).
7. Write overview (Overall + major patterns).
8. Write detail paragraphs with selected evidence.
9. Review grammar, spelling, word count, and paragraph spacing.

## <span style="color: #00838f">Detail Language Toolkit</span>
### Trend verbs
- rose
- increased
- declined
- fell
- remained stable
- fluctuated
- peaked

### Approximation language
- approximately
- around
- about
- just under
- just over

### Comparison language
- higher/lower than
- more/fewer than
- compared with
- while/whereas
- in contrast

## <span style="color: #d4af37">Common Beginner Mistakes</span>
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Writing too much detail and missing key trends.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Giving reasons/opinions not shown by the chart.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Missing overview or adding numbers in overview.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Reporting data randomly without clear grouping.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Writing body paragraphs year-by-year for pie charts without category comparison.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Mixing map timelines in one paragraph instead of using the base-year method.

## 9) 60-Second Final Check
- Did I include a clear overview?
- Is my opening concise and accurate?
- Did I avoid opinion language?
- Did I use accurate approximations where needed?
- Are comparisons present where relevant?
- Is word count at least 150 and still concise?
- Is paragraphing visually clear?
