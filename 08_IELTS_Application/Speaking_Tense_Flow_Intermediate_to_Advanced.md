# Speaking Tense Flow Intermediate to Advanced

## Core Goal
> [!info] Rule
> All students should control tense flow, not only tense accuracy: start clearly, expand logically, and finish naturally.

## All Students: 3-Step Tense Control
1. Anchor tense: choose one main time frame first.
2. Expand tense: add one supporting tense for reason, background, or result.
3. Close tense: finish with a clear ending tense (present result or future plan).

## Intermediate Flow

### Part 1 (Personal Topics)
Flow:
- Present Simple -> Present Perfect -> Present Continuous

Use:
- habit -> duration/experience -> current change

Template:
- I usually __ because __.
- I have __ for/since __, so __.
- Recently, I am __ because __.

Model:
- I usually study at night because my house is quieter then.
- I have followed this routine for three years, so it feels natural to me.
- Recently, I am adjusting my schedule because my exam is getting closer.

### Part 2 (Narrative)
Flow:
- Past Continuous -> Past Simple -> Past Perfect

Use:
- background scene -> main event -> earlier cause

Template:
- I was __ when __.
- Then I __, and __.
- I later realized that I had __.

Model:
- I was waiting at the station when my phone battery died.
- Then I borrowed a charger from a stranger, and I caught the last train.
- I later realized that I had forgotten my power bank at home.

## Upper-Intermediate to Advanced Flow

### Part 1 (Natural Range)
Flow:
- Present Simple -> Present Perfect Continuous -> Future Simple

Use:
- routine -> ongoing effort -> short plan

Model:
- I usually practice speaking with a partner after dinner.
- I have been doing this for months, so my confidence has improved.
- I will continue this method until my test date.

### Part 2 (High-Band Story Control)
Flow:
- Past Perfect -> Past Simple -> Present Reflection

Use:
- prior background -> key event -> current lesson

Model:
- I had prepared a full speech before I joined the competition.
- However, I forgot the script on stage and spoke from memory.
- That experience has taught me to practice with only keywords.

### Part 3 (Abstract Discussion)
Flow:
- Present Simple (general truth) -> Present Perfect (recent trend) -> Conditional/Future (implication)

Use:
- claim -> evidence trend -> policy prediction

Model:
- Public transport reduces congestion in major cities.
- In recent years, several countries have expanded rail systems successfully.
- If governments keep investing in this sector, urban mobility will improve further.

## Advanced Linking Blocks for Speaking
- Cause: because, since, as
- Contrast: although, while, whereas, however
- Result: so, therefore, as a result
- Time sequence: when, before, after, by the time
- Condition: if, unless, provided that

## Common Tense-Flow Mistakes (All Levels)
> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Mixing present and past without a reason in the same event timeline.

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Using present perfect with finished past time markers (in 2019, yesterday).

> [!warning] Band Penalty
> Skipping earlier-past reference in stories where past perfect is needed.

## 14-Day Speaking Tense Upgrade Plan (All Students)
- Days 1 to 4: Master one flow for Part 1 and record 5 answers daily.
- Days 5 to 8: Practice Part 2 narrative flow with 2 cue cards per day.
- Days 9 to 11: Practice Part 3 flow with opinion topics and conditionals.
- Days 12 to 14: Mix all three parts in one mock test with self-correction notes.
