# <span style="color: #1a3a52">IELTS Speaking Part 2: Exact Cue Cards, Sample Answers, and Sentence Flow Formulas</span>

## <span style="color: #00838f">Real IELTS Part 2 Format</span>

1. You get 1 minute to prepare.
2. You speak for 1 to 2 minutes.
3. You receive one cue card with bullet points.
4. Aim for coherent flow, accurate grammar, and precise vocabulary.

## <span style="color: #1b5e20">Cue Card 1: Advertisements</span>

Describe an advertisement that persuaded you to buy a product.
You should say:

- what advertisement it was
- where you saw it
- what was good about it
- why it made the product attractive

Sample answer:
An advertisement that persuaded me was for a project-based data science course. I saw it on YouTube while searching for Python tutorials. It stood out because it focused on mentorship and practical projects instead of vague promises. I found it attractive because it addressed a real learning gap in my preparation.

Flow formula:
Topic -> Platform -> Strong feature -> Personal reason -> Final judgment

## Universal Sentence Flow Formula (Any Part 2 Card)

1. Context sentence.
2. Background sentence.
3. Detail sentence one.
4. Detail sentence two.
5. Turning-point sentence.
6. Feeling sentence.
7. Reflection sentence.
8. Closing relevance sentence.

## GRE-Accurate Lexical Bank

1. Methodical, resilient, pragmatic, articulate, compelling.
2. Coherent, sustainable, rigorous, insightful, transformative.
3. Credibility, precision, consistency, adaptability, long-term impact.

## Band 8+ Delivery Checklist

1. Use natural sequence markers.
2. Keep tense control stable.
3. Use one advanced word precisely.
4. End with a reflective line.
5. Memorize structure, not scripts.

## Operational Drill Loop (Part 2)

Use this cycle for 15 to 20 minutes:

1. Select one cue card and plan for 60 seconds.
2. Speak for 2 minutes without stopping.
3. Replay and mark tense slips, unclear pronouns, and repeated words.
4. Re-speak the same card with cleaner grammar and better sequencing.
5. End with one reflective closing sentence.
